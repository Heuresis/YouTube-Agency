# Dual-Mode Content Strategy

> Most creator-content advice forces a binary choice: either go niche (and stay small but convert hard) or go broad (and grow audience but never monetize). The dual-mode strategy refuses the binary. The encoded channel runs **both** — and the architecture connects them.

---

## The two modes

### Authority mode (niched)

**Purpose:** Convert existing audience into back-end offer applications.

**Style:**
- Belief-installation arc — each video installs one of the 8 required beliefs the audience needs to hold before buying
- Expertise demonstration — the creator's specific judgement / mechanism / framework is the spine of the video
- Opinion-led — the creator commits to a contrarian take or unique synthesis
- Length: typically 8–25 minutes (long enough to fully install a belief)
- Awareness target: usually Solution-aware or Product-aware (the viewer already knows the problem; needs the solution / the specific product)
- CTA: explicit. Pinned VSL, application link, lead-magnet bridge.

**Examples of what authority videos answer:**
- "Why most [target audience] stay stuck at [specific revenue band]"
- "The 3 things I do differently when [specific operational decision]"
- "Why [common advice] is wrong and what to do instead"
- "How I [specific transformation outcome] in [specific timeframe]"
- "The framework I use for [specific high-stakes decision]"

These videos do not chase mass appeal. They convert.

### Reach mode (broad)

**Purpose:** Grow the audience pool beyond the niche.

**Style:**
- Contrast format — the structural pattern is some version of "A vs B," "before vs after," "1-vs-many," "then-vs-now," "[option A] vs [option B]"
- Curiosity-driven — every section opens a loop; the viewer can't predict what comes next
- Retention-heavy — minimum 3 open-loops, pattern-interrupt cadence ≤60s, callback in close
- Mass-appeal hook — the first 3 seconds are designed to engage the Reptilian brain (movement / contrast / threat / mystery) regardless of whether the viewer knows anything about the niche
- Length: typically 4–8 minutes (shorter; broader audience won't commit 20 minutes to an unknown creator)
- Awareness target: usually Unaware or Problem-aware (the viewer doesn't know they have the problem yet; the video introduces them)
- CTA: soft. Channel architecture (pinned VSL, end-screen, community tab) does the conversion work; the reach video itself doesn't sell.

**Examples of what reach videos look like:**
- "1 [specific archetype] vs [contrasting archetype N]"
- "I tried [unusual experiment] for [timeframe] — here's what happened"
- "Why [counterintuitive observation about the niche]"
- "[Specific decision] turned [unexpected outcome]"
- "[Public-figure archetype] does this; [contrasting archetype] does that"

These videos do not convert directly. They grow the audience.

## The mix ratio

Default starting mix: **65% authority + 35% reach**.

The ratio shifts by channel stage:

| Channel stage | Authority % | Reach % | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–10K subs | 80 | 20 | Audience is too small; reach videos can't compound. Focus on converting the audience you have. |
| 10K–50K subs | 70 | 30 | Audience is starting to compound. Add reach videos selectively. |
| 50K–250K subs | 60 | 40 | The reach mode starts paying for itself. The channel architecture handles the conversion. |
| 250K–1M subs | 55 | 45 | Reach drives most subscriber growth at this scale; authority drives the back-end. |
| 1M+ subs | 50 | 50 | Both modes compound at scale. The mix balances. |

These are starting points. The exact mix calibrates per niche and per channel based on actual performance.

## Why reach without authority is a trap (and vice versa)

**Reach-only channels** grow audience but don't convert. The back-end is empty. Subscribers accumulate but revenue is mostly ad-share and sponsorship — both of which require massive scale to be meaningful. The creator stays trapped in volume.

**Authority-only channels** convert but don't grow. The audience pool is the niche. Once the niche is saturated, growth stops. Revenue caps at the back-end's ceiling × the audience size.

The dual-mode channel grows AND converts. Reach videos pull non-target viewers in; channel architecture (pinned VSL, end-screen, community tab) routes them. Some subset of those non-target viewers turn out to be target after all — they self-select via the channel architecture, not via being directly addressed in the reach video.

This is the structural advantage. The reach video stays clean (no sales pitch); the channel architecture does the conversion work in the background.

## How the OS routes between modes

When the creator runs `/idea-farm`, the OS produces ideas tagged by mode. The creator (or the producer) picks the week's slate respecting the mode mix.

When the creator runs `/write-authority-script`, the OS calibrates:
- Compartment thresholds: Identity ≥ 70, Audience ≥ 70, Offer ≥ 60
- Hook formulas favored: Bound-Promise, Contrarian-Frame, Social-Proof-Stack
- Length target: longer (8–25 min)
- CTA: explicit, end-and-mid placement
- Verification: retention-floor + sophistication-stage match (must match the declared awareness level rigorously)

When the creator runs `/write-reach-script`, the OS calibrates:
- Compartment thresholds: Identity ≥ 60, Audience ≥ 50, Hook & Retention ≥ 70, Channel Architecture ≥ 60 (because channel architecture does the conversion)
- Hook formulas favored: Contrast, Curiosity-Gap, Statistic, Universal
- Length target: shorter (4–8 min)
- CTA: soft, channel-architecture-only (no offer pitch in the script)
- Verification: retention-floor + contrast-format compliance (the reach mode lives or dies on retention)

`/write-script` (without mode declaration) refuses to run. Mode declaration is required (INV-12).

## The cross-niche bridge

Reach videos pull viewers who aren't in the target niche. Some of them are target-after-all (they didn't know they had the problem until the reach video introduced it). The bridge skill (`/cross-niche-bridge`) designs the path from "non-target reach viewer" to "target back-end customer":

1. **End-screen routing** — the reach video's end-screen points to a "if you actually wanted to know more about X" authority video, not the back-end pitch
2. **Pinned VSL** — for viewers who watched the reach video and clicked into the channel page
3. **Community tab** — for viewers who subscribed but haven't watched authority content yet
4. **Email lead-magnet** — for viewers who clicked the reach video's description link
5. **Welcome sequence** — for new subscribers, segments by which video they came from (reach video → softer onboarding; authority video → faster conversion)

The bridge is the architectural payoff. Without it, reach videos are wasted growth. With it, every reach video grows the future-converter pool.

## Common dual-mode failure modes

1. **Reach video with authority CTA.** "Subscribe for more business advice" at the end of a "1-vs-9-5" video. The CTA jolts the viewer out of the reach context. They came for entertainment-education; they got an offer. Bounce. Fix: reach videos use only soft CTAs (end-screen + pinned-VSL routing).

2. **Authority video with reach hook.** "Wait until you see what I found" opens an authority video about pricing methodology. The hook misqualifies the audience. Casual viewers click; serious viewers don't. Authority retention drops. Fix: authority hooks are bound-promise, contrarian-frame, or social-proof — explicit about what the video delivers.

3. **No mode declaration.** Creator says "write me a script about X" without specifying mode. Output is mode-confused — half authority structure, half reach hooks. Retention-floor catches it. Fix: every script invocation declares mode. The OS refuses to draft without it.

4. **Mode mix inverted for stage.** A 5K-subscriber channel running 60% reach + 40% authority. Audience is too small for reach to compound — the videos get 200 views and disappear. Authority videos would get 2K views and produce 5 applications. Fix: read the mix-by-stage table; rebalance.

5. **Channel architecture gap.** Reach videos perform — pull thousands of new viewers. But the channel page has no pinned VSL, no clear pillar playlist, no email lead-magnet. The new viewers subscribe (maybe) and forget. No conversion. Fix: build the channel architecture before scaling reach.

6. **Authority videos all targeted to the same awareness level.** Channel only speaks to Solution-aware viewers. The Problem-aware viewers (the bigger pool) bounce. The Most-aware viewers (the closer-to-buying pool) feel the content is too basic. Fix: multi-awareness strategy. Different authority videos for different awareness levels, sequenced via channel architecture.

## How to start

If you're new to the dual-mode strategy:

1. Run `/content-mix-strategy` — the OS reads your channel-stage and recommends the starting mix.
2. Run `/idea-farm` — get a backlog. Tag each idea by mode.
3. Pick the week's slate respecting the mix. (E.g., 70/30 means roughly 7 authority videos for every 3 reach videos over a quarter.)
4. Build channel architecture FIRST (`/build-channel-architecture`). Without it, reach videos waste their growth.
5. Run the first authority video. Then the first reach video. Compare retention curves. Calibrate.
6. After 8–12 videos in each mode, run `/audit-retention` to see what's compounding. Adjust mix or skill calibration.

The encoding flywheel does the rest. Each cycle, the OS gets sharper at predicting which authority topics convert, which reach formats grow, and how to route between them.

---

## What this strategy refuses to do

- **It refuses to do "viral content for its own sake."** Every reach video has a routing path back to the channel architecture. If a reach idea has no routing path, it's not in the OS's idea-farm output.
- **It refuses to inject sales into reach videos.** The reach video stays clean. Channel architecture converts.
- **It refuses to make authority videos boring.** Authority mode still requires hook density and retention engineering. The structural rigor of authority content doesn't excuse low retention.
- **It refuses to abandon authority for reach when reach starts working.** The temptation is real — reach videos are easier to make and grow audience faster. But a channel that abandons authority loses its conversion engine. The OS enforces the mix.
