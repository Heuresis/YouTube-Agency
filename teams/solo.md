# Team Configuration — Solo Creator

> The creator is the entire team. The OS is the leverage layer. Everything that can be encoded is encoded; what remains is the highest-leverage 20% the creator does themselves.

---

## When this configuration applies

- 0–25K subscribers
- Pre-revenue or $5K–$25K/mo
- No editor, no thumbnail designer, no community manager
- Creator self-edits or uses a freelance editor for ad-hoc projects
- 1–4 long-form videos per month + intermittent Shorts

This is the most common starting point for a coach / consultant / info-product creator launching a YouTube presence as the front door to their offer.

---

## Operating principle for this configuration

**Encode everything except the four irreducibles: on-camera, taste calls, sales calls, parasocial-trust contract.**

The creator does:
1. **On-camera delivery** — the creator's face/voice is unduplicable. No agent records the creator's videos.
2. **Taste calls** — final yes/no on title-thumbnail pairs, sponsor fit, script approvals, offer pricing.
3. **Sales calls** — inbound applications convert via the creator's voice on the call. Setters or closers can be added when revenue scales but solo-config keeps this owner-only.
4. **Parasocial-trust contract decisions** — anything that affects the long-term creator-audience trust contract is the creator's call.

Everything else routes through the OS.

## Weekly cadence

A solo creator running this OS spends approximately:

- **Monday (2–3 hours)**: Read leak audit, idea farm review, week's content priorities decided
- **Tuesday (3–4 hours)**: Research / outline pass on the week's primary video
- **Wednesday (4–6 hours)**: Record session — the creator's on-camera time
- **Thursday (1–2 hours)**: Review system-drafted thumbnail briefs / titles / descriptions; approve
- **Friday (1–2 hours)**: Community cadence (comments, community-tab posts), inbound application review, sales calls
- **Saturday (optional)**: Buffer / re-record / additional content
- **Sunday (1 hour)**: Library compound review — patterns surfaced from the week feed back into compartments

**Total: 12–18 hours per week** for the creator. The OS absorbs the rest.

Without the OS, this cadence requires 30–50 hours per week or hiring a 2–3-person team.

## When to graduate to creator-plus-editor

Signals that solo-config is hitting its ceiling:
- Editing time is consuming > 10 hours per week of creator time
- Creator is publishing late because edit isn't done
- Creator has the budget for an editor ($500–$3K/mo)
- The creator's editing taste is consistent enough to brief an editor
- Output quality plateauing because creator is exhausted by the post-production load

Graduate to the `creator-plus-editor.md` configuration. See that file for the new skill ownership matrix.

## Common solo-config failure modes

1. **Creator over-owns the script** — they spend 6 hours hand-writing what the OS could draft in 20 minutes. The script gets marginally better; everything else suffers. Fix: trust the OS for the structural draft, do the taste pass only.
2. **Creator skips the leak audit** — without the audit, the OS can't tell upstream from downstream. Fix: a 30-min Monday read of `/leak-audit` is non-negotiable.
3. **Creator self-edits everything** — at solo-config scale, self-editing is the dominant time sink. The OS can't make video editing faster — but it can produce editor briefs that make hiring an editor low-friction. Hire sooner than feels comfortable.
4. **Creator under-records** — solo-config works only if the recording schedule is consistent. The OS can produce 100 scripts; if no recording happens, nothing publishes. Block the recording day.
5. **Creator over-iterates on titles/thumbnails** — solo-config creators sometimes spend 3 hours picking from 8 OS-generated title options. Pick fast; the title is recoverable post-publish. The retention is not.
