# Team Configuration — Full Studio

> The creator + writer + editor + thumbnail designer + community manager + producer/ops. The OS still owns the encoded brain; humans execute against it. The creator's role narrows to on-camera + strategic taste + sales conversations.

---

## When this configuration applies

- 100K+ subscribers
- $100K–$500K+/mo revenue
- 5+ person team:
  - Producer / ops manager (or chief of staff)
  - Writer / researcher
  - Editor (1–2 editors)
  - Thumbnail designer (or graphic designer)
  - Community manager / customer success
  - Optional: short-form specialist, sponsor-ops, sales setter
- 8+ long-form videos per month + daily Shorts + regular sponsor integrations
- Creator's role: on-camera, strategic decisions, taste calls, sponsor relationships, key sales calls, brand-direction

---

## Operating principle for this configuration

**The creator is the strategist + the on-camera persona; the OS is the encoded brain; the team executes against the OS.** Every team member uses the OS to know what to produce; the OS uses each team member's specific skills to execute production.

This configuration is where the OS's compounding advantage becomes most visible. The encoded library means a new hire onboards in 1–2 weeks instead of 2–3 months. Specialist skills compound across the team because everyone reads the same compartments.

## Roles + skill ownership

### Producer / Ops Manager
The producer is the human orchestrator — the one who looks at `/kpi-dashboard` and `/leak-audit` and decides what gets prioritized. They don't make taste calls; they coordinate.

Skills owned:
- `/kpi-dashboard` daily review + escalation to creator
- `/leak-audit` weekly read + agenda for creator-team weekly meeting
- `/build-sop` — produces team SOPs
- Cross-team handoffs (writer → editor → thumbnail designer → publishing)
- Sponsor-ops coordination
- Hiring / onboarding new team members

### Writer / Researcher
The writer takes the OS's draft scripts and does the voice-and-substance pass. With Elite-tier encoding, this is a light pass. With less-encoded compartments, this is heavier.

Skills owned:
- `/research-brief` execution (the OS provides the structure; the writer fills it with depth)
- `/idea-farm` curation (filter the rolling backlog, propose the week's slate)
- `/series-architect` — proposes multi-video sequences to the creator
- Voice-pass on `/write-authority-script` and `/write-reach-script` outputs
- `/write-pinned-vsl` substantive draft (creator does final pass)
- `/sales-call-script` revisions from real call data

### Editor (1–2)
Editor 1 owns long-form. Editor 2 (when present) owns Shorts + clips.

Skills owned (Editor 1):
- `/editor-brief` execution for long-form
- Thumbnail design (if no thumbnail designer)
- `/publish-checklist` execution
- Color grade + final delivery

Skills owned (Editor 2 / Short-Form Specialist):
- `/repurposing-cascade` execution
- Shorts editing
- Clips for social media
- TikTok / Reels distribution

### Thumbnail Designer (if separate)
- `/thumbnail-brief` execution
- A/B test design + measurement
- Channel-wide visual consistency
- Reports to producer; works with editor

### Community Manager
- `/community-cadence` execution (with creator's voice profile loaded)
- Comments triage + reply (high-signal flagged for creator)
- Community-tab posts
- DM triage
- Top-commenter recognition + advocacy mechanics
- Optional: lead-magnet delivery + welcome-sequence email management

### Optional: Sales Setter
- Inbound application qualification
- Call booking + confirmation cadence
- Show-rate stack execution
- Reports inbound velocity to producer

### Creator
- On-camera (4–8 hours per week)
- Taste calls (titles, thumbnails, sponsor fit, script approvals)
- Sales calls on high-ticket back-end offer
- Brand direction
- Strategic-layer compartment updates (when audience or offer evolves)

## Cadence

A full-studio configuration runs at these rhythms:

**Daily (producer, 2 hours)**: KPI pulse, team standup, blocker triage
**Weekly (creator, 1 hour)**: Leak-audit read + strategic-layer decisions
**Weekly (team, 1 hour)**: Cross-functional sync; producer presents the week's slate
**Recording session (creator, 4–8 hours)**: Often weekly or bi-weekly; multiple videos
**Continuous (writer, 25–35 hours)**: Research, scripting, voice-pass
**Continuous (editor 1, 30–40 hours)**: Long-form edits
**Continuous (short-form specialist, 20–30 hours)**: Shorts + repurposing
**Continuous (thumbnail designer, 15–25 hours)**: Thumbnails, A/B tests, channel-art
**Continuous (community manager, 20–30 hours)**: Comments, community tab, email
**Continuous (sales setter, 30–40 hours when present)**: Inbound calls

**Creator total: 8–12 hours per week.** Most of the creator's content-side work is on-camera + taste-pass.

## What the OS adds at this scale

At full-studio scale, the OS's value compounds in three ways:

1. **Onboarding speed.** A new team member reads SYSTEM.md + INVARIANTS.md + ENCODING.md + the relevant skill SKILL.md files and is operating at production-grade in 1–2 weeks. Without the OS, the same role takes 8–12 weeks.

2. **Decision consistency.** Every team member makes decisions against the same encoded brain. The writer's voice-pass + the editor's cut-density + the thumbnail designer's composition + the community manager's comment-reply tone all reference the same `creator_identity_matrix` and `audience_intelligence_system`. There's no drift.

3. **Library compounding.** Every published video feeds back into the swipe-file. Patterns surface. The next cycle starts where the last cycle ended. The team gets sharper without anyone having to "remember" what worked — the library remembers.

A full-studio team without the OS is constantly re-deciding things. A full-studio team with the OS is executing against decisions that were made once and now compound.

## Common failure modes

1. **Creator becomes a bottleneck on taste calls.** With 8 videos in flight, the creator is asked to approve 30+ items per week. Fix: pre-approve patterns. The creator approves the *patterns* (e.g., "this is the kind of thumbnail I like"); the team executes against pattern. Creator only intervenes on outliers.

2. **Producer doesn't enforce the OS.** Team starts making decisions outside the OS — "we'll just do it this way for this video" — and decisions stop flowing back to the compartments. Fix: producer's role explicitly includes "every decision returns to the encoded brain." If a one-off decision happens, the producer captures it and decides whether it goes into the OS or stays one-off.

3. **Encoding goes stale.** The audience evolves; the offer evolves; the channel architecture evolves. The OS doesn't auto-update to reflect this. Fix: quarterly encoding-refresh cycle. The producer + writer + creator do a 3-hour audit on every compartment. Update what changed.

4. **The team "uses the OS less" as they get experienced.** Senior team members start trusting their judgement over the OS. Quality often holds short-term but encoding-coherence drifts. Fix: the leak-audit-weekly catches drift. The producer checks team-output-vs-OS-prediction monthly.

5. **Hiring without onboarding to the OS.** New hires start working before they've internalized the OS. They produce drift from day one. Fix: 2-week formal onboarding to the OS before any production work. No exceptions.

## What stays the creator's, even at full scale

Four things never leave the creator, no matter how big the team:

1. **On-camera performance** — the creator's voice/face/presence is unduplicable
2. **Strategic-layer decisions** — niche pivots, offer changes, big positioning calls
3. **Sponsor relationships at the brand level** (not the operational level)
4. **The parasocial-trust contract** — every audience-facing decision that touches the long-term creator-audience relationship

Everything else can be encoded and delegated.
