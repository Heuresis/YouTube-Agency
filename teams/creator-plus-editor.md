# Team Configuration — Creator + Editor

> The creator + 1 editor. The OS still owns most decisions; the editor specializes in cuts, b-roll, supers, and music. Production capacity roughly doubles vs solo.

---

## When this configuration applies

- 25K–100K subscribers
- $25K–$100K/mo revenue
- Creator + 1 dedicated editor (in-house or full-time freelance)
- 4–8 long-form videos per month + regular Shorts cadence
- Creator no longer self-edits

The editor is the first hire because editing is the first work to consume disproportionate creator time. Editor is hired before thumbnail designer, before community manager, before a writer.

---

## Operating principle for this configuration

**Encode the editor's decisions wherever they're consistent.** Cuts at every transition, b-roll types, music-band, super style — all encoded into the editor's brief template that the OS produces per video.

The editor stops "deciding what to cut" video-by-video; instead they execute the brief and surface the 5–10% of cases where the brief doesn't fit.

Skill ownership matrix changes for production-side skills:

| Skill | Owner | Change vs solo |
|---|---|---|
| `/editor-brief` | OS produces; editor consumes | New: this skill is the editor's daily input |
| `/thumbnail-brief` | OS produces; editor or creator executes | Editor may take this over as their second skill |
| `/publish-checklist` | OS produces; editor handles uploads | Editor owns the upload mechanics |
| `/repurposing-cascade` | OS produces; editor executes the cuts | Editor adds Shorts production to their workload |

Other changes:
- The creator's recording day can be longer (3–5 hours instead of 2–3) because there's no self-editing pressure
- Two videos can be in production at once (one being edited, one being recorded)
- Shorts production becomes consistent because the editor handles cut-down

## Editor onboarding flow

The OS produces a 2-week onboarding checklist for new editors:

**Week 1:**
- Read `creator_identity_matrix.on_camera_persona` to understand pace, energy, signature visual elements
- Watch the channel's top-10 videos by retention; note recurring cut patterns
- Read `production_and_post.b_roll_grammar` and `production_and_post.editing_rhythm`
- Edit one 5-minute Short under creator supervision
- Edit one full long-form video with creator review at every 25% milestone

**Week 2:**
- Edit two long-form videos with end-of-edit review
- Take over thumbnail design (if scope) — read `production_and_post.thumbnail_design_system`
- Take over publishing mechanics — `publish-checklist` end-to-end
- Begin Shorts cascade — process one long-form into 3–5 Shorts

**End of week 2:** editor has done at least one full video cycle without creator intervention beyond approvals. Edit-time-per-video should be tracked; target: stable by week 4.

## The editor's authority

What the editor decides on their own:
- Specific cut placement (within the brief's density target)
- Specific b-roll selection (within the brief's source pool)
- Specific music track within the band
- Super placement and timing
- Caption styling
- Color grading

What requires creator approval:
- Departures from the brief
- Music selections outside the licensed library
- Anything that touches the creator-as-character distinction (e.g., showing footage of the creator looking unprofessional, even if "candid")
- Sponsor segment treatment beyond the brief

## Cadence

A creator-plus-editor configuration runs:

- **Monday (creator, 2 hours)**: Leak audit + week priorities
- **Tuesday (creator, 3 hours)**: Research + outline
- **Wednesday (creator, 4–6 hours)**: Recording day. Often two videos shot in one session.
- **Thursday (creator, 1 hour)**: Approve thumbnails, titles, descriptions. Editor brief ships.
- **Thursday–Friday (editor, 6–10 hours per video)**: Edit
- **Friday (creator, 1 hour)**: Final review of edited video. Approve.
- **Saturday (editor, 2–3 hours)**: Repurposing cascade (Shorts, cuts)
- **Saturday (creator, 1 hour)**: Community cadence

**Creator total: 12–14 hours per week.** **Editor total: 25–35 hours per week.**

---

## Common failure modes

1. **Brief drift** — editor stops reading the brief, starts editing on autopilot. Cuts get sloppy. Fix: the OS surfaces "brief-deviation" diagnostics weekly; editor + creator reset.
2. **Creator over-reviewing** — creator spends 3 hours per video on revision notes. Wastes creator time. Fix: the editor is hired for taste; trust them on cuts. Creator should only flag the 1–2 issues per video that matter.
3. **Editor exhausted before creator** — editor capacity caps content output. Fix: hire a 2nd editor before quality drops, OR slow the publishing cadence (1 long-form/week is fine).
4. **No clear thumbnail ownership** — creator and editor both think the other is doing it. Thumbnails ship inconsistent. Fix: explicit ownership — pick one, write it into the team config.
5. **Editor not part of leak audits** — editor doesn't know which videos worked / why. Fix: editor gets the weekly leak audit. They learn from the channel's data.

---

## When to graduate to full studio

Signals that creator-plus-editor is hitting its ceiling:
- Both editor and creator at capacity, can't add a video to the slate
- Thumbnail design quality plateauing because editor isn't a thumbnail specialist
- Community is too big for creator + OS to handle without dropping signal
- Creator is doing tasks the OS could route to a writer or community manager but no one's there

Graduate to the `full-studio.md` configuration.
