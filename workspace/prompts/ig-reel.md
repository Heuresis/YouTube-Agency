# ig-reel — short-form generator (the attention engine)

> Short-form is the **attention engine** that feeds the funnel (`workspace/STRATEGY.md`): organic build-in-public *gets the lead*, then bio-link → pinned VSL → the offer path. The same pack also covers **launch/offer promo reels** and **paid ad creatives**. One pack, **three jobs.** Keep the surface's *character* — the unguarded, real-operator energy that earns short-form reach — and change only what it points at. The production-grade sibling for YouTube Shorts is the `/write-shorts-script` skill.

## Before you generate — read these
1. `workspace/VOICE.md` — the spoken-voice rules + reserved-vocabulary discipline + banned vocab. **Read scripts aloud; if your mouth feels weird, the line is wrong** (`spec/voice-gates.md`). Non-negotiable.
2. `workspace/PROOF-BANK.md` — every number traces to a row. **Never invent a number** (INV-6). Not verified → `[CONFIRM: $X]` slot.
3. `workspace/STRATEGY.md` — the funnel, the short-form charter, any open launch window. `workspace/CONTENT-ENGINE.md` — the short-form format menu.

## Inputs (ask for any missing — don't guess)
- **Job:** A / B / C.
- **Topic / the one thing this reel is about** (a build moment, a client fix, a mechanism beat, the launch).
- **Format family** (menu below) — or let the generator pick by topic.
- **The proof** — which PROOF-BANK row(s)? If a number isn't `[VERIFIED]`, it's a `[CONFIRM: …]` slot, never invented.
- **Niche** (ads/promo) — who's it targeting?
- **Awareness** (ads) — problem / solution / product / unaware (drives the hook angle — INV-13).

## The universal reel skeleton (every reel, every job)

Five beats. The hook is the entire game — it earns or loses the next 0.5s. (INV-11 Shorts rule: 2 mechanisms in the first 3s, 1 more in 3–15s.)

```
[1] HOOK         0–3s   (1–5s max)  — cold open. The scroll-stopper. Sound-OFF readable.
[2] PROBLEM      3–7s               — the gap / the moment-before / the cost. One beat.
[3] FIX-or-PROOF 7–14s              — the build, the receipt, the artifact ON SCREEN.
[4] PAYOFF       14–20s             — the one-line lesson or the "so what." Fragment.
[5] CTA          20–end             — imperative + image, not a question. Destination per job.
```

**Beat rules (apply to spoken VO + on-screen text both):**
- **Cold open only.** Banned: "In this video I'll show you…", "What's up everyone," "Hit subscribe before we dive in." Lead with the *moment before the realization*.
- **Show, don't claim.** Every claim → an artifact on screen (payment screen, dashboard, file tree, DM, the build). The visual carries the trust.
- **Overlays ≤ 8–10 words**, sound-off legible, **no emoji on overlays** (legibility; emoji only in captions, sparingly, only if load-bearing).
- **Numbers said in full in VO** ("twenty-three clients"), tight on overlay ("23 clients").
- Fragments. "And"-openers. One-word punchlines. No BUT/THEREFORE mid-sentence. The channel catchphrase (if `workspace/VOICE.md` defines one) once, max, only if genuinely earned.

**Format / audio spec (all reels):**
- 9:16 vertical, **1080×1920, ≥30fps.** Burned-in captions **always** (sound-off viewing).
- Keep text clear of the **top ~220px / bottom ~420px** (platform UI overlap).
- Hook frame must read with **sound off** — overlay + motion in the first frame.
- Audio per job (table above). For ads: **no copyrighted/trending audio** — original VO only.

| 2 | **Dashboard / output-reveal B-roll** | Show the *output*, not the process. Screen-record of the live system doing the thing; VO over the top. | "This is what a client logs into" — then let the screen talk. | A, B, C |

## Job A — Organic / build-in-public
The top of the funnel. Keep the character. Apply the skeleton with family 1/3/5.
- **Lead with the moment**, not "here's how to." The realization happens *on camera*.
- **CTA is a soft bridge, not a pitch** (`workspace/VOICE.md` CTA architecture): "Building these in public — follow for the messy version" / "Full build's in my bio." Route curious viewers to the pinned VSL or to the YouTube long-form (the social→YT bridge is usually the channel's biggest owned lever). **Never** "like and subscribe" as the only ask.
- **Caption** restates the hook in line 1, recaps the substance for non-watchers, ends with the bio-link bridge. 1 sparing emoji max.
- **Proof-first language only.** Reserve program vocabulary for most-aware content.

## Job B — Launch / offer promo
Conversion-intent, but still platform-native — **earn the pitch.**
- **Structure:** proof/mechanism beats *first* (families 2 + 4 carry this), the ask *last*. Never ask before the mechanism lands.
- **The ask = the launch destination** (bio link, launch window per `workspace/STRATEGY.md`) — imperative + image: *"Bring your laptop. We're building one live."* **Banned promo CTAs:** countdown-to-fake-scarcity, "doors close at midnight," "free strategy call" with no deliverable, "click the link below" with no context.
- **Confirm the time/URL before posting** — `[CONFIRM: launch time + registration URL]`.
- **Test one variable at a time:** post 2 promo cuts that differ **only in the hook** (or only in the CTA), same body — so the winner tells you what moved registrations.
- **Income discipline:** any claim about what *attendees/students* can make carries the earnings-disclosure frame (`workspace/PROOF-BANK.md` honesty section) and is never guaranteed (NEVER-11). The creator's *own* results are self-proof, not an earnings claim — keep client ROI and audience income distinct.

## Job C — Paid ad creative

### C.1 — UGC ad script (45–75s, operator-style)
Shot **vertically on a phone, in a real setting** (office, car, desk — *not* a studio). The format signals "real operator, not a polished marketer."

```
Sec 1–5   — HOOK: pattern-interrupt with a specific dollar amount or a specific workflow gap.
Sec 6–20  — PROBLEM: the gap in {{niche}}, with the workflow × frequency × cost math.
Sec 21–40 — FIX + RECEIPT: what we built for an anonymized {{niche}} client + the specific outcome
            ({{$X}} recovered / {{Y}} hours saved). Real tool named. Artifact on screen.
Sec 41–55 — DISQUALIFIER: who this is / isn't for (raises inbound quality, = authority).
Sec 56–70 — CTA: book the audit / register / DM keyword. One ask.
Sec 71–75 — HONEST SIGN-OFF: one line that anchors operator-credibility.
            "If your gap doesn't match this, reply and I'll tell you who to talk to instead."
```
**Ad constraints:** no "Hey what's up guys" intro · no emoji on overlays · reads naturally aloud · 1–2 specific dollar amounts + 1 real tool name + 1 anonymized client receipt · the earnings disclosure is **NOT in the ad** (it lives on the landing page / offer body) · original VO, no trending audio · proof-first language only.

### C.2 — Awareness hook grid (4 angles × 5 hooks = 20)
Map the hook to where the buyer's head is. The buyer is an experienced operator — anti-hype, specific, named tools.

```
Target: {{niche}} operators. Build 4 angles × 5 hooks = 20.

A · PROBLEM-AWARE   — knows the operational gap. Open with the specific gap + its cost.
B · SOLUTION-AWARE  — knows the category exists, not your approach. Open with the differentiator.
C · PRODUCT-AWARE   — has seen competitors overpromise + doesn't trust them.
                      Open with disqualification + proof (the skeptic's angle).
D · UNAWARE         — doesn't know the gap exists. Open with the workflow walkthrough.

Per hook: (1) text overlay ≤8 words, no emoji  (2) VO line, first 5s, ≤15 words
          (3) visual hook — what's on screen for the first 3s
          (4) B-roll style — UGC phone / screen-record / static / talking head
Output: a table, ranked by predicted hold-rate within each angle.
Constraints: anti-hype, specific $ + named tools throughout, no emoji on overlays,
disclosure NOT in hooks, operator-grade.
```

### C.3 — Iterate: test ONE variable at a time
From a known-converting ad, spin **8 variants that change exactly one thing** so the next winner reveals *what* moved performance:
```
V1 same hook, different mechanism in body       V5 same body+CTA, hook = specific-dollar-led
V2 same hook, different proof point             V6 same body+CTA, hook = contrarian-led
V3 same hook, different CTA (call/audit/DM)     V7 same body+CTA, hook = pattern-interrupt-led
V4 same body+CTA, hook = curiosity-led          V8 same hook+body, different visual format
```
Per variant: exact copy · the one change · the hypothesis · the metric to watch (CPM / CPC / CPL / cost-per-booked-call / cost-per-closed-deal). **Never change two things in one test.**

### C.4 — Ad pre-flight
- **Signal ≥7 on the hook segment specifically** (`workspace/prompts/signal-check.md`).
- **Compliance lens** for regulated niches (health, finance, professional services) — no outcome guarantees, no income-glitch framing (NEVER-11).
- **Reserved vocab:** proof-first only. No program vocabulary in cold ads.
- **Receipt path:** every $ ties to a real anonymized client or cited data — or it's a `[CONFIRM: …]` slot.

## Hard constraints (the reel fails review if any break)
- **No invented numbers.** Every figure → a PROOF-BANK `[VERIFIED]` row or a `[CONFIRM: …]` slot (INV-6).
- **Cold open** — never the banned intros. Lead with the moment.
- **Show, don't claim** — artifact on screen for every claim.
- **Overlays ≤ 8–10 words, no emoji; captions ≤ 1 emoji if load-bearing.**
- **Earn the pitch** (B + C) — no ask before the mechanism/proof lands.
- **CTA = imperative + image, not a question.** No banned CTAs (fake-scarcity timers, "free call" w/o deliverable, bare "link below," like+sub as the only ask).
- **Income claims carry the disclosure frame**; disclosure NOT in ad hooks (lives on landing/offer page). Client ROI ≠ audience income. Never guarantee income (NEVER-11).
- **Reserved-vocabulary discipline** — proof-first language on all three jobs.
- **Read it aloud** — passes the blind-output test (`spec/voice-gates.md`): could the creator say it off the cuff on a live?

## Pre-ship checklist (run before handing off)
1. **Read the VO aloud** — could the creator say it unscripted? (No → rewrite.)
2. **Banned-vocab scan** (INV-7) — strip on sight.
3. **Number audit** — every figure verified or `[CONFIRM: …]`. Nothing invented.
4. **Hook signal ≥7** (`workspace/prompts/signal-check.md`); 9+ = indistinguishable from the creator.
5. **CTA** = imperative + image, correct destination for the job, no banned CTA.
6. **Overlays** ≤ 8–10 words, no emoji, sound-off legible, inside safe margins.
7. **Job B/C only:** pitch lands *after* proof; one variable per A/B test.
8. **Job C only:** compliance lens passed; disclosure off the creative, on the landing page.
