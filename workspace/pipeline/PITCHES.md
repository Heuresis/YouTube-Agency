## Worked example — illustrative (invented; replace with your own)

## E. Why We Don't Sell One-Off Fixes (We Sell Systems) · `reach · sales-reality — grounded operator-opinion`

**Titles**
- Why We Don't Sell One-Off Fixes (We Sell Systems)
- Selling {{the commodity unit}} Is a Trap — Here's What Actually Pays
- The Difference Between a {{$small}} Fix and a {{$large}} System

**The idea.** The reach format that converts honestly — a *real take from running the business*, not a trend-jack. Pulls everyone selling the commodity unit and shows them they're leaving the real money on the table. Installs **a system beats a pile of fixes.** No new client data needed — it's the operator's defended opinion (Asymmetry, per `workspace/prompts/signal-check.md`). Routes the crowd to the case studies. Films now: no proof-gather gate.

**The hook (cold open)**
> I stopped selling one-off {{fixes}} about a year ago, and it's the single thing that took us from charging a few hundred bucks to charging {{full-number large figure}}. `[CONFIRM: the real before/after price points]`
> *(beat)*
> And I know how that sounds. Right now everybody's out here selling {{the commodity unit}} — one workflow, one little thing that does one task. And there's nothing wrong with it. It works, you'll make some money.
> You're basically selling somebody a single security camera, though.
> *(beat)*
> The guy down the street is selling the whole security system — cameras, sensors, monitoring, the alarm — for ten times the price. Same parts. Completely different business.
> So let me show you the difference. Because honestly it's the whole game.

**Scripting layout**
1. **Cold open** — "I stopped selling {{commodity unit}}, it changed what we charge" + the security-camera analogy
2. **Why the one-off is a trap** — anyone builds it from a tutorial, you're a commodity, a cheaper tool drops and you're done
3. **What a system actually is** — the connected whole; the owner stops holding it together in their own head
4. **The example** — take one function → break it into every task it touches = a system `[PROOF-BANK row for the worked example]`
5. **Why they pay 10x** — firing you is like firing a whole team; the perceived value is different in kind
6. **The reframe** — "think in business processes, not individual tasks"
7. **Close** — bridge to a case study + the one CTA destination
