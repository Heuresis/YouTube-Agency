# Creator Intake Form — The Artifact-Gathering Pass

> The collection half of onboarding. This form gathers what already **exists** — files, numbers, links, raw voice material. The interview half is `workflows/client-onboarding/intake.md` (the 30-minute expert download), which extracts what the creator knows tacitly; run this form first, then run the interview on top of what it surfaces.
>
> **The rule: files over forms.** If a doc already exists, send the doc — don't rewrite it. Raw and messy beats clean and summarized; the system encodes from source material, not from polished answers. Budget: one file-dump session + ~60 minutes of answers. Voice memos count as answers.

**Legend:** ✅ = pre-filled from public info (creator corrects if wrong) · ⬜ = needed from the creator · [OPERATOR] = the onboarding operator fills before kickoff

---

## 1 — The file dump (the single biggest ask)

Drop everything below into a shared folder / zip, **as-is**. Don't clean, don't curate, don't dedupe.

- ⬜ **AI context profiles already in use** — ChatGPT custom instructions, Claude project docs, GPT knowledge files, any "about me / about my business" system prompts
- ⬜ **Knowledge bases** — Notion exports, Google Docs, internal wikis: delivery SOPs, fulfillment docs, client-onboarding docs, anything that explains *how you do everything*
- ⬜ **Story / positioning docs** — bios, about-page drafts, podcast one-sheets, anything ever written about who you are
- ⬜ **Content docs** — scripts, hooks files, ideas lists, content calendars, anything you draft captions/posts/videos from
- ⬜ **Sales materials you personally touch** — proposals, SOWs, audit templates, decks, pricing one-pagers
- ⬜ **Email / newsletter sequences**, if any exist

*Markdown / Notion / Docs preferred. Screenshots fine. Voice memos fine.*

---

## 2 — Context profile (confirm + fill the gaps)

[OPERATOR] pre-fills everything findable from public sources (LinkedIn, site, podcast appearances, prior channels) as ✅ lines; the creator corrects anything wrong, then fills:

- ⬜ **Background timeline** — roles, companies, businesses, credentials. The career arc the channel's authority stands on.
- ⬜ **The founding story** — the actual moment/reason you left the old path and started this. Voice memo it; the unrehearsed telling is the asset.
- ⬜ **Prior chapters** — earlier ventures, wins, failures, detours. Which are tellable on camera, and as what (win / failure / lesson)?
- ⬜ **Lane clarification** — what is this channel's lane, stated in one line? If other faces or channels share the same business (co-founders, a company channel, an existing personal brand), define the lane each owns. Two faces must not make the same video; the whole positioning hangs on this answer.
- ⬜ **The NDA / off-limits line** — which past employers, clients, or numbers carry confidentiality limits? How specific can credibility claims get (scale numbers, systems, war stories) before legal gets nervous? List what's usable and what's banned.
- ⬜ **Personal bio** — age, where you're based, family/hobby/identity details you're willing to use on camera (and the ones you're not).

---

## 3 — Proof bank seed (numbers + artifacts)

Hard rule: **no number ships without a source.** Client results ≠ your income; both need receipts.

- ⬜ **3–5 real results you produced** — client engagements, builds, launches, transformations. For each: who (niche + size) → what changed → the price/cost → the measurable result.
- ⬜ For each: can the client/subject be **named**, or anonymized-by-niche only?
- ⬜ **Artifacts per result** — diagrams, dashboards, redacted invoices, screenshots, Loom walkthroughs, testimonials. Whatever exists.
- ⬜ **Personal numbers you'll state on camera** — and the ones you won't. List both explicitly.
- ⬜ **Employer-era or prior-chapter stories** — scale, incidents, "how the big players do it" mechanics. Whatever's tellable per the §2 off-limits line.

---

## 4 — The plan (strategy — one paragraph or one voice memo each)

- ⬜ **YouTube's job.** What does the channel exist to do — fill the offer's pipeline? Build category authority? Feed an audience business? Pick the primary; the rest are side effects.
- ⬜ **The funnel.** Viewer clicks what → lands where → buys what, at what price. Direct links if they exist.
- ⬜ **The buyer.** Who exactly is watching when it's working — title, business size, the problem in *their* words.
- ⬜ **The lane split** (carried from §2). Confirm or redraw the working hypothesis the operator writes here: which lane this channel owns vs. every adjacent face/channel in the same business.
- ⬜ **12-month picture.** What does "it worked" look like — subs, pipeline, revenue, or something else? One concrete sentence.

## 6 — Voice pack (so scripts sound like you, not like AI)

- ⬜ **3–5 unscripted talking videos** — Looms to clients, podcast clips, stories. Raw beats polished; this is the main encoding source.
- ⬜ **10 captions/posts you wrote yourself** (not ghostwritten, not generated)
- ⬜ **One 5-minute voice memo**, answering off the cuff: (a) the best work you ever did, (b) what pisses you off about your industry, (c) explain what you do to a 10-year-old
- ⬜ **Phrases you say constantly** · words you'd **never** say

---

## 7 — Channel + operations

- ⬜ YouTube handle/URL + current state (subs, videos — or "new/dead")
- ⬜ **Analytics access** — operator/manager access to YouTube Studio, or screenshots if there's any history (overview + best/worst video)
- ⬜ Destination links: booking URL, site, lead magnet, link hub
- ⬜ **Hours/week** you'll actually give YouTube · recording setup · editor or no editor · who approves before publish

---

## Minimum to start (if everything else stalls)

1. The file dump (§1) — even half of it
2. The voice pack (§6)
3. Three proof results with real numbers (§3)
4. One paragraph: YouTube's job + the funnel it feeds (§4)
5. The lane-split answer (§2 + §4)
6. [OPERATOR] The reference-channel mimic list (§5)

Everything else can be back-filled while the first scripts are drafted.

## Where each section routes

The form is the raw input; encoding distributes it. Tag every encoded value with its confidence source per the `company.yaml` convention (`[SOLID]` / `[INFER]` / `[GAP]`).

| Form section | Feeds workspace/ | Feeds company.yaml |
|---|---|---|
| §1 File dump | Raw corpus behind all four operating docs | Source material for `[SOLID]` tags across every compartment |
| §2 Context profile | `STRATEGY.md` (positioning), `VOICE.md` (persona, no-fly zones) | Compartment 1 — `creator_identity_matrix` |
| §3 Proof bank seed | `PROOF-BANK.md` | Compartment 3 — `offer_architecture` (proof, case studies); Compartment 2 — `trust_signals_required` |
| §4 The plan | `STRATEGY.md` | Compartments 3 + 4 — `offer_architecture`, `channel_architecture` |
| §5 Content strategy inputs | `CONTENT-ENGINE.md` | Compartment 5 — `content_engine` |

| §7 Channel + operations | `STRATEGY.md` (goals, constraints) | Compartment 1 — `basic_info`; Compartment 11 — `operations_intelligence` |

**Next step after collection:** run the interview (`workflows/client-onboarding/intake.md`) against the gathered material — the five tacit-expertise questions plus the compartment-by-compartment walk. The form gives the interview its evidence; the interview gives the form its judgment calls.
