# Creator Intake — The 30-Minute Expert Download

> When a creator first encodes their channel, the OS runs an intake interview to fill the strategic-layer compartments. This is the encoded version of the "30-minute expert download" methodology.

---

## When to run

- First-time creator setup (clone the template, fill `company.yaml`)
- New strategic-layer hire (a writer, producer, or strategist joining the team needs to internalize the encoding)
- Quarterly encoding-refresh (compartments drift; intake re-runs to catch the changes)
- After a major channel pivot (new niche, new offer, new ICP)

---

## The five questions (extract tacit expertise)

These five questions are designed to surface what the creator knows tacitly but hasn't articulated. Run them in order. Don't skip.

### Q1 — What do you check first?

When a new viewer / lead / customer / situation arrives, what's the first signal you check? What tells you "good fit" or "bad fit" within 10 seconds?

**Why this question matters**: This question surfaces the creator's *prioritization heuristic*. The first thing they check is, by definition, the highest-leverage signal in their decision logic. This goes into Audience Intelligence (the trust signals) and Sales & Conversion (the qualification criteria).

### Q2 — What do beginners always get wrong?

When someone new to your domain tries to do what you do, what's the first mistake they make? The mistake everyone makes that you never make?

**Why this question matters**: This question surfaces the creator's *invariant principle* — the thing they hold true that the market doesn't. This goes into Creator Identity (contrarian beliefs, core philosophy) and into Content Engine (the topics that derive from this principle).

### Q3 — What decision can only you make?

In your business, what's the decision that only you make? The one that, if you delegated, the team would mess up because it requires your specific judgement?

**Why this question matters**: This question surfaces the creator's *irreducible role*. This is what stays with the creator no matter how big the team gets. Everything else can be encoded and delegated. This goes into Operations & Intelligence (team structure, what stays with the creator) and into the parasocial-trust contract (Creator Identity).

### Q4 — When do you override the standard process?

When do you break your own rules? What's the situation where you say "I know my SOP says X, but in this case I'm doing Y"?

**Why this question matters**: This question surfaces the creator's *exception logic*. The OS encodes the SOP in the durable skills layer; the exception logic goes into the Tacit Principles section of relevant skills. Without exception logic, the OS produces output that's correct in the average case and wrong in the edge case.

### Q5 — What separates good from excellent?

In your specific work, what makes the difference between competent output and elite output? What's the move that nobody outside your work sees but it's the difference?

**Why this question matters**: This question is the most important. It surfaces the creator's *taste* — the standard they hold their work to that the market hasn't articulated. This goes into the verification gates (what to check beyond the mechanical), the Tacit Principles (the unwritten rules), and the Blind Output Test calibration.

## The deeper interview (compartment-by-compartment)

After the five questions, the intake walks through the 11 compartments in order. For each compartment, the interviewer (creator or skilled team member) reads the schema and asks specific questions:

### Compartment 1 — Creator Identity Matrix
- What's your communication style — direct / consultative / storytelling / analytical / contrarian?
- What's your one-sentence worldview about your domain?
- What contrarian beliefs do you hold that the niche doesn't?
- What's your unique mechanism — the named thing you do that nobody else does?
- What 5–10 phrases do you literally say verbatim that signal "this is you"?
- What 5–10 phrases break your voice if used?

### Compartment 2 — Audience Intelligence System
- Who is the ideal viewer / customer? Demographics + psychographics.
- What pain points do they experience that nobody else articulates well?
- What's the dominant limiting belief — Worthless (status), Helpless (capability), or Hopeless (safety)?
- What awareness level do they sit at — Unaware / Problem-aware / Solution-aware / Product-aware / Most-aware?
- What's the actual language they use when describing their pain? (Pull from comments, sales calls, surveys.)
- What 10+ objections come up in sales calls?

### Compartment 3 — Offer Architecture
- What's the back-end offer? Coaching / mastermind / course / info-product / agency?
- What's the price point?
- What's the core promise — the transformation?
- What's the unique mechanism that makes the offer work?
- What's the value stack? Bonuses?
- What's your LTV : CAC ratio? (Must be 3:1+ for the OS to greenlight content scaling.)

### Compartments 4–11
Run through each compartment's schema in order. The intake fills as much as the creator has data for; flags everything else as `[GAP — fill in cycle 2]`.

## What the intake produces

- A filled `company.yaml` with at least Foundation tier (50%+) on Compartments 1, 2, 3
- A list of compartment gaps to fill in cycle 2 (often Compartments 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11)
- A first-cycle Blind Output Test baseline (run after the OS has produced 3+ outputs)
- A library-compound entry capturing the intake itself as a structural pattern (becomes input to the next intake)

---

## Cadence after intake

- Week 1: creator runs `/build-channel-architecture`, `/idea-farm`, `/research-brief`, `/write-authority-script`. First content drafted.
- Week 2: creator records and publishes the first OS-produced video.
- Week 4: `/audit-retention` runs on the first published video; creator sees the OS's prediction vs. actual.
- Month 2: 3–4 videos published; first Blind Output Test run; encoding moves to Foundation tier (≥50% completeness).
- Month 3: Compartments 4–11 starting to fill from real channel data; encoding moves toward Solid tier (≥75%).
- Month 6: Compartments at 75–85% across the board; OS produces drafts that need light voice-pass; Solid tier achieved.
- Month 12: Compartments at 90%+ on the strategic layer; Blind Output Test pass rate at 80%+; Elite tier achieved on most skills.

## Common intake failure modes

1. **Creator answers in marketing language, not real language.** "We help entrepreneurs build their dream lifestyle." That's brochure copy. Real language sounds different. Fix: ask follow-up "what would you say to a friend at a dinner party?"
2. **Creator doesn't know their audience's actual language.** They say "they want freedom and impact." That's their projection, not the audience's words. Fix: pull comments, sales-call recordings, survey responses; extract verbatim.
3. **Creator's offer is muddy.** "It's like a coaching program but also a community and there's a course." Multiple offers blended. Fix: separate the offers; pick the ONE that drives most revenue; encode that one fully; the rest as offer-ladder.
4. **Creator can't articulate their unique mechanism.** "I just help people figure it out." That's vapor. Fix: probe — "what's the specific thing you do that nobody else does?" Keep probing until a named mechanism emerges. If it doesn't, the channel doesn't have one yet, and that's its top-priority work.
5. **Intake gets rushed.** Creator wants to skip to skills. Compartments stay at Skeleton tier. Skills produce generic AI output. Fix: do not skip the intake. The OS's quality is a function of compartment completeness. Bad inputs → bad outputs.
6. **Creator answers what they wish were true vs. what is true.** "My audience consists of high-achievers willing to invest." Reality: most are tire-kickers. Fix: ask for evidence. Real numbers from real sales calls.

---

## What the OS refuses to skip

The OS will not produce skill output that depends on a compartment below threshold. It will instead enter interview mode and ask the questions needed to fill the compartment. This is annoying for creators who want to skip ahead. It's also the only way to keep output quality at creator-grade.
