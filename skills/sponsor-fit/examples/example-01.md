# Example: sponsor-fit output

## Context
Sponsor pitch from a project-management software company offering $4,500 mid-roll for a 13-min video.

## Output

```markdown
# Sponsor Fit — Project-Management SaaS Archetype

**Date:** 2026-05-03

## 1. Sponsor Profile
- Product / service: project-management SaaS designed for small service teams
- Sponsor archetype: SaaS productivity tool
- Audience match claim: sponsor claims 60% audience overlap with target
- CPM offered: $4,500 for mid-roll on a 13-min video; estimated impressions 8-12K = $37-56 CPM
- Integration type: mid-roll, 90-second integration
- Term offered: 1 video, optional 3-video pack

## 2. Audience-Fit Check
- Would the audience plausibly use this? **CONDITIONAL** — service operators do use PM software, but channel's anti-positioning is "more tools is the wrong fix." Sponsoring a PM tool sends a mixed signal. Audience overlap is real (probably 40-50%, not the claimed 60%) but the channel's stated worldview rejects "buy more software" as a solution.
- Evidence: ICP includes Asana / Notion in tech stack (compartment 2 — they already have PM tools). Channel philosophy explicitly rejects "tool-stack" framing.

## 3. Brand-Fit Check
- Aligns with parasocial-trust contract? **NO**
- Conflicts with `what_creator_wont_do`:
  - "I will not host sponsorships from companies I haven't tested for 60+ days" — creator hasn't tested this product
  - "I will not produce content that frames the audience's problem as 'wrong tool'" — sponsoring a PM tool implicitly frames it that way
- Channel philosophy alignment: directly conflicts with "more tools is the wrong fix" anti-positioning

## 4. Quality Check
- Creator personally tested ≥ 60 days? **NO** — sponsor is willing to comp the product but creator hasn't used it
- Quality verdict: cannot evaluate without testing

## 5. Economics Check
- Channel CPM band: $30-50 for sponsored integrations on 1-2K subscriber early-stage authority channel (high-buying-power audience density elevates CPM)
- CPM offered: $37-56 — within band
- ROI estimate for sponsor: unknown without click-through data
- Sustainability for channel: $4,500 is meaningful early-stage revenue, but the trust cost likely exceeds the revenue

## 6. Disclosure Compliance
- Region(s): US (50%) + Canada / UK / EU (50%)
- Required disclosure: FTC #ad/#sponsored within first 30s of integration + verbal "This video is sponsored by X" + same for ASA UK and country-specific EU
- Disclosure language acceptable to draft: yes — but parasocial concern remains separate from disclosure compliance

## 7. Verdict
- **NO**
- Reasoning: sponsor fails 3 of 6 checks:
  1. Brand-fit fail — directly conflicts with channel anti-positioning ("more tools is the wrong fix") and with `what_creator_wont_do` (untested product, tool-framing)
  2. Quality fail — no 60+ day test
  3. Audience-fit conditional — real but mixed signal
- The economics ($4,500) is real; the trust cost (1 sponsorship that frames the audience problem as "wrong tool" undoes 4-5 authority videos worth of positioning) exceeds it
- Conditions for reconsidering in future: (a) sponsor product genuinely complementary to Role-Design-First framework (e.g., PM tool that organizes role-based ownership not task-based), (b) creator runs 60+ day test and finds it valuable, (c) sponsor agrees to integration framing that doesn't conflict with anti-positioning

## 8. Integration Script (N/A — verdict NO)
```
