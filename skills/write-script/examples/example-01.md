# Example 01 — the first-30-days retention script (B2B-services founder channel)

> A full worked pass of `/write-script`: the decision trail (mode → awareness → belief → differentiation), the structure choices, and a reconciled script excerpt showing the planning-vs-spoken doctrine in action. The creator, channel, and numbers below are invented for calibration.

## The brief

**Channel:** a B2B-services founder — runs a firm that builds client-onboarding systems for marketing agencies. The channel teaches agency operators; the back-end is a done-with-you systems program.
**Pitch (from `/idea-farm`):** "agencies lose the clients they just signed, and nobody talks about when the loss actually happens."
**Working title:** "Your New Client Decided to Fire You on Day 19"
**Desired action:** download the onboarding diagnostic (lead magnet) → email → pinned VSL.
**Length:** 11 min.

## Step 1 — STRATEGY (the decision trail)

| Decision | Call | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Mode** | authority | Niched topic, installs a belief, routes to the back-end. (The channel's `workspace/CALIBRATION.md` tunes authority proof-forward — case-study-led rather than lecture-led — a calibration note on authority, not a fourth mode.) |
| **Awareness** | problem-aware → solution-aware | Agency owners feel churn; they don't know onboarding is the lever. So the hook's words name the *felt symptom* (the week-two silence), never the mechanism. |
| **Sophistication** | skeptical market | The niche's top videos all say "communicate more, send weekly reports." The audience has heard it; tips-register copy reads as noise. |
| **The ONE belief** | *Client retention is decided by the onboarding sequence in the first 30 days — not by the account manager's effort in month three.* | The belief that makes the back-end (an onboarding system) the obvious next step. One belief, installed all the way. |
| **Differentiation (≥ 3 of 6)** | different insight (retention is decided at day ~19, not at renewal) · better storytelling (one churn arc told day-by-day with artifacts, instead of a tip list) · different positioning (the operator-of-record who pulled the records and counted — "we counted," not "experts say") | Per `six-differentiation-paths-detailed`. |
| **Zero-sum thesis** | beats the niche's top "client retention tips" listicle on insight (day-19 framing vs renewal framing), proof specificity (counted records, a real teardown timeline), and narrative (single arc vs list) | Written down before drafting, per `twenty-eighty-inversion`. |

## Step 2 — STRUCTURE

- **Hook stack:** Contrarian-frame (Reptilian) + identity-mirror (Limbic) + Statistic + bound-promise (Neocortex) — 4 mechanisms in 30s.
- **Loops:** L1 "which day yours breaks on" (opens 0:24, closes 8:40) · L2 "the one email that predicts renewal" (opens 1:10, closes 7:15) · L3 "what the other three agencies run" (opens 0:28, closes 9:20, callback in close).
- **Body:** 4 movements, each re-hook → story → lesson. Pattern interrupts ≤ 60s apart.
- **CTA:** soft — diagnostic lead magnet. **Binge loop:** the week-two-silence video.

## Step 3/4 — the reconciled excerpt (planning beats annotated, spoken text clean)

The planning notes carry the BUT/THEREFORE skeleton. The spoken lines never do.

```markdown
[PLAN — hook. Skeleton: the viewer believes churn is decided at renewal BUT the records
show the decision lands around day 19 THEREFORE the first two weeks are the lever.]

(0:00) — COLD OPEN [on-camera, direct]
The client you signed three weeks ago has already decided whether they're going to
fire you. (SHOW: churn-date scatter — 31 accounts, decision window highlighted) They
decided somewhere around day nineteen. And nothing you ship this quarter moves that
decision as much as what you did in the first two weeks.

(0:12) If you run an agency, you know the week-two quiet. Kickoff call goes great,
everyone's excited, and then the replies start landing a day late, then two days late,
and you tell yourself they're busy. (SHOW: inbox timeline, reply gaps widening)

(0:24) We pulled the onboarding records from fourteen agencies we work with. Eleven of
them lost their newest client inside ninety days, and in all eleven the quiet started
in the same fourteen-day window. [LOOP 1 OPEN] By the end of this video you'll have the
day-by-day sequence the other three run, and you'll know which day yours breaks on.
[LOOP 3 OPEN]

[PLAN — movement 2, the teardown. Skeleton: the account team ran the standard playbook
BUT the client had gone quiet by day twelve THEREFORE effort was never the variable —
the sequence was. L2 stays open until 7:15.]

(3:10) So here's what their onboarding actually looked like, and I want you to count
how much of it is yours. (SHOW: anonymized onboarding timeline, day 0 → day 81)
Contract signed on a Friday. Kickoff the next Thursday — six days of nothing, six days
where the client is wondering what they just paid for. Kickoff goes well, the way
kickoffs always do. Deliverables land on time. The Monday report goes out like
clockwork. And the account churned on day eighty-one anyway. [CREATOR: confirm exact day]

(3:48) The account manager on this one was good, and I want to say that part clearly —
this was never a talent problem. She did everything the playbook asked. The playbook
just has no move for day two, or day four, or day nine, and those turned out to be the
days the client was deciding on. (SHOW: the playbook page, first task dated day 6)
```

### The reconciliation, line by line

The framework-skin draft of the teardown beat read:

> *"Six days. No touchpoint. No proof. The client, already deciding."*

Fragment-stacked — the synthetic tell. The reconciled version keeps the **same beat** in the creator's run-on conversational cadence ("six days of nothing, six days where the client is wondering what they just paid for"). Same skeleton underneath (playbook ran BUT client went quiet THEREFORE the sequence is the variable). Zero "but"/"therefore" spoken — the contrast lives in "She did everything the playbook asked. The playbook just has no move for day two." That is the whole game.

| Beat | Planning note (BUT/THEREFORE skeleton) | Spoken delivery (clean) |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | believes renewal-day BUT records show day ~19 THEREFORE first two weeks are the lever | "They decided somewhere around day nineteen. And nothing you ship this quarter moves that decision as much as…" |
| Teardown | playbook ran BUT quiet by day 12 THEREFORE sequence, not effort, is the variable | "She did everything the playbook asked. The playbook just has no move for day two…" |

## The lesson for the next script

Get the voice anchor right first (the creator's *unedited* phrasing beats any cleaned draft), do the 80% positioning before writing a line, then assemble the components — and run every beat through the reconciliation table so the mechanics never drag the framework's skin into the spoken text.
