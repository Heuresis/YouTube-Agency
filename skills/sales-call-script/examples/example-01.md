# Sales Call Script — The Step-Out Program ($8K)

**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Call length target:** 25 min
**Discovery framework:** 8-stage adapted
**Closing methodology:** crossroads (Helpless audience needs explicit choice framing)

## Opening (3 min)
"Hey [name], thanks for taking the time. Before we dive in — quick context on the call. We have 25 minutes. I want to understand your specific situation, what you've tried, where you're stuck, and whether the program is genuinely a fit. If it's not a fit — I'll tell you, and I'll point you to what is. If it is, we'll talk about what enrollment looks like. Sound good?"

[Permission established. Agenda set.]

## Discovery (15 min)

### Q1 — Context
"In your application you said [paraphrase Q3 — their delegation history]. Walk me through the last 12 months. What did you try, in what order, and what happened?"

### Q2 — Current state
"Right now, what does your delivery week look like? How many hours, how much of it is you personally, what does it cost you?"

### Q3 — Pain (real layer)
"What does the cost of staying where you are look like over the next 12 months? Not the obvious revenue cost — the actual cost. Personal, family, business growth ceiling."

### Q4 — Buying trigger
"What's making this conversation happen right now? Why this month?"

### Q5 — Capability audit
"You've tried delegation before. What blocked it? Hiring? Onboarding? Reverting under pressure? Solo attempt without checkpoint?"

### Q6 — Outcome desired
"If you stepped out of delivery in 12 weeks — what changes for you? What does the next 6 months look like that's different?"

### Q7 — Objection surface
"What concerns do you have about a 12-week program at this price? Walk me through what's giving you hesitation."

### Q8 — Decision criteria
"If we said yes today, what would have to be true for you to commit? What's the structural decision that has to happen?"

## Pitch (5 min)

"Okay, based on what you said — [synthesize their pain + desire in their own words]. That's exactly what the Step-Out Program is for. Quick walkthrough:

- Mechanism: Role-Design-First framework. 4 components: outputs / decisions / checkpoints / SOP-ownership. We design the role you want to delegate first; hiring comes second.
- Structure: 12 weeks, group cohort of 8-12 operators in your stage, weekly group calls + 4 1:1 strategic check-ins + role-library template pack + delegation-reset playbook.
- Outcome: by week 12, you have at least one role designed + filled or being filled, with the structural foundation in place.
- Investment: $8,000 one-time or 3 × $2,975. Skill-acquisition guarantee — full refund if you complete + submit work + don't have a delegate-able role designed by week 12.

Is this the structural piece you were looking for, or is there something else underneath that we haven't talked about?"

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Underlying belief | Reframe (verbatim) |
|---|---|---|
| "I've tried delegation before, didn't work" | Helpless | "Your last attempt failed because the role didn't exist before the hire. That's the upstream piece this program addresses. The framework is sequence-reversal — design first, hire second. Most operators here have failed at delegation 1-3 times before. That's the typical starting point." |
| "$8K is more than I planned to spend" | Worthless or readiness gap | "Walk me through the math. Founder time recovered + revenue growth typical = 4-12× ROI in year 1 for operators who complete the program. The investment math works if you're at $200K+ revenue. Are you saying $8K is hard cash flow, or hard psychologically?" |
| "I should be able to figure this out alone" | Worthless | "I tried that for 4 years. Most operators in the cohort tried it for 2-3+. Solo redesign almost always misses the structural blind spot — there's nobody outside the system to catch what you can't see from inside. The accompaniment is the thing that closes the gap." |
| "What if I drop out halfway" | Hopeless | "The skill-acquisition guarantee covers that. Complete the program + submit the work + don't have a delegate-able role designed by week 12 = full refund. What we're betting on is the structure works for operators in your situation. We're holding the risk." |
| "Is now the right time?" | Readiness | "Tell me about your next 90 days. If you push this 6 months, what's structurally different that makes it easier later? Usually nothing — just compounded delivery hours and another failed delegation attempt. But if you have a specific reason..." |
| "My business is too unique" | Worthless | "Every operator here believes that. Some are genuinely unique in their judgement work. None are unique in their delivery work — that's the part that systematizes. The framework preserves the unique part. We design around it." |
| "I want to think about it" | Readiness or undecided | "Okay — what specifically do you want to think about? If it's the structural fit, I can answer. If it's the budget, let's name that. If it's the timing, let's name that. The 'think about it' often hides one of those three — which one is closest?" |

## Close — Crossroads methodology

"You've got two paths from here. Path 1: you don't enroll. You go back to the same operating model. 12 months from now, you'll likely be where you are now or slightly worse — same revenue, same hours, same delegation pattern. The math hasn't changed.

Path 2: you enroll today. You start in 2 weeks. By week 12 you have at least one role designed + filled. By month 6, your weekly hour cost is structurally lower. By month 12, you're operating in a fundamentally different mode.

Both paths are real choices. The cost of Path 1 is the operating model you described as untenable on this call. The cost of Path 2 is $8,000 + 5 hours/week for 12 weeks.

Which path are you choosing?"

[If yes] → Enrollment + onboarding sequence (Yes branch below)
[If no] → "Walk me through what's behind the no — is it timing, fit, or budget?"
[If undecided] → "What would have to be true to make the decision?"

## Why this output is calibration-grade

Objection handling uses reframes from audience library (not generic "let me address that"). Crossroads close calibrated to Helpless audience (explicit choice framing helps the dominant belief commit). Post-call sequence has 3 branches (yes/no/undecided), each with cadence. No income guarantees ("4-12× ROI typical" framed as range from prior cases, not promised). Banned-vocab clean.
