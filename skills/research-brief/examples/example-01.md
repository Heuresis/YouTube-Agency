- Topic: "The 6 Patterns of Failed Service-Business Delegation"
- Mode: authority
- Target awareness: Problem-aware
- Belief: 1 ("Delegation failure isn't a hiring problem; it's structural")

## 2. Competitive Scan
| Archetype | Angle | Working | Missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| The hiring-coach archetype | "Hire better people" | Concrete advice on interviewing, vetting | No address of what role they're being hired into |
| The SOP-evangelist archetype | "Document everything" | Tactical SOP templates | Treats SOPs as foundational; they're third in the sequence |
| The VA-management archetype | "Get a great VA" | Practical task offload | Confuses task delegation with role design |

**Differentiation:** none of the 4 archetypes addresses the structural premise — the role doesn't exist yet. Hiring fails because there's no role for the hire to walk into. This channel's angle is the upstream sequence: design role → fill role → systematize role.

## 3. Fact-Check
| Claim | Source | Confidence |

## 4. Structural Beats Hypothesis
- **Hook hypothesis:** Contrarian-Frame + Statistic-Shock + Bound-Promise (3 mechanisms in first 30s)
  - Reptilian (0-3s): "Your last delegation hire failed. The problem wasn't the person you hired."
  - Limbic (3-15s): "I've watched this pattern in 80 service businesses. Across all 80, the same 6 failure patterns repeat. You're probably 2 of them."
  - Neocortex (15s+): "By the end of this video, you'll know which patterns you're in — and what to fix structurally before you try to hire again."

- **BUT-THEREFORE arc:**
  - This: every operator with the bottleneck problem tries to delegate
  - BUT: most attempts fail in 60-90 days — and the failure pattern repeats
  - THEREFORE: the fix isn't trying again with better people; the fix is upstream structural redesign

- **Beats:**
  1. Hook + claim setup (90 seconds)
  2. Pattern 1 — "Delegating tasks instead of roles" (90 sec)
  3. Pattern 2 — "Hiring before designing the role" (90 sec)
  4. Pattern 3 — "No decision-rights handoff" (90 sec)
  5. Pattern 4 — "SOPs without ownership" (90 sec)
  6. Pattern 5 — "Reverting under pressure" (90 sec)
  7. Pattern 6 — "Solo delegation attempt with no accompaniment" (90 sec)
  8. Synthesis — the structural fix preview (90 sec)
  9. Close + CTA — lead magnet (the 6 Failure Patterns PDF) + pinned VSL link (60 sec)

- **Loop architecture:**
  - Loop 1: open at 0:08 — "by pattern 4, you'll see why your last system died" — close at pattern 4 reveal
  - Loop 2: open at 2:30 — "the most common pattern is also the easiest to fix" — close at pattern 5 (most common = pattern 5)
  - Loop 3: callback in close — "earlier I said 80 businesses — here's the one that broke the pattern"

- **Close + CTA:**
  - Lead magnet: "The 6 Failure Patterns of Service-Business Delegation" PDF — diagnostic that surfaces which patterns the operator falls into
  - Soft pinned-VSL mention (channel-architecture handles deeper conversion)

- "hire better people" (oversimplification + topic of competitor archetype)
- "scale your business" (canonical creator anti-phrase)
- "automate your work" (channel anti-positioning)
- "outsource everything" (wrong frame)
- "delegate or die" (cliché)
- "fire yourself from your business" (rhetoric)

## 7. Recommended Hook Formula
**Contrarian-Frame** primary + **Statistic-Shock** secondary + **Bound-Promise** structural commitment.

Reasoning: target awareness is Problem-aware → Solution-aware bridge. Audience has already tried delegation 1-4 times unsuccessfully (Helpless / Capability transformation). Contrarian-frame ("the problem wasn't the person") interrupts their default attribution. Statistic ("80 businesses, 6 patterns") backs authority + creates curiosity gap. Bound-promise ("by the end you'll know which patterns you're in") justifies watch time. PAST and AID hooks weaker for this topic — audience isn't story-led on this specific pain; they're pattern-led.

## Why this output is calibration-grade
The brief produces an obvious hook in seconds — the script-writer doesn't need to invent. VOC quotes are verbatim with sources. Competitor archetypes are anonymized + analyzed for what's missing (not what's wrong with them). Fact-check has no fabricated stats; "most" / "many" / "80+ businesses" all backed by creator's counted experience. BUT-THEREFORE arc is explicit. Loop architecture has 3 loops with specific open/close placements. Recommended hook formula is reasoned against awareness level + transformation type, not picked arbitrarily.
