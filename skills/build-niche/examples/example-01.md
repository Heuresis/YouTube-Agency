# Example: build-niche output

## Context
- Mode: n/a (foundations skill)
- Channel stage: pre-launch (creator has 6 years domain experience, no published channel yet)
- Compartment completeness at run time: 0% — seeder skill, no thresholds

## Input
"I'm a former operations consultant. I want to start a YouTube channel that converts to my $8K group coaching program for self-employed service providers (consultants, agencies, freelancers under $500K revenue) who feel stuck in delivery and want to systematize. My differentiation thesis is that I think most productivity content for this audience misses the actual problem: they don't need more tools, they need to remove themselves from delivery. Help me lock the niche."

## Output

```markdown
# Niche Definition — [Channel Handle TBD]

**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Version:** 1.0
**Confidence:** HIGH

## 1. Primary Niche
Operations & systematization for self-employed service providers

## 2. Sub-Niche
Self-employed service providers ($100K–$500K revenue) stuck in delivery, who want to systematize their business so they can step out of doing-the-work and into running-the-business.

## 3. The 3-Vector Score
- Creator depth: 9/10 — 6 years of operations consulting, lived the problem, built the playbook for clients. 250+ videos here without burnout is realistic.
- Audience economics: 8/10 — service providers in this revenue band have $5K–$15K of discretionary budget for systems work. The $8K offer is priced inside the audience's natural buying range.
- Offer alignment: 10/10 — niche audience IS the offer's target avatar. No bridge required.
- **Total: 27/30** — viable. Lock.

## 4. Differentiation Thesis
- Primary path: **Unique enemy / contrarian frame** — "more tools is the wrong fix; the right fix is removing yourself from delivery." The niche is saturated with productivity-tool-stack content; very little speaks to the founder-bottleneck root cause.
- Secondary path: **Unique credibility source** — 6 years of inside-the-business consulting where the operator saw the actual problem repeated across 100+ engagements. The credibility is "I've watched this fail in 100 businesses, here's the pattern."
- Anti-positioning: NOT a productivity channel. NOT a Notion-template channel. NOT a hustle-hard channel. The channel is operations-as-leverage-out-of-delivery.

## 5. Adjacent Niches (Reach-Mode Pool)
| Adjacent Niche | Shared Limiting Belief | Reach-Mode Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Founder burnout / delegation | Hopelessness about ever stepping out of delivery | "I quit my agency CEO role at $1.2M revenue because I was the bottleneck — here's what I learned" 1-vs-N format |
| Hiring & team-building for solos | Helplessness about managing people | Before-vs-after format — a solo's team grows from 0 to 4 |
| Pricing & packaging for service businesses | Worthlessness about charging more | Then-vs-now format — a service provider doubles rates after switching from time-billing to outcome-billing |
| The "trapped operator" archetype | All three triadic beliefs (Worthless / Helpless / Hopeless) | Documentary-style storytelling about real (anonymized) operator turnaround stories |

## 6. Out-of-Scope List
1. Productivity-app reviews — would attract the audience the channel actively rejects
2. AI-tool-stack videos — same; pulls wrong viewer
3. Time-management hacks — frames the problem incorrectly (bottleneck is structural, not time)
4. "Quit-your-job, start-an-agency" content — wrong stage of audience
5. Investment / passive income content — different limiting belief, wrong audience economics for the offer
6. Personal lifestyle / vlog content — breaks the parasocial-trust contract (the on-camera persona is operational, not lifestyle)
7. Drop-shipping / e-commerce niches — wrong audience pain
8. "Make $X in 30 days" income-claim content — Truth Gate violation risk + wrong belief frame

## 7. The 5-Year Test
- Can creator make 250+ videos here without burnout? **YES**
- Reasoning: the operations-as-leverage thesis has the depth to support multi-series progression: foundational ops → delegation → systems → team → scaling → exit. 5+ years of progressive content without repeating.
```

## Why this output is calibration-grade
The niche definition is narrow enough to be specific (sub-niche names the audience demographic, revenue band, and limiting belief) but broad enough to support 250+ videos. The differentiation thesis names the enemy (more-tools framing) which gives every future video a contrarian angle. The adjacent niches are tested on belief overlap, not topic adjacency, so reach-mode videos pull the right viewer. The out-of-scope list includes hard calls (lifestyle vlog, AI-tool-stacks) that would perform but break trust — the spine is visible.
