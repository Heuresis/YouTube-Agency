# Series Build — 5–15 Video Belief-Installation Series

> Designing a 5–15 video belief-installation series. Each video in the series targets a specific belief in the 8 required beliefs (or a niche-calibrated belief stack), routes to the next video in the sequence, and compounds toward conversion to the back-end offer. Series are higher-effort to design than standalone videos but produce structurally durable conversion infrastructure.

## When to invoke this playbook

A series-build is appropriate when:

1. **The audience has multiple beliefs to install before they buy.** Most high-ticket offers ($3K+) require all 8 of the standard beliefs (Reproduction → Derivative Desire → Can-Fulfill → Urgency → This-Offer → Better/Faster → Trust-Person → Trust-Expertise). A single video struggles to install all 8 from cold.
2. **The audience is solution-aware → product-aware.** They know the problem; they're evaluating solutions. The series carries them across solution-evaluation into product-commitment.
3. **The channel has at least 3 months of single-video learnings.** Series design relies on knowing what the channel's hooks, retention curves, and conversion patterns look like. Premature series-building wastes effort.
4. **The creator has a coherent body of teaching to span 5–15 videos.** Without enough material, the series stretches thin.
5. **The channel architecture supports series playlists.** End-screens, playlist sequencing, and description-link stack must support the series's navigation.

## Pre-design prerequisites

- Channel Architecture compartment ≥ 70%
- Audience compartment ≥ 70% (the limiting belief and awareness distribution must be locked)
- Offer compartment ≥ 60%
- Hook & Retention OS compartment ≥ 50% (calibrated from prior single-video data)

If thresholds are not met, the OS routes to upstream skills first.

## Step 1 — Awareness-level mapping

Identify which awareness level the series targets:

- **Unaware → Problem-aware series** — the audience doesn't yet know they have the problem. Series 1: name the problem; series 2: dimensionalize the problem; series 3: open the question of solutions. Rare in the OS's bullseye but applies to some niches.
- **Problem-aware → Solution-aware series** — the audience knows the problem; series introduces the solution category. Common entry-point series.
- **Solution-aware → Product-aware series** — the audience is evaluating solutions; series positions the operator's specific mechanism / offer. The most common conversion series.
- **Product-aware → Most-aware series** — the audience knows the operator's offer exists; series surfaces the specific objections and resolves them. The pre-buy series.

The series's primary awareness target informs every video's tone, hook formula, depth, and CTA.

## Step 2 — Belief mapping

Map each video in the series to the belief(s) it primarily installs. The 8 standard beliefs:

1. **Reproduction (this is real)** — outcomes documented; results provable
2. **Derivative Desire (I want what they have)** — identity-aspiration; transformation desirability
3. **Can-Fulfill (this can solve my problem)** — mechanism explained; competence demonstrated
4. **Urgency (now matters)** — cost of inaction; window-of-opportunity
5. **This-Offer (this specifically)** — differentiation; specificity of the offer's vehicle
6. **Better/Faster (vs alternatives)** — competitive positioning; tradeoff clarity
7. **Trust-Person (the operator)** — credibility; parasocial trust; integrity demonstration
8. **Trust-Expertise (the method)** — track record; depth-of-method evidence

A 5-video series typically maps 1–2 beliefs per video. A 10-video series typically maps 1 belief per video, with reinforcement layered. A 15-video series typically devotes 2 videos to the highest-leverage beliefs (Trust-Person, Can-Fulfill) and 1 video each to the others.

For niche-specific belief stacks (e.g., personal-finance has additional credibility and regulatory beliefs), substitute the niche-calibrated stack. The mapping discipline is the same.

## Step 3 — Sequence design

The series's order matters. Beliefs install best in a specific sequence; misordering collapses conversion.

### Standard 8-belief installation sequence

**For a 8-video series mapping 1:1 to beliefs:**

1. Reproduction — "this is real" — open the series with proof. A case study or named outcome.
2. Trust-Person — "you can trust me" — operator-credibility video. About-me, lived experience, credentials.
3. Trust-Expertise — "the method works" — the named mechanism / framework introduction.
4. Can-Fulfill — "this can solve YOUR problem" — applied mechanism on the audience's specific situation.
5. Better/Faster — "this beats alternatives" — comparison to the niche's standard solutions.
6. Derivative Desire — "I want what they have" — identity-aspiration and transformation visualization.
7. Urgency — "now matters" — cost of inaction; window-of-opportunity.
8. This-Offer — "this specifically" — the back-end offer's specifics and CTA.

This is structurally equivalent to the 15-step VSL spine at the series-of-videos level. The pinned VSL compresses the same arc into 30–60 minutes; the series stretches it across 8 videos for audiences who need slower pacing or who don't watch one 30-minute video.

### Alternate sequence: the case-study-led series

Some channels lead with case studies (the case-study archivist's pattern):

1. Case 1 — full case study, anonymized client, named outcome
2. Case 2 — different segment, different outcome; pattern emerges
3. Case 3 — different segment again; pattern is now durable
4. Mechanism reveal — "here's the structural pattern across the cases"
5. Self-application — "here's how the pattern applies to your situation"
6. Differentiation — "here's why this beats [alternative approaches]"
7. Trust-Person — operator-credibility; how they came to develop the pattern
8. Offer — the specific back-end vehicle

This sequence works when the channel's primary credibility lever is documented case work.

### Alternate sequence: the contrarian-led series

For channels with a strong contrarian thesis:

1. Contrarian frame video — "here's what the niche says; here's why it's wrong"
2. Replacement-frame intro — "here's the structural alternative"
3. Mechanism dive — name and explain the mechanism
4. Application — apply the mechanism to typical audience situation
5. Case proof — case study showing the mechanism in action
6. Trust-Person — operator's credibility for this contrarian frame
7. Urgency / cost-of-inaction — "the niche's mistake costs you X"
8. Offer — the back-end vehicle that delivers the alternative

This sequence works for the contrarian-narrator archetype's audiences.

## Step 4 — End-screen routing logic

Each video in the series routes via end-screen to:

- **Primary**: the next video in the series (the natural sequel)
- **Secondary** (optional): the foundational playlist or the channel-thesis video (for new viewers landing on a mid-series video)

The end-screen pattern should be deterministic — the viewer who completes Video 3 sees the Video 4 end-card prominently. The end-screen is not "the algorithm-suggested next" but the channel architecture's belief-installation path.

For viewers who land on a mid-series video (e.g., they searched a topic and found Video 5 of a 8-video series), the description and pinned comment link to Video 1 of the series with framing: "This is Video 5 of an 8-video series. Start here: [Video 1 link]."

## Step 5 — Completion-rate diagnostics

A series's success metric is completion rate — what percentage of viewers who started Video 1 watched all the way through to Video N. Benchmarks:

- **5-video series**: target 30–40% completion (Video 1 → Video 5)
- **8-video series**: target 20–30% completion
- **10–15-video series**: target 15–25% completion

Below the band: diagnose where the drop happens. Run `/audit-retention` on each video; identify the highest-drop video; revise it.

Common drop-off points:

- **Video 2** — the trust-Person video drops if the audience didn't bond with the operator in Video 1. Revise Video 1's parasocial-trust mechanism.
- **Video 4** — the can-Fulfill video drops if the mechanism feels too generic. Revise the application's specificity.
- **Video 6** — the derivative-desire video drops if the identity-transformation isn't compelling. Revise the identity framing.
- **Video 7 / Video 8** — the urgency / offer video drops if the prior videos didn't build sufficient belief. Revise upstream rather than the urgency / offer videos themselves.

The OS resists fixing the late videos when the leak is upstream. INV-1 (six-layer diagnostic) applies at the series level.

## Step 6 — CTAs by series position

CTAs (calls to action) calibrate by position in the series:

- **Videos 1–3** (early series, belief-foundation) — soft CTA: "subscribe + watch the next video." No back-end offer pitch yet.
- **Videos 4–5** (mid-series, mechanism / application) — slightly stronger CTA: "subscribe + watch the next video + lead-magnet bridge for those wanting to apply this."
- **Videos 6–7** (late series, differentiation / urgency) — explicit but not pushy CTA: "if this resonates, the [back-end offer] is the next step. Watch the pinned VSL for details."
- **Video 8 / final** (offer video) — direct CTA: "apply for [back-end offer] / book a call / pinned-VSL link."

The CTA escalation matches the belief-installation curve — pitching too early collapses conversion; pitching too late leaves money on the table.

## Step 7 — Production approach

Production decisions for series content:

- **Visual continuity** — the series's videos should share visual grammar (same set, same framing, same color palette, same lower-thirds). Continuity signals "this is a series" before the viewer reads it.
- **Audio continuity** — same intro music, same transition stings, same background music tier. Audio sameness is parasocial-trust infrastructure.
- **Title pattern** — series titles share a structural pattern. "Series Name — Part 1: [topic]." Or numbered subtitles. The pattern signals the series at a glance.
- **Thumbnail series-mark** — a small visual element on each thumbnail signals "this is part of the series" (a numbered tag, a color band, a series badge).

Production effort is concentrated upfront. Once the series's visual / audio / title grammar is locked, each new video is faster to produce.

## Step 8 — Series launch logistics

Two launch patterns:

### Drip launch (preferred for most series)

- Video 1 publishes Week 1
- Video 2 publishes Week 2
- And so on for 5–15 weeks

Drip launch matches normal cadence. The audience absorbs the series at their pace. Each video in the series has its own launch (algorithmic surfacing, audience response, leak-audit). The series compounds slowly but durably.

### Burst launch (occasional)

- All N videos publish in the same week (e.g., Mon / Tue / Wed / Thu / Fri for a 5-video series)
- The audience binges; the algorithm's signal is strong; the series compounds quickly

Burst launch is operationally heavy and risks overwhelming the audience. Reserved for major series or course-launch tie-ins.

## Step 9 — Series playlist setup

The series gets its own playlist on the channel. Playlist setup:

- **Title**: clear and search-optimized
- **Description**: explains the series, lists each video, and references the foundational playlist
- **Order**: series-natural order, locked (do not allow algorithm to reorder)
- **Featured-section placement**: prominent on the channel page
- **End-of-each-video routing**: the end-screen auto-routes to the next video in the playlist

A well-architected series playlist behaves like a course — the viewer enters at Video 1, watches through, and finishes with a clear next-step.

## Step 10 — Post-series leak audit + library-compound

After the final video publishes (drip launch) or the launch week ends (burst launch):

- Run `/leak-audit` specific to the series
- Compute completion rate (Video 1 → Video N)
- Compute application rate per series-video (which video drove the most applications?)
- Compute close rate for applications that came from the series

Run `/library-compound` to extract:
- Which beliefs installed well (high-completion videos at that belief's position)
- Which beliefs underinstalled (low-completion or low-conversion videos)
- Hook patterns that worked across the series
- Retention curve archetypes per video

The post-series compound updates compartment 6 (hook & retention OS), compartment 8 (distribution & algorithm), and feeds the next series's design.

## Failure modes

### Premature series

**Failure**: building a series before the channel has 3+ months of single-video learnings. The series's calibration is wrong; completion rates underwhelm.
**Prevention**: gate. Don't run series-build until single-video benchmarks are stable.

### Misordered beliefs

**Failure**: pitching the offer (Video 8) before installing Trust-Person (Video 2) because the operator wanted to "get to the close faster." Conversion collapses; viewers bounce mid-series.
**Prevention**: the belief-mapping discipline (Step 2 + Step 3) is non-negotiable. The OS gates on it.

### Inconsistent production tier

**Failure**: Video 1 is high-production; Video 5 is low-production. The series's coherence breaks; viewers lose trust.
**Prevention**: visual / audio / title grammar locked in step 7. Production tier held constant.

### Series stretch

**Failure**: a 5-video idea stretched into 12 videos to "get more content." Each video underinstalls its belief; completion craters.
**Prevention**: the belief-mapping (Step 2) is the constraint. If the series only has 5 beliefs to install, it's a 5-video series.

### Series compression

**Failure**: an 8-belief installation crammed into 4 videos. Each video tries to install too many beliefs; none install fully.
**Prevention**: same — the belief-mapping is the constraint.

### Skipping the post-series compound

**Failure**: the series finishes; the operator moves on. The series's data isn't extracted; future series make the same mistakes.
**Prevention**: `/library-compound` is non-negotiable post-series.

## Series naming conventions

Recommended:

- **Series Name — Part N: [Topic]** — clear, deterministic
- **The [Mechanism] Series, Part N** — for mechanism-first-educator archetype channels
- **Numbered "Episode N" titles** — for serialized story-driven content

Avoid:

- "Part 1 of Many" / "Part 1 of ?" — implies operator hasn't planned the series
- Vague series names that don't telegraph the topic
