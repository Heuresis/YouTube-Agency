# Reach Script Template

> Long-form reach script template. Used for reach-mode videos that target broad-appeal audiences beyond the niche. Mode: reach. Target length: 4–10 minutes (700–1500 words). Awareness target: problem-aware (typically). Hook formulas favor contrast / contrarian / story-driven structures.

## Frontmatter

```yaml
title: <working title>
mode: reach
awareness_target: <unaware | problem-aware | solution-aware (lower-bound only)>
length_target_min: <4-10>
word_count_target: <700-1500>
content_pillar: <pillar from compartment 5; reach pillar>
target_avd_pct: <typically 55-60>
target_ctr_pct: <typically 8-14>
hook_formula: <Contrast | Contrarian-Frame | Statistic | Curiosity-Gap | Story-Beat>
contrast_format_type: <1-vs-N | before-vs-after | then-vs-now | cheap-vs-expensive | newbie-vs-expert | none>
end_screen_target: <foundational playlist OR pinned VSL>
cta: <typically channel-architecture-only or soft-subscribe>
```

## Section 0 — Cold open (0:00–0:05)

For reach: the visual must be immediately unmistakable. The audience is broader and less committed; the first 5 seconds is everything.

```
[VISUAL: <high-contrast visual — character, setup, or thumbnail-aligned moment>]
[AUDIO: <energy-forward; high-pace>]

<HOOK SENTENCE — telegraphs the contrast / contrarian / curiosity-gap immediately>
```

## Section 1 — Contrast / contrarian-frame stack (0:05–0:30)

Telegraph the structural surprise immediately. For reach videos, retention mechanisms are denser than authority.

```
[CONTRAST STACK — montage of A vs B (or 1 vs N) in 3–5 quick cuts]
OR
[CONTRARIAN FRAME — "everyone says X, here's why X is wrong"]

<COMMITMENT — what the viewer is signing up for>

<BOUND PROMISE — specific outcome + duration of payoff>

[PATTERN INTERRUPT — cut / b-roll / supers]
```

## Section 2 — Re-hook + premise (0:30–1:30)

Set up the premise the body will pay off.

```
"Here's the question we're going to answer, and why most people get it wrong."

<PREMISE — 3–5 sentences that frame the reach payoff>
<STAKES — why this matters; cost-of-inaction or curiosity-gap>
```

## Section 3 — Body block 1 (1:30–4:00)

For contrast formats: introduce the first comparison. For contrarian: dismantle the first canonical belief.

```
[RE-HOOK]

<COMPARISON or DISMANTLE — 60–90 seconds>

<EVIDENCE — specific examples; voice-of-viewer language>

[CALLBACK to opening; new loop opens]

[PATTERN INTERRUPT]
```

## Section 4 — Body block 2 (4:00–6:30)

Second comparison or second canonical belief.

```
[RE-HOOK]

<COMPARISON OR DISMANTLE 2>

<RESOLUTION ASYMMETRY — name the lever; this is the value-add the viewer takes home>

[CALLBACK]
```

## Section 5 — Synthesis (6:30–7:30)

Pull the contrast/contrarian into a single takeaway. Resolution-asymmetric: one side wins on dimension A; the other on dimension B; the real lever is dimension C.

```
<SYNTHESIS — 30–60 seconds>

<IDENTITY-ASPIRATIONAL FRAMING — link the takeaway to who the viewer can become>
```

## Section 6 — CTA (7:30–end)

For reach videos, CTAs are channel-architecture-driven (subscribe + foundational playlist + light pinned-VSL reference). The reach video does not pitch the back-end offer directly — the channel architecture routes the converted viewer.

```
<SOFT CTA — 20–40 seconds>

"If this resonated, the channel goes deeper on [topic]. The starting playlist is [foundational playlist link]."

[END-SCREEN: foundational playlist OR pinned VSL routing]
```

## Production cues for reach

- **Cut density**: higher than authority (4–7 cuts per minute)
- **B-roll density**: 50–70% — visual variety holds the broad audience
- **Music**: dynamic — energy shifts at every section transition
- **On-screen text**: prominent; the broad audience often watches with sound off until committed
- **Color grading / motion graphics**: production-tier higher than typical authority video; reach videos compete in the algorithmic surface and must look it
- **Pattern-interrupts**: every 30–45s for reach (denser than authority's 60s)

## Banned reach hook patterns

INV-7 + INV-17 apply. Banned:
- "You won't believe what happens next"
- "Wait for it"
- "This will blow your mind"
- "Smash that subscribe"
- Manufactured shock without payoff
- Engagement-bait phrasings

Genuine reach mechanisms:
- Contrast format (1-vs-N, before-vs-after, etc.)
- Contrarian frame (niche-canonical belief flipped)
- Story-beat (specific personal stake, dated, sensory)
- Statistic shock (specific, sourced number)
- Open question (genuine, audience-research-driven)

## Verification checklist (pre-publish)

- [ ] Hook density ≥ 3 mechanisms in first 30s (INV-11)
- [ ] Pattern-interrupt cadence ≥ every 45s
- [ ] Title-thumbnail-promise alignment is precise (INV-10)
- [ ] No engagement-bait (INV-17)
- [ ] No banned vocabulary (INV-7)
- [ ] Predicted AVD ≥ reach-mode floor for channel
- [ ] Mode declared: reach
- [ ] Awareness target appropriate for reach
- [ ] CTA is channel-architecture-only (no direct offer pitch)
- [ ] End-screen routes to foundational playlist or pinned VSL

## Word count by length

| Video length | Target word count |
|---|---|
| 4 minutes | ~700 |
| 6 minutes | ~1000 |
| 8 minutes | ~1300 |
| 10 minutes | ~1600 |

Reach videos run lower wpm because of higher cut density and higher b-roll coverage (which has less voiceover).

## Reach video distinct from authority

The structural difference: reach videos do not install belief toward the offer. They build audience and route via channel architecture. The reach video stays clean (no offer pitch); the channel architecture (pinned VSL, end-screen, community-tab, email capture) does the conversion.

Mixing offer pitch into reach videos collapses both — reach drops because pitch dilutes the entertainment value, and conversion drops because the audience isn't ready.
