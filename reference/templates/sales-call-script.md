# Sales Call Script Template

> Inbound application call script. The sales call is the final stage of the application funnel — the call where qualified applicants close into the back-end offer or are mutually disqualified. The script is a structured framework, not a verbatim read; the operator (or closer) adapts in real time but follows the structural sequence.

## Pre-call preparation

Before the call, the closer reviews:

- The applicant's full application form (8–12 questions)
- Notes from any prior touchpoints (email, DM, prior application)
- The applicant's videos watched (if traceable)
- Any case studies in the closer's pocket that match the applicant's segment

Preparation is 5–10 minutes per call. Calls without preparation underperform structurally.

## Call structure (40–60 minutes)

### Block 1 — Frame + rapport (0:00–5:00)

Set the agenda. Establish the call's structure. Put the applicant at ease.

```
"Thanks for the application — appreciate the detail you put into it.
Here's how I want to spend the next [40–50] minutes:
- I'm going to ask you some questions about your situation, what you've tried, where you're stuck
- If we figure out we're a fit, I'll walk you through how we'd work together
- If we figure out we're not, I'll point you to where you should look instead
- Sound good?"
```

The frame is honest. The applicant knows the call has two outcomes (yes or no), not three (yes, no, or "let me think about it"). This filters noise early.

### Block 2 — Diagnosis (5:00–20:00)

80% applicant talking, 20% closer talking. Dig into:

- Current state — revenue, role, situation, scale
- Past attempts — what they've tried, why it didn't work
- Root cause — what they think the constraint is

Discovery questions (calibrate to discovery_framework):

```
"Walk me through your situation right now. Where are you, in [revenue/scale/state]?"

"What have you tried to fix this? What worked, what didn't?"

"If you had to name the one thing that's keeping you stuck, what would it be?"

"What's the cost to you of staying where you are for the next [6–12 months]?"
```

The applicant talks; the closer asks clarifying questions. Resist the urge to pitch.

### Block 3 — Gap + cost-of-inaction (20:00–30:00)

Reflect back the gap between current and desired state. Quantify cost of staying stuck.

```
"OK so what I'm hearing is:
- Right now you're at [current state]
- You want to be at [desired state]
- The gap is [specific gap]
- What's been blocking you is [their own diagnosis]
- The cost of staying stuck for another year would be [specific cost]"
```

The cost-of-inaction is calibrated. For business audiences: revenue impact. For health audiences: well-being impact. For mindset audiences: identity-level impact.

The applicant agrees with the framing or corrects it. Either way, the gap is now explicit.

### Block 4 — Mechanism + pitch (30:00–40:00)

Connect the applicant's situation to the offer's mechanism. How would the offer specifically solve their gap?

```
"Here's how we'd work together:
- The first [time period] we'd focus on [specific intervention]
- That would address [their stated blocker]
- By [time period], you'd have [specific outcome]
- The mechanism we use is [named framework] — here's how it works for someone in your position: [specific application]"
```

The pitch is specific. Generic "we'll help you grow" underperforms specific "we'd start by collapsing your three pricing tiers into one and raising price 2.4x — that's the move for your stage."

State the price clearly, once.

```
"The investment for [program] is $[X]. That includes [specific deliverables]."
```

Then silence. The applicant responds.

### Block 5 — Objection + close (40:00–50:00)

Handle 1–3 objections maximum. More than 3 is a signal of poor fit; offer them a clean exit rather than push.

#### Common objections

**"I need to think about it"**
This is a soft no. Calibrate:

```
"OK — what specifically do you want to think about?
[applicant explains]
Let's address that now: [response to specific concern].
After we talk through that, are you ready to make a decision today, or are you still thinking?"
```

If they're still "thinking" after addressing their concern: it's a soft no. Book a 7-day follow-up; do not chase.

**"I can't afford it right now"**
Calibrate:

```
"What would have to be true financially for this to be a yes?
[applicant explains]
[If they can find a way: payment plan / financing / timing]
[If they genuinely cannot: 'OK — when would be the right time? Let's reconnect then.']"
```

**"I need to talk to my partner / co-founder"**
Calibrate:

```
"Of course. When can you have that conversation?
[set timeline]
Let's book a follow-up call after that conversation. If your partner's a yes, we go forward; if not, no problem."
```

**"How is this different from [competitor]?"**
Calibrate:

```
"Good question. The structural difference is [specific structural difference]. The [competitor] approach focuses on [their angle]; we focus on [our angle]. For your situation specifically, [why our angle fits better]."
```

The differentiation is clear and specific. Generic "we have more support" underperforms.

#### Closing methodology

Per closing_methodology:

**Crossroads close**:
```
"OK — there are two paths from here:
Path 1: You decide this isn't for you, you go back to [current approach], and 12 months from now you're at [predictable trajectory].
Path 2: You commit to this today, we start [specific timing], and 12 months from now you're at [transformed trajectory].
Given those two paths, what's the move?"
```

**Assumptive close**:
```
"Great. Here's what happens next:
- Once you join, you'll get access to [first deliverable] within 24 hours.
- Your first call with [coach / mentor] is [scheduled timing].
- The investment is $[X]. I'll send the payment link right now.
[Send link.]"
```

**Question close**:
```
"On a scale of 1–10, where are you on saying yes to this?
[applicant responds]
[Address whatever's keeping them below 10.]
What else needs to be true to get you to a 10?"
```

**Takeaway close**:
```
"Honestly — based on what you've shared, I'm not sure this is the right fit. We work best with [specific profile]. You're [in or near that profile, but with caveat].
If you want to move forward, I'm happy to. But I want to be straight with you about what I'm seeing.
What do you want to do?"
```

The takeaway close works for high-conviction operators. The applicant either commits despite the takeaway (high-quality close) or self-disqualifies (right outcome).

## After the call

### If yes

- Send payment link live during the call OR within 1 hour
- Send onboarding email at T+1h
- Welcome to the program at T+24h
- First check-in at T+7d

### If soft-no ("I need to think")

- T+24h: thank-you email with call recording or notes
- T+72h: case-study email — anonymized client win that addresses their hesitation
- T+7d: follow-up offering a final question call OR final yes/no decision
- T+14d: respectful close-out

### If hard-no

- T+0: thank-you email, route to channel's free content
- T+30d: re-engagement check-in if appropriate

### If disqualified

- T+0: polite explanation; route to lower-ticket entry if available; route to free content otherwise

## Verification checklist (per call)

- [ ] Pre-call review of application completed
- [ ] Call recorded (with applicant consent — required in many jurisdictions)
- [ ] Frame set in Block 1
- [ ] 80% applicant talking in Block 2
- [ ] Gap and cost-of-inaction explicit in Block 3
- [ ] Mechanism connection in Block 4
- [ ] Price stated clearly, once
- [ ] Maximum 3 objections handled in Block 5
- [ ] Clear yes / soft-no / hard-no determined
- [ ] Post-call sequence triggered

## Call cadence and economics

- **Show rate target**: 65–75%
- **Close rate target**: 30–40% on $3K–5K offers; 25–35% on $5K–10K; 15–25% on $10K+
- **Average call length**: 40–50 minutes; below 30 minutes signals weak diagnosis; above 60 minutes signals indecision

## Verification logs

Per call, log:
- Outcome (yes / soft-no / hard-no / disqualified)
- Objections raised (for compartment 6 and pinned VSL revision)
- Pain language captured (verbatim, for compartment 2)
- Notes for the post-call sequence

The call notes feed cycle-2:
- Recurring objections → revise pinned VSL or call script
- Recurring pain language → update voice-of-viewer in compartment 2
- Recurring "I need to think" → diagnose where in the funnel applicants are losing conviction

## Sales-team escalation

For channels with a setter / closer team:

- Setter qualifies inbound applications
- Setter books the call with the closer
- Closer runs the call per this script
- Setter follows up post-call per the post-call sequence
- Quarterly: closer + setter review the recorded calls for calibration
