# Example Scripts — The Calibration Corpus (proven high-performers, annotated)

> The **calibration corpus** the scriptwriting skills style-match against — real high-performing scripts with structure breakdowns, for proven-pattern reference before drafting. The specimens are deliberately from *other* niches (a clothing-brand commerce channel, a TikTok-Shop educator), because the lesson is structural: **steal the skeleton, never the surface — strip hype-stack copy.** Each entry: the format → the hook (verbatim-style, anonymized) → the reusable skeleton → what transfers to **your** niche → the hype-surface to strip.
>
> ⚠️ Every specimen leans on surface moves most authority channels ban: hype power-words, "make $X" promises, fake scarcity, manufactured urgency. The *architecture* is gold; the *copy* is contraband. Run every borrowed line through the channel's `phrases_to_avoid` and `banned_hook_patterns` (`company.yaml`, Compartments 1 + 6) and `workspace/VOICE.md` before it ships.

## A · "I stole a giant's strategy" — case-study/story (a clothing-brand commerce channel)

**Type:** Case-study/story · **Result framing:** a five-figure-per-month outcome attributed to one borrowed mechanism.

**Hook (verbatim-style, anonymized — study the contrast + specificity):**
> *"[Category giant] built a $5-billion empire with one marketing strategy that almost nobody talks about. I stole it, applied it to my [small brand], and now consistently generate over $100,000 every single month. When I first started, I was lucky to make a few hundred bucks on a good day, wondering how the hell [the giant] could sell out entire collections in seconds…"*

**The reusable skeleton (the "stole a giant's strategy" format):**
1. **Hook** — giant's result + "one strategy nobody talks about" + *I stole it* → my result + my humble start (relatability).
2. **Part 1 — decode the giant** — the giant's mechanism, told as the giant's story.
3. **Part 2 — the named framework** — the creator's own 4-phase rename of the mechanism. Each phase = re-hook → mechanism → my specific example.
4. **Part 3 — the counter-intuitive deepener** — the part everyone misses, *after* the obvious win.
5. **CTA** — "this scratches the surface" → free training.

**What transfers to YOUR niche:**
- The **"I reverse-engineered a giant"** frame — a fitness coach: *"I stole a pro team's recovery protocol and ran it on a 45-year-old desk worker."* A B2B consultant: *"I stole a Fortune-500 ops playbook and installed it in a 12-person firm."*
- **Naming the framework** — a named, ownable mechanism is what makes a method teachable and quotable. Map this to your channel's `unique_mechanism` (Compartment 1).
- **Phase structure with a specific example per phase** — walk your own framework with a real client/student beat at each step.
- The **post-X counter-intuitive section** ("the part everyone misses") — the deepener that separates the channel from surface-level coverage of the same topic.

**Strip:** the scarcity manufacturing ("only making 50"), "unstoppable demand," any drop-hype. Authority channels sell the durable mechanism, not artificial scarcity.

## B · "The mentor's N principles" — borrowed-authority teach (a clothing-brand commerce channel)

**Hook (verbatim-style, anonymized — mentor + barrier-removal promise):**
> *"…he's a source of inspiration for anyone who's ever dreamed of [the outcome]. I studied every piece of advice he ever put on the internet. You don't need a [credential], and you don't need to be born gifted — you just need his 3 principles…"*

**Skeleton (the "mentor's N principles" format):**
1. Hook — mentor authority + "you don't need [common barrier], you just need [N] principles."
2. **Part 1 — the problem with the obvious approach** — reframe *before* teaching.
3. Principles 1→N, each: the mentor's quote → the principle (named) → *my* application with specifics.
4. Outro — genuine enthusiasm + free-resource CTA.

**What transfers to YOUR niche:**
- **"You don't need [common barrier], you just need [your named mechanism]"** — the barrier-removal wedge. Pick the credential or resource your audience wrongly believes is the prerequisite, and remove it in the first line.
- **Reframe-before-teach** — name the wrong game the audience is playing before presenting the right one. Sets up every principle as an answer, not a list item.
- **Named principles with the source's quote + your application** — works with any mentor, book, or your own earlier-failure arc as the quoted authority.

**Strip:** little — this is the cleanest specimen in the corpus. Keep the humility and the specificity.

## C · "It's actually simple" — anti-complexity comprehensive guide / soft VSL (a clothing-brand commerce channel)

**Hook (verbatim-style, anonymized — the anti-complexity open):**
> *"Everyone's out here making [the pursuit] seem complicated when it's actually really simple. 'You need $10K to start.' 'You need to go to [school].' 'You need to know how to [hard skill].' It's all noise. I started [my business] without any of that…"*

**Skeleton (the comprehensive "free course" / soft-VSL):** anti-complexity hook → "this isn't a basic tutorial, it's the complete blueprint" → 6–8 numbered chapters covering the whole arc, each with mistakes-to-avoid + specifics → **"simple, not easy"** honest outro → community CTA.

**What transfers to YOUR niche:**
- The **anti-complexity hook** fits any over-mystified niche — list the three false barriers your audience repeats, then knock them down with your own start.
- The **chaptered comprehensive guide** — "how we deliver [your core engagement], start to finish" — is the single strongest authority-mode format for solution-aware viewers: it demonstrates the whole mechanism instead of teasing it.
- The **"simple, not easy"** turn — the honest-cost close that separates an authority channel from the hype channels covering the same promise.

**Strip:** the "easy"/"brain-dead" register and any manufactured effortlessness; keep the structure and the honest turn. If the niche genuinely is hard, say so — that admission *is* the trust move.

## D · "Don't do the trendy thing, do this instead" — by-the-numbers comparison (a TikTok-Shop educator)

**Type:** Contrarian comparison. **Hook (verbatim-style, anonymized):**
> *"What if I told you there's a side hustle everyone's going crazy about [this year], but 99% of people are completely missing a way better opportunity right in front of them?"*

**Skeleton (the by-the-numbers comparison — the most transferable specimen in the corpus):**
1. Hook — the trendy thing everyone's chasing + "there's a better way."
2. **Fair framing** ("X is legit, but…") — disarms the skeptic before the pitch.
3. Define both options across **4 explicit metrics** (startup cost / skill required / time-to-result / earnings ceiling).
4. **Run the actual numbers** side by side — logic ladders + real math = belief.
5. The 3-step system for the better option.
6. CTA — community / free resource.

**What transfers to YOUR niche:**
- The **by-the-numbers comparison** maps to any "old way vs your way" beat — *"$1,500 one-off project vs $15K system engagement — same work, different positioning"*, *"renting the audience vs owning the list"*, *"cutting calories vs building the protocol."* Use the exact 4-metric structure.
- **"X is legit, but here's the better ROI on your time"** — fair framing disarms a skeptical, sophisticated buyer better than attack framing. This is the anti-guru move rendered as structure.
- **Show the math, let it sell** — when the numbers run on screen, the script never has to persuade; the comparison does it.

**Strip:** the "literal teenagers making $X" social proof, "life-changing money," and the click-pressure CTA. Keep the metric-by-metric rigor.

## E · "Receipt title + exact blueprint" and "the window is open" (a TikTok-Shop educator)

**Two skeletons worth stealing:**

- **Receipt-title + exact-blueprint** — *"I've made $[unround number], here's my exact blueprint"*: hook with the *unround* number (a figure like 302,786 reads as a receipt; "300K" reads as a claim) → social proof (students/results) → setup → research method → 3-part content formula (hook / breakdown / CTA) demonstrated on *real examples* → outro. → Your version: **the unround number is the receipt** — $28,400 beats "$30K" every time. "Here's the exact system, on a real example" is authority mode's whole job.
- **The "gold rush / be early" opportunity frame** — historical analogy (a past gold-rush moment) → "the window is now" → why early movers win. → Your version: the **"why now"** section of any offer-adjacent video — name the structural shift that opened the window in your niche. *Use the urgency frame; never attach a fake deadline.*

**Strip:** "easy money," salary-replacement bait, and the manufactured "60 days before it's saturated" countdown. A real window needs no fake clock.

## The universal skeleton these all share

| Beat | What the corpus does | Your authority-mode version |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Giant's result / contrarian frame / unround number + humble start | Real receipt + "I was where you are [N] months ago" |
| **Disarm** | "X is legit, but…" | Honest-cost framing — concede what's true before the reframe |
| **Reframe** | "the obvious approach is the wrong game" | Name the wrong game your niche is playing |
| **Named mechanism** | A named blueprint / N principles / 4 metrics | YOUR named mechanism (Compartment 1 — `unique_mechanism`) |
| **Proof per beat** | A specific drop / a real video | A real client number, shown on screen |
| **Honest turn** | "simple, not easy" | The honest-cost line in the creator's own words |
| **CTA** | Free training → community | Lead magnet (audience) + application/call (buyers) — per the Compartment 3 bridge |

**The one move that beats the whole corpus:** these scripts *claim* results; an authority channel *shows the receipt*. Proof is the moat — lean on it everywhere the corpus leans on hype.
