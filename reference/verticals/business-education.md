# Business Education — Vertical Playbook

> Vertical-specific playbook for business-education YouTube channels (entrepreneurship, agency-acquisition, B2B sales, info-product mentorship, consulting). Read alongside `reference/benchmarks/business-education.md` for performance bands.

## Vertical positioning advice

Business education is a high-density, high-CPM, high-skepticism niche. Positioning gates:

1. **Specific operator-stage credibility.** $X/mo MRR personally operated, $X coaching outcomes documented, specific industry expertise. Generic "I'm a business expert" is rejected by the audience.

2. **Named mechanism / framework.** The specific system the operator uses — the 8-belief framework, the 6-blockage diagnostic, the [Audience]-[Pain]-[Method]-[Result] structure. Mechanism is the IP.

3. **Stage-specific positioning.** $0–$25K MRR audience, $25K–$100K, $100K–$500K, $500K+ — each stage has different problems and different optimal content. Channels that try to serve all stages serve none well.

4. **A defensible contrarian frame OR a defensible expertise position.** Either the channel positions against the niche's consensus (contrarian) or it stakes out an expertise position the niche acknowledges (analytical-explainer / mechanism-first).

5. **Honest acknowledgment of audience program-fatigue.** The audience has typically bought 1–3 info products in the niche before finding the channel. Most have been burned. The operator who acknowledges this and structurally differentiates earns trust.

## Typical offer structures

- **High-ticket coaching / mastermind** — $5K–$30K typical; pinned-VSL → application → call → close architecture
- **Done-for-you services** — agency-style; $5K–$50K depending on scope
- **Group programs / cohorts** — $2K–$10K; multi-week structured cohort
- **Self-paced courses** — $497–$3K; lead-magnet → email → tripwire → core architecture
- **One-on-one consulting** — $300–$1,000+ per hour; selective; not always at scale
- **SaaS / software products** — $97–$497/mo recurring; often complementary to coaching

OS bullseye: creators with high-ticket coaching / mastermind in the $5K–$30K band, agency-acquisition operators with offers in the $5K–$30K band.

## Typical audience awareness distribution

- Unaware: 5–10%
- Problem-aware: 20–30%
- Solution-aware: 30–40%
- Product-aware: 20–30%
- Most-aware: 10–15%

Business audiences skew more product-aware and most-aware than personal-finance because they have actively bought info products before. The channel's job is to differentiate against alternatives the audience has already evaluated.

## Channel architecture archetypes that work

### The agency-acquisition architect (stage-tiered)

Foundational playlist with stage-tier introduction. Pillar playlists per stage ($0–$25K, $25K–$100K, $100K–$500K). Audience self-selects into their stage and binges. Cases anonymized as agency-type + scale + niche.

### The long-form-VSL specialist architect

Foundational playlist anchored by:
1. Channel-thesis video
2. Mechanism introduction
3. Case-study #1
4. Authority deep-dive on the mechanism
5. Case-study #2

Pinned VSL is heavily resourced; conversion path is pinned-VSL-driven.

### The contrarian-narrator architect (in business)

Foundational playlist starts with the contrarian thesis. Pillar playlists deepen the replacement frame. The channel's reach is contrarian-frame-driven; the conversion is mechanism-application-driven.

### The case-study-archivist architect

Documented client engagements as the channel's primary asset. Library compounds via segment-organized playlists. Conversion via pattern-matching — the audience finds a case that matches their situation.

## Content pillars typical for this niche

Recommended 4–5 pillars:

1. **Foundations / fundamentals** (15–20%) — for $0–$25K stage; entry-level business mechanics
2. **Operator scaling** (25–30%) — for $25K–$500K stage; the bullseye conversion zone
3. **Sales / conversion** (15–20%) — universal; high-CPM-strong
4. **Team / leverage** (15–20%) — for scaling-stage operators
5. **Strategy / mindset** (10–15%) — operator-mindset; identity-aspirational

## Voice of viewer in this niche

Recurring pain language to capture:

- "I'm stuck at $X/mo and I can't figure out why"
- "I feel like I'm doing everything but nothing's working"
- "I'm tired of [doing the work] — I want to [scale / get out / sell]"
- "Every program I've bought has been recycled"
- "I want a system, not more tactics"
- "I can't find the right [team / hire / closer]"
- "My offer's good but I can't get traffic / can't close / can't deliver at scale"

## Conversion path specific to business education

Pinned VSL conversion:

- Length: 40–60 minutes typical for $5K+ offers
- Hook: stage-aware ("if you're stuck at $X/mo, this is for you") — tier-specific filter
- Hero story: operator's own scaling arc + mentor-of-other-operators arc; calibrated to audience sophistication
- Price anchoring: investment-vs-return framing; cost-of-inaction tied to specific revenue plateau
- Crossroads close: 12-month-trajectory-comparison; specific per audience stage
- Identity-aspirational close: "the operator who [transformation outcome]"

## Common failure modes specific to this niche

### Stage-mismatch

**Failure**: video calibrated for $100K/mo stage when audience is at $20K/mo. Audience bounces; conversion fails.
**Prevention**: stage-tier discipline per video. Stage-tagged content.

### Recycled-program detection

**Failure**: applying generic info-product tactics audience has already seen. Audience disqualifies.
**Prevention**: INV-5, INV-6, INV-16 enforced. Differentiation must be structural, not surface.

### Earnings-claim violation

**Failure**: "make $10K/mo guaranteed" framing. FTC scrutiny; audience trust collapse.
**Prevention**: substantiated claims only; "our average client" with documented data; never "guaranteed."

### Generic case studies

**Failure**: case studies without segment + scale + mechanism specificity. Pattern-matching fails.
**Prevention**: anonymized-precision rule — segment + scale + niche specifically; mechanism extracted explicitly.

### Operator-flex production

**Failure**: lifestyle-flex production grammar that signals "guru" archetype. Audience cynicism activates.
**Prevention**: production grammar discipline. Restraint over flex. The operator's authority comes from outcomes, not lifestyle.

## Regulatory considerations

Lower regulatory burden than personal finance, but:

- **FTC**: #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate content; income-claim substantiation
- **Earnings claims**: substantiated by documented client data
- **Affiliate disclosure**: INV-15
- **Testimonials**: anonymized properly; verifiable; not fabricated (INV-5, INV-6)
- **Cohort / mastermind purchase-tier disclosures**: clear and conspicuous

## Library compound entry priorities

- Hook patterns by stage (different hooks work for $0–$25K vs $100K+)
- Mechanism-application case studies
- Differentiation-vs-alternative content
- Stage-bridge content (helping audience progress through stages)
- Anonymized case-study library
