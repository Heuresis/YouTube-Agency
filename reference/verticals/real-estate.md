# Real Estate — Vertical Playbook

> Vertical-specific playbook for real-estate YouTube channels (residential investing, commercial, multi-family, BRRRR / flipping, market analysis, real-estate education). Read alongside `reference/benchmarks/real-estate.md`.

## Vertical positioning advice

Real estate is a geography-sensitive, capital-intensive, credibility-gated niche. Positioning gates:

1. **Operator-stage credibility.** Specific portfolio size, deal count, market expertise. "Real-estate investor" is generic; "20-unit multi-family operator in [region]" is specific.

2. **Sub-niche specificity.** Residential vs commercial. Multi-family vs single-family. Buy-and-hold vs flipping vs syndication. Geography-specific. The narrower the sub-niche, the broader the channel can reach within it.

3. **Geography-aware content strategy.** Real estate is geography-specific. US-residential investing has different mechanics than UK-buy-to-let. The channel commits to a primary geography or explicitly addresses multi-geography (rare).

4. **A clear investing philosophy.** Buy-and-hold vs flipping, leverage-heavy vs cash-buyer, off-market vs on-market sourcing. The philosophy is the structural anchor.

5. **Market-cycle awareness.** Real estate is cyclical. The channel's content must acknowledge current cycle stage (rising / peak / declining / trough) and calibrate advice accordingly.

## Typical offer structures

- **Coaching / mastermind** — $5K–$30K typical; multi-month structured programs
- **Done-for-you services** — turnkey investment services; $5K–$50K+ depending on scope
- **Self-paced courses** — $497–$3,000; foundational frameworks
- **Membership / community** — $50–$500/mo; ongoing market access + community
- **Syndication / fund participation** — investor-only; SEC-regulated; not the channel's primary offer
- **Live events / mastermind retreats** — $3K–$15K per event

OS bullseye: creators with coaching / mastermind in the $5K–$30K band, application-funnel-driven conversion.

## Typical audience awareness distribution

- Unaware: 5–10%
- Problem-aware: 20–30% (people considering real-estate-investing for the first time)
- Solution-aware: 30–35%
- Product-aware: 25–30%
- Most-aware: 10–15%

The audience is typically capital-rich; the conversion gate is more about confidence and education than affordability.

## Channel architecture archetypes that work

### The case-study-archivist architect (real-estate signature)

Documented deals are the primary asset. Foundational playlist anchored by featured case studies (anonymized). Pillar playlists organized by deal type (single-family, multi-family, commercial, BRRRR, flipping). Library compounds as a deal-walkthrough catalog.

### The market-analyst architect

Channel positions on market analysis and timing. Foundational playlist establishes the analytical framework. Pillar playlists organized by market segment / region / asset type. Analytical-explainer archetype applied to real estate.

### The agency-acquisition operator architect (in real estate)

Channel teaches scaling a real-estate-investing portfolio or a real-estate-services business (property management, agency). Stage-tiered library: $0 → first deal → 5 deals → portfolio scaling.

## Content pillars typical for this niche

Recommended 4–5 pillars:

1. **Deal walkthroughs / case studies** (25–30%) — anonymized deal stories
2. **Market / timing analysis** (15–20%) — regional / asset-type market analysis
3. **Strategy / philosophy** (20–25%) — buy-and-hold, flipping, BRRRR, syndication, etc.
4. **Operator-scaling content** (15–20%) — for portfolio-builders
5. **Mistakes / lessons / postmortems** (10–15%) — failure-stories build trust

## Common failure modes specific to this niche

### Geography-mismatch

**Failure**: content for one geography presented as universal. Audience in different markets gets misled.
**Prevention**: explicit geography stating. "This applies to US-residential; international viewers should consult local market dynamics."

### Cycle-stage neglect

**Failure**: content that worked in one market cycle promoted as evergreen. Audience misled.
**Prevention**: cycle-aware framing. "This was effective in 2018–2021 conditions; current market dynamics differ in [X ways]."

### Specific-investment-recommendations

**Failure**: recommending specific properties or syndications without proper licensing. Regulatory exposure.
**Prevention**: structural-pattern teaching, not specific recommendations. "Look for properties with X characteristics" not "buy this specific property."

### Past-performance-as-future-promise

**Failure**: showing portfolio appreciation and implying it will continue. Misleading + regulatory.
**Prevention**: "past performance is not indicative of future results" framing. Acknowledge market dependence.

### Capital-class assumptions

**Failure**: content assuming the audience has $500K+ to invest, while the audience is actually $50K-tier. Mismatch.
**Prevention**: capital-tier specificity. Different content for different capital classes.

## Regulatory considerations

Real estate is regulated:

- **License-specific**: real-estate-broker / agent licensing varies by jurisdiction
- **SEC**: investment-advice in real-estate-securities (REITs, syndications) is SEC-regulated
- **Earnings / appreciation claims**: substantiated only; no guaranteed-returns
- **Securities promotion**: SEC scrutiny on syndication / fund-promotion content
- **Affiliate disclosure**: INV-15 + real-estate-specific FTC scrutiny
- **Geography-specific**: each country / state / province has its own rules

## Channel-stage progression

Real-estate channels typically progress through:

- **Months 0–6**: niche-finding; sub-niche calibration; library 5–25 videos
- **Months 6–18**: case-study-library building; first coaching cohort; library 25–75 videos
- **Months 18–36**: portfolio-evidence compounds; coaching scales; library 75–150 videos
- **Years 3+**: niche-authority status; library at 150+ videos; coaching + back-end at scale

The case-study library is the dominant lever. Real-estate channels with strong, segment-organized deal libraries earn long-tail demand and high-trust conversion.
