# Personal Finance — Vertical Playbook

> Vertical-specific playbook for personal-finance YouTube channels. Application of frameworks + benchmarks + operator archetypes calibrated to this vertical specifically. Read alongside `reference/benchmarks/personal-finance.md` for performance bands and `reference/operators/personal-finance-mentor-archetype.md` for the dominant operator archetype.

## Vertical positioning advice

Personal finance is a credibility-gated niche. The channel's positioning lives or dies on:

1. **Credentials or lived experience that meet the niche's credibility floor.** CFP / CFA / CPA designations, multi-decade investing track record, documented business / financial-life-stage achievement. Without one of these, the credibility floor is not met and the audience disqualifies the operator within 30–60 seconds of any video.

2. **A specific named mechanism the channel owns.** The 3-bucket system, the cash-flow triangle, the wealth-stairway model — all examples of structural mechanisms calibrated to finance. Without a mechanism, the channel competes on topic ("how to save money") with thousands of other channels and loses.

3. **A specific life-stage / income-tier / situation the channel is calibrated for.** "Personal finance for self-employed creatives in their 30s" is a viable position. "Personal finance" is not. The narrower the position, the broader the channel can reach within it (counter-intuitive but consistent across the vertical).

4. **A clear contrarian frame against the niche's canonical advice.** Most personal-finance audiences have heard the canonical advice (skip the latte, invest in index funds, max your 401(k)) ad nauseam. The channel that earns attention has a structural alternative or a structural critique, not a re-statement of consensus.

## Typical offer structures

Back-end offers in personal finance:

- **One-on-one financial planning / advisory** — typically credentialed-professional-only; $2K–$15K depending on scope
- **Group coaching / mastermind** — non-credentialed-eligible; $3K–$15K typical price point
- **Self-paced courses** — $97–$2,000; lower-friction; higher-volume
- **Membership communities** — $20–$100/mo; recurring; relationship-deepening
- **Done-with-you wealth-building services** — for higher-net-worth audiences; $10K–$50K
- **Retreats / events** — premium tier; $5K–$25K per event

The OS's bullseye is creators with an offer in the $3K–$15K coaching/mastermind band, where the pinned VSL conversion path applies cleanly. Lower-ticket offers (courses) work but have different commercial structure (lead-magnet bridge → email sequence → tripwire → core offer rather than pinned VSL → application → call).

## Typical audience awareness distribution

For a mature personal-finance channel:

- Unaware: 5–10%
- Problem-aware: 25–35%
- Solution-aware: 30–40%
- Product-aware: 15–25%
- Most-aware: 5–10%

The distribution skews toward problem-aware and solution-aware because the audience is actively seeking solutions but evaluating across the niche's many channels.

The pinned VSL targets product-aware → most-aware. Authority videos target solution-aware. Reach videos target problem-aware. The channel architecture bridges across the levels.

## Channel architecture archetypes that work

### The credibility-gated architect

Foundational playlist anchored by:
1. Channel-thesis video — the operator's worldview and credentials
2. Mechanism introduction — the named system (3-bucket, cash-flow-triangle, etc.)
3. Case study — anonymized client transformation
4. Awareness-bridge video — connects problem-aware to solution-aware
5. Authority deep-dive — the mechanism applied at depth

Pillar playlists:
- Foundations (debt elimination, emergency fund, baseline)
- Investing (calibrated to the channel's investing philosophy)
- Tax / advanced strategy
- Real-estate (if relevant)
- Mindset / financial-life-stage content (intersects with mindset / philosophy vertical)

### The case-study-archive architect

For operators with active client / case flow:

Foundational playlist anchored by:
1. Channel-thesis
2. Featured case study #1
3. Featured case study #2
4. Mechanism introduction
5. Case study #3 with explicit framework extraction

Pillar playlists organized by client segment (e.g., "first-time-investors," "$100K-saver-tier," "self-employed").

### The contrarian-narrator architect

For operators with strong contrarian frame against finance-canon:

Foundational playlist anchored by:
1. Contrarian-thesis video — "why most personal-finance advice is wrong"
2. Replacement-frame introduction
3. Mechanism that follows from the replacement frame
4. Case study showing the mechanism in action
5. Authority deep-dive

The contrarian frame is the channel's reach engine; pillar playlists then deepen the replacement frame's application.

## Content pillars typical for this niche

Recommended 4–5 pillars:

1. **Foundations** (25–30%) — debt elimination, emergency fund, basic budgeting. Beginner-tier audience entry.
2. **Investing** (25–30%) — calibrated to the channel's investing philosophy (passive index investing, active investing, real-estate, alternative).
3. **Tax & advanced strategy** (15–20%) — for product-aware → most-aware audience; conversion-strong.
4. **Mindset / life-stage** (15–20%) — intersects with mindset vertical; identity-aspirational; trust-deepening.
5. **Market / news commentary** (10–15%) — timely; reach-strong; lower long-tail.

Adjust per the operator's specific positioning. Channels heavily weighted toward investing will reduce foundations; channels for first-time-investors will increase foundations.

## Regulatory considerations

This is the highest-regulation vertical in the OS catalog. Compliance is structural:

### US (SEC + FTC)

- **Investment advice**: registered investment advisers (RIAs) can give specific advice; non-registered cannot. The line is well-documented in SEC guidance.
- **Securities promotion**: SEC scrutiny on syndication / private-placement promotion content
- **FTC #ad disclosure**: on all sponsored / affiliate content, including affiliate-finance-products (brokerages, robo-advisors, etc.)
- **Income claims**: substantiated only; no guaranteed returns

### UK (FCA)

- **Finfluencer rules** (effective 2024): FCA-authorised vs unauthorised promotional content; specific guidance for influencers promoting financial products
- **ASA**: clear ad labeling on sponsored content

### EU (country-specific)

- **MiFID II implications**: investment-advice and securities-promotion under MiFID-aligned rules per country
- Country-specific finfluencer regulations (Germany, France, Italy, Spain all have evolving rules in 2024–2026)

### Australia (ASIC)

- ASIC finfluencer guidance on financial advice
- Affiliate disclosure standards

### Canada (provincial securities regulators)

- Securities promotion rules per province

The compliance discipline is documented in `spec/sponsor-disclosure.md` and is enforced at publish (INV-15). Beyond INV-15, this vertical has additional scrutiny on:

- Specific stock / asset / investment recommendations
- Past-performance claims
- Earnings promises
- Personal client data exposure

## Voice of viewer in this niche

Recurring pain language patterns to capture in compartment 2:

- "I don't know where to start"
- "I make decent money but [I have nothing to show / I'm always broke]"
- "I'm worried I'll never [retire / catch up / get ahead]"
- "I tried [generic advice] but it didn't work for me"
- "I don't trust [Wall Street / banks / brokers]"
- "I'm afraid of making the wrong decision"
- "I feel behind compared to [peer group]"

These pain-language phrases are voice-of-viewer for hooks, problem-section copy, and cost-of-inaction framing.

## Conversion path specific to personal finance

For pinned VSL conversion in this niche:

- Length: 35–55 minutes typical (shorter than general business; the audience commits to long-form once credibility is established)
- Hook: loss-aversion-led ("here's what you're losing right now") more than gain-promise
- Three-Brain Decision Sequence: explicit and slow — Reptilian (fear acknowledged), Limbic (specific cost named), Neocortex (structural model introduced)
- Hero story: calibrated to audience sophistication; for credentialed-CFP audience, 2–4 minutes credentialed-evidence; for non-credentialed-coaching audience, 5–8 minutes lived-experience story
- Price anchoring: aggressive comparison-to-cost-of-inaction (the silent compounding cost of not solving the financial problem)
- Identity-aspirational close: "the kind of person who doesn't worry about money decisions"

## Common failure modes specific to this niche

### Credibility floor not met

**Failure**: operator without credentials, lived experience, or track record. The audience disqualifies within 60 seconds.
**Prevention**: Run `/build-niche` and `/extract-creator-voice` to verify credibility floor. If not met, the operator should not be running a personal-finance channel as primary.

### Generic positioning

**Failure**: "personal finance channel" without sub-niche or audience-tier specificity. The channel competes with thousands of others and loses.
**Prevention**: Run `/build-niche` to lock the sub-niche, the life-stage, the income-tier. Specificity is the moat.

### Compliance violation

**Failure**: specific investment advice, unsubstantiated returns claims, missed affiliate disclosure. Regulator action; platform action.
**Prevention**: INV-15 enforced; jurisdiction-specific rules per `spec/sponsor-disclosure.md`. Compliance review at publish.

### Recycled-program tactics

**Failure**: applying generic info-product-niche tactics (manufactured urgency, value-stack theater, fake testimonials) to personal finance. The audience detects and disqualifies.
**Prevention**: INV-5, INV-6, INV-16 enforced. The personal-finance audience is more discerning than generic info-product audiences.

### Lifestyle-flexing production

**Failure**: production grammar that signals lifestyle-flex (cash close-ups, expensive watches, exotic-location backgrounds). This activates the audience's unregulated-finfluencer detection and erodes credibility.
**Prevention**: production grammar restraint. Visual restraint reinforces credibility positioning.
