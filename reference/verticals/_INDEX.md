## Vertical-specific calibration

Each vertical has:

- **Different positioning gates** — what credentials / lived experience / specific positioning the operator must meet to enter the niche credibly
- **Different typical offer structures** — the bands of price tier, format, and conversion architecture that work in the niche
- **Different audience awareness distribution** — the typical mix of unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / product-aware / most-aware viewers
- **Different channel architecture archetypes** — the structures that route the audience effectively
- **Different content pillars** — the topical splits that align with the niche
- **Different voice-of-viewer pain language** — the verbatim phrases that reflect the niche's audience
- **Different conversion path** — the funnel architecture (pinned VSL vs lead-magnet bridge vs hybrid)
- **Different failure modes** — niche-specific ways the channel fails
- **Different regulatory considerations** — the compliance discipline per niche

## How to use these playbooks

1. **At channel build (Phase 1 in `channel-launch.md`)**: identify the channel's vertical; read the relevant playbook fully; integrate the playbook's positioning advice into `/build-niche` and `/extract-creator-voice`.

2. **At channel-architecture build**: the playbook's "Channel architecture archetypes that work" section informs `/build-channel-architecture`. The "Content pillars typical for this niche" section informs the pillar split.

3. **At offer-design**: the playbook's "Typical offer structures" section informs `/design-offer`. The price tier and format choices follow the niche's conventions.

4. **At series build**: the playbook's awareness-distribution and pain-language sections inform `/series-architect`.

5. **At leak audit**: the playbook's "Common failure modes" section informs `/leak-audit`.

6. **At regulatory review**: the playbook's "Regulatory considerations" section is the niche-specific compliance reference.

## Cross-vertical dependencies

The verticals are not isolated. Several have heavy intersection:

- **Business education ↔ creator-economy** — creator-business operations
- **Personal finance ↔ business education** — founder-finance, tax strategy
- **Mindset / philosophy ↔ all verticals** — the trust-deepening layer for adjacent niches
- **Tech-explainer ↔ productivity / career** — technical productivity content
- **Real estate ↔ personal finance** — real-estate as wealth-building
- **Fitness / health ↔ mindset / philosophy** — body-mind, identity-transformation

Operators in one vertical often borrow frameworks from adjacent verticals while maintaining primary positioning.

## Verticals not covered

The OS is calibrated for coach / creator / info-product / thought-leader bullseye verticals. Some verticals fall outside scope (per INV-8):

- **Entertainment / lifestyle vlog** — different decision logic
- **Gaming** — different decision logic
- **Kids content** — COPPA compliance model
- **Faceless niche channels** — different decision logic
- **Pure ad-revenue channels** — different commercial structure

If a creator's primary niche is in this out-of-scope list, the YouTube Content OS template is wrong fit. Other templates apply.

## Calibration discipline

Verticals evolve. Niche conventions shift over years. Platform mechanics change. These vertical playbooks should be reviewed annually minimum and updated as:

- Regulatory environment changes
- Platform mechanics shift
- Sub-niches mature or fragment
- New operator archetypes emerge in the niche
- Audience-composition characteristics shift

The OS's library-compound mechanism feeds vertical-specific learnings back into these playbooks. As channels in each vertical operate over multiple cycles, the playbooks mature.
