# Creator Economy — Vertical Playbook

> Vertical-specific playbook for creator-economy YouTube channels (content about content, channels about channels, marketing for creators, creator-business operations). Read alongside `reference/benchmarks/creator-economy.md`.

## Vertical positioning advice

Creator economy is a self-referential, transparency-demanding, sophistication-aware niche. Positioning gates:

1. **Lived creator-economy experience.** Own channel growth, own creator-business build, own agency or course experience. Audiences detect inauthenticity immediately because they're creators evaluating other creators.

2. **A specific platform or sub-domain expertise.** "Creator economy" is too broad. "YouTube long-form growth," "Instagram content systems," "creator-business monetization," "platform-specific algorithm explainer." Specificity wins.

3. **A clear positioning vs the niche's saturation.** The niche is competitively dense. The channel must own a specific frame, mechanism, archetype, or contrarian position.

4. **Transparency about own metrics where appropriate.** Income transparency, channel-data transparency, revenue-source transparency. The audience expects and rewards it.

5. **Acknowledged-iteration discipline.** The platform changes; tactics change; the channel's earlier advice ages. Operators who acknowledge evolution earn trust; operators who don't lose it.

## Typical offer structures

- **Course / cohort** — $497–$5,000; multi-week structured creator-business program
- **Coaching / mentorship** — $3K–$30K; one-on-one or small-group
- **Mastermind** — $5K–$25K; multi-month creator-mastermind
- **Membership / community** — $50–$500/mo; ongoing access
- **Agency for creators** — services arm; $5K–$50K project-based
- **Software / SaaS** (analytics tools, thumbnail tools, etc.) — $20–$200/mo recurring
- **Templates / packs** — $30–$500; ancillary

OS bullseye: creators with course / coaching / mastermind in the $1K–$25K band.

## Typical audience awareness distribution

- Unaware: 5–10%
- Problem-aware: 25–35%
- Solution-aware: 30–35%
- Product-aware: 20–25%
- Most-aware: 10–15%

Creator-economy audiences are often product-aware (have evaluated multiple courses / coaches in the niche).

## Channel architecture archetypes that work

### The creator-economy meta-creator architect

The dominant archetype. Foundational playlist establishes the operator's creator-economy worldview. Pillar playlists organized by topic (growth, monetization, platform-mechanic, business-operations).

### The contrast-format viral specialist architect (in creator-economy)

Channel runs reach-mode contrast formats applied to creator-economy topics. "Cheap creator setup vs expensive," "Algorithm of 2025 vs 2026," "Beginner channel vs scaling channel."

### The case-study-archivist architect (in creator-economy)

Anonymized creator case studies. Library organized by archetype + scale (e.g., "long-form-VSL specialist at 50K subs," "contrast-format viral specialist at 200K subs"). Anonymization rules apply — never name real creators.

## Content pillars typical for this niche

Recommended 4–6 pillars:

1. **Platform-mechanic teardowns** (15–20%) — algorithm, monetization, retention mechanics
2. **Growth strategy** (20–25%) — channel-growth, audience-building
3. **Monetization** (15–20%) — sponsor, course, coaching, SaaS, affiliate
4. **Creator-business operations** (15–20%) — team, systems, hiring
5. **Anonymized creator case studies** (15–20%) — pattern-extraction
6. **Industry / market analysis** (10–15%) — platform / industry trends

## Conversion path specific to creator-economy

Both pinned-VSL and lead-magnet-bridge architectures are common. Pinned-VSL works for high-ticket coaching ($5K+). Lead-magnet-bridge works for course offerings ($500–$3K).

For pinned VSL:
- Length: 30–50 minutes
- Hook: contrarian-creator-frame or specific-creator-mistake-call-out
- Hero story: operator's own creator-economy arc + coached-other-creators arc
- Mechanism: the operator's specific growth / monetization / systems framework
- Identity-aspirational close: "the kind of creator who [transformation]"

For lead-magnet-bridge:
- Magnet: a checklist, template, or framework specific to a video's topic
- Welcome sequence: 5–10 emails over 14–21 days
- Tripwire ($97–$497): foundational course
- Core ($1K–$5K): comprehensive program

## Common failure modes specific to this niche

### Recycled-creator-advice detection

**Failure**: content the audience has heard from other channels. Detected immediately.
**Prevention**: differentiation discipline. Operator-specific frameworks; specific anonymized cases; specific mechanism.

### Inauthentic income-transparency

**Failure**: fabricated income screenshots or inflated revenue claims. Audience cross-checks; trust collapses.
**Prevention**: INV-5, INV-6 enforced. Transparency, where shared, is verifiable.

### Engagement-bait disguised as growth advice

**Failure**: "comment below if..." in a video about audience growth. Hypocritical.
**Prevention**: INV-17 enforced.

### Manufactured "viral moment" framings

**Failure**: claiming a moment was viral when it wasn't, or staging "real moments" as content. Audience detects.
**Prevention**: INV-16 (parasocial-honesty) enforced. Genuine moments only.

### Platform-update neglect

**Failure**: continuing to teach old algorithm patterns after a platform update. Audience misled.
**Prevention**: update discipline. New videos when platform changes; pinned-comment-updates on older content.

## Regulatory considerations

- **FTC**: #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate / creator-tool affiliate content (high-volume in this vertical)
- **Earnings / growth claims**: substantiated only; heightened scrutiny because of niche history
- **Income-transparency move**: when sharing revenue, accuracy is gated by INV-5
- **Affiliate-link compliance**: especially important in this vertical

## Channel-stage progression

Creator-economy channels typically progress through:

- **Months 0–6**: niche-finding; positioning; library 10–30 videos
- **Months 6–18**: audience-building; first course / cohort; library 30–75 videos
- **Months 18–36**: course / coaching at scale; library 75–150 videos
- **Years 3+**: niche-authority; library at 150+ videos; mastermind + back-end at scale

The library compound is moderate — platform-mechanic videos age, but creator-business-operations content tends to compound. Mixed evergreen / timely content.
