# Fitness & Health — Vertical Playbook

> Vertical-specific playbook for fitness / health YouTube channels (training, nutrition, body-composition transformation, athletic performance, wellness). Read alongside `reference/benchmarks/fitness-health.md`.

## Vertical positioning advice

Fitness / health is a high-reach, transformation-driven, regulation-aware niche. Positioning gates:

1. **Visible operator transformation OR credentialed expertise.** Either the operator demonstrates physical results (their own body / athletic performance / client transformations) OR has credentials (certified personal trainer, registered dietitian, exercise scientist, sports medicine professional). Without one, the credibility floor is not met.

2. **A specific sub-niche.** "Fitness" is too broad. "Strength training for women over 40," "endurance for masters runners," "hypertrophy for natural lifters" — specificity wins. The narrower the position, the broader the audience can grow within it.

3. **A clear training / nutrition philosophy.** Generic advice loses to channels with a specific philosophy (e.g., powerlifting-focused strength, evidence-based hypertrophy, intuitive eating, paleo / keto / Mediterranean-aligned). The philosophy is the structural anchor.

4. **Health / regulatory awareness.** This vertical has heightened FTC scrutiny on weight-loss / health claims. The operator who handles regulatory boundaries explicitly earns audience and platform trust.

5. **Visual production tier appropriate to the niche.** Training videos benefit from visual demonstration. Production investment in camera / lighting / form-shooting matters more here than in pure-talking-head niches.

## Typical offer structures

- **Coaching programs** — $300–$5,000 per cohort or per-month; structured nutrition / training programs
- **Custom-coaching** — $200–$1,500/mo; one-on-one tier
- **Self-paced courses / programs** — $97–$497; foundational training plans
- **Membership / community** — $20–$100/mo; ongoing programming + community
- **Supplements / nutrition products** — varies; product-led monetization layered with content
- **Live workshops / certifications** — $500–$3,000; intensive training events

OS bullseye: creators with coaching programs in the $500–$5,000 band, where lead-magnet → email → tripwire → core or pinned-VSL → application → call architectures both work.

## Typical audience awareness distribution

- Unaware: 15–25% (broad-reach niche pulls many unaware viewers)
- Problem-aware: 30–40%
- Solution-aware: 25–30%
- Product-aware: 10–15%
- Most-aware: 5–10%

Fitness audiences skew more unaware / problem-aware than business / finance because the niche pulls broad reach. The channel architecture must bridge unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware before the back-end conversion.

## Channel architecture archetypes that work

### The transformation-narrative architect

Foundational playlist anchored by transformation case studies (operator's own + clients'). Pillar playlists organized by training discipline / nutrition approach. Library compounds as a transformation-content catalog.

### The science-based-explainer architect

Channel positions on evidence-based, citation-heavy content. Foundational playlist introduces the channel's evidence-based approach. Pillar playlists deepen specific topics (training, nutrition, recovery, supplementation). Analytical-explainer archetype applied to fitness.

### The case-study-archivist architect (in fitness)

Documented client transformations as the channel's primary asset. Anonymized client transformations as case studies; library organized by client segment (gender, age-tier, sport-tier).

## Content pillars typical for this niche

Recommended 4–5 pillars:

1. **Foundations / form** (20–25%) — exercise technique, training basics
2. **Programming / strategy** (20–25%) — training programs, periodization
3. **Nutrition** (20–25%) — eating strategy, calibrated to channel philosophy
4. **Transformation / case studies** (15–20%) — visual proof; conversion-strong
5. **Mindset / motivation** (10–15%) — intersects with mindset vertical; identity-aspirational

## Voice of viewer in this niche

Recurring pain language:

- "I'm doing everything but [not seeing results]"
- "I can't [stay consistent / commit / show up]"
- "I keep [getting injured / plateauing / regressing]"
- "I don't know [what to eat / how to train / how much]"
- "I tried [diet / program / workout] but it didn't work"
- "I want to [look / feel / perform] like [aspiration]"
- "I'm intimidated by [gym / lifting / running]"

## Conversion path specific to fitness / health

Pinned VSL conversion:

- Length: 25–45 minutes (shorter than business; the audience is more visual / less verbal)
- Hook: transformation-promise calibrated to audience (specific outcome + time-frame)
- Hero story: operator's own transformation OR coaching-of-others transformation
- Mechanism explanation: structural training / nutrition philosophy explained with visual demonstration
- Price anchoring: investment-vs-cost-of-staying-stuck (health, energy, identity)
- Identity-aspirational close: "the kind of person who [identity attribute]"

## Common failure modes specific to this niche

### Body-shaming / exploitative framing

**Failure**: weight-loss content with body-shaming overtones or before-after deception. Audience attrition + platform-policy concerns.
**Prevention**: ethical content discipline. Honest framing; no shame-driven hooks; consensual / honest before-afters.

### Health-claim violations

**Failure**: medical claims ("cure," "treat") without licensing. FDA / regulator-equivalent scrutiny.
**Prevention**: avoid clinical-medical claims. Refer-to-professional disclaimer where appropriate.

### Supplement-claim overreach

**Failure**: unsubstantiated supplement effects or transformation promises. FTC scrutiny.
**Prevention**: substantiated claims; sponsor disclosure (INV-15); honest evaluation.

### Dishonest before-after

**Failure**: lighting, posing, time-elapse undisclosed manipulation in transformation content. Audience detects; trust collapses.
**Prevention**: honest demonstration. Document time-elapsed, methodology, individual variation.

### Generic positioning

**Failure**: "fitness channel" without sub-niche. Lost in volume.
**Prevention**: sub-niche specificity per Run `/build-niche`.

## Regulatory considerations

Health / fitness is regulated:

- **FDA / equivalent**: no medical claims; supplement claims must be substantiated
- **FTC**: weight-loss claims subject to enhanced scrutiny; #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate content
- **Medical-advice boundaries**: no diagnosis, no treatment claims; refer-to-professional language standard
- **Body-image considerations**: ethical content discipline; no exploitative framing
- **Sponsor disclosure**: INV-15 + supplement / fitness-specific FTC enhanced scrutiny
- **Athletic / training-injury liability**: clear "consult your physician before starting any exercise program" language

## Library compound entry priorities

- Transformation-content patterns (anonymized cases)
- Form / technique tutorials (evergreen)
- Nutrition strategy frameworks
- Voice-of-viewer pain-language by life-stage / sub-niche
- Sponsor-relationship audit (which sponsors aligned with audience; which didn't)

## Cross-vertical dependencies

Fitness / health intersects with:

- **Mindset / philosophy** (motivation, identity transformation, body-mind)
- **Personal finance** (budget-aware fitness, cost-of-fitness considerations)
- **Productivity / career** (energy management, performance for knowledge workers)

## Channel-stage progression

Fitness / health channels typically progress through:

- **Months 0–6**: niche-finding; sub-niche calibration; library 5–25 videos
- **Months 6–18**: audience-building; back-end-offer launch; library 25–75 videos
- **Months 18–36**: case-study library compounds; coaching scales; library 75–150 videos
- **Years 3+**: niche-authority status; library at 150+ videos; coaching at scale

The vertical's monetization is heavily back-end-offer-driven (RPM is structurally low). Channels that build a strong back-end can outperform their platform RPM by 5–10x.
