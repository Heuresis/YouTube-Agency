# The Pillar Content Strategy (Broad-Appeal vs Niched Mix)

## Origin archetype
Senior YouTube script consultant who reverse-engineered the channels that successfully grow audience while converting to high-ticket offers.

## What it solves
A channel that runs only niched-conversion content has a ceiling. A channel that runs only broad-appeal content has weak conversion. The pillar strategy mixes both intentionally.

## Structure — two parallel modes on one channel

**Mode A — Broad-appeal / reach mode.**
- Title formula: tends toward unaware or problem-aware (Five Awareness Levels).
- Pillar allocation: tilts toward Pillar 1 + Pillar 4 (primary expertise + philosophy / lessons).
- Title formula split: tilts toward Myth-Busting (highest CTR) + Trend (reactive content).
- Function: feeds the channel new viewers; expands the addressable audience.

**Mode B — Authority / niched mode.**
- Title formula: tends toward solution-aware or product-aware (Five Awareness Levels).
- Pillar allocation: tilts toward Pillar 1 + Pillar 2 (primary + secondary expertise, niche-specific).
- Title formula split: tilts toward How-To + Case Study (highest conversion-relevant content).
- Function: converts existing viewers into qualified leads, calls, and customers.

## Operating ratio
Approximately 60% authority/niched + 40% broad-appeal/reach is a common starting allocation, adjustable based on:
- Channel maturity (new channels need more reach, mature channels can run more authority).
- Niche saturation (saturated niches need more reach to break through; un-saturated niches can run more authority directly).
- Operator's offer maturity (offers without product-market fit need more authority content to refine; PMF-validated offers need more reach).

## When to use
Continuous content-calendar planning. The dual-mode allocation is a standing default; specific videos move between modes based on opportunity.

## Why it works
The two modes serve different links in the funnel. Reach mode generates impressions and discovery; authority mode converts impressions into qualified pipeline. A channel without reach starves; a channel without authority leaks.

## Variants observed
Some operators advocate single-mode focus (e.g., "only run authority content"); others advocate 50/50. The 60/40 form retained here is the practical operating ratio for most online-coach / info-product creators in current platform conditions.
