# The Brand Pillar Split (50 / 25 / 15 / 10)

## Origin archetype
Personal-brand framework specialist working with online coaches and high-ticket info-product creators.

## What it solves
A different question from the title-formula split. "What is each video *about* (topic-wise)?" — distinct from "what *form* does each video take." The 50/25/15/10 split fixes the topic mix at the brand-positioning layer.

## Structure — four content pillars with allocation

| Pillar | Allocation | Type |
|---|---|---|
| Pillar 1 | 50% | Primary expertise — the operator's flagship topic |
| Pillar 2 | 25% | Secondary expertise — adjacent / supporting topic |
| Pillar 3 | 15% | Industry / tool updates |
| Pillar 4 | 10% | Lessons / philosophy / personal frame |

## Each pillar requires four artifacts when planned
1. What it covers
2. How you want to be positioned within this pillar (positioning is per-pillar, not just per-channel)
3. Target audience for this content (different audiences per pillar are accepted)
4. Three sample hook ideas

## The two-layer split
Brand pillars (50/25/15/10) decide what each video is *about*. Title formulas (40/30/20/10) decide what *form* each video takes. The two are orthogonal — every video has both a pillar assignment and a formula assignment.

## Worked structural example
A 10-video plan for a coaching channel might look like — 5 videos in Pillar 1 (primary expertise: e.g., scaling coaching offers), 2.5 videos in Pillar 2 (adjacent: e.g., team-building), 1.5 in Pillar 3 (tools: e.g., automation), 1 in Pillar 4 (philosophy: e.g., founder-mindset). Across those 10 videos, 4 are how-tos, 3 are case studies, 2 are myth-busts, 1 is trend.

## When to use
Content-calendar planning. The two-layer split is a 2D matrix that forces conscious choice on both axes — without it, calendars naturally drift toward "all primary expertise + all how-to," which is the most common channel-failure mode.

## Why it works
Decoupling topic from form lets the operator serve multiple viewer intents per topic without diluting the brand. A myth-bust video in Pillar 1 reaches a different audience than a how-to in Pillar 1, but both reinforce the same brand positioning.

## Variants observed
Some operators use a 60/30/10 three-pillar split; others use a 50/30/20 split. The 4-pillar / 50/25/15/10 form retained here is the canonical default in the source corpus, with explicit acknowledgment that it does not match the lifestyle-content "60% aspiration / 40% value" split (that lives at a different layer).
