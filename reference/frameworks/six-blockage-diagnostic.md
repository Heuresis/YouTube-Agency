# The Six Blockage Diagnostic

## Origin archetype
Conversion-architecture specialist running 8-figure offers who developed a triage diagnostic before building any new infrastructure.

## What it solves
Most "low-converting funnel" diagnoses misidentify the blockage. The operator builds new content / new ads / new offers when the actual constraint is somewhere else upstream. The diagnostic forces the operator to identify the single bottleneck before doing any work.

## Structure — six diagnostic questions in order

1. **Lead generation & appointments** — do we have traffic / appointments at all? (Volume problem.)
2. **Lead quality** — are the leads the RIGHT people for our offer? (Targeting problem.)
3. **Offer architecture** — is the offer itself irresistible to the right person? (Product / packaging problem.)
4. **Sales / closing** — when we get the right person on a call, can we convert? (Sales-process problem.)
5. **Team / operations** — once they buy, can the team deliver? (Capacity problem.)
6. **Mindset / aura / operator presence** — is the operator showing up as the believable leader of the offer? (Identity problem.)

## Triage rule
Identify THE bottleneck. Fix that first. Everything else is noise. Building new content while the actual blockage is "leads aren't right for offer" wastes weeks.

## When to use
Before any major content sprint, funnel rebuild, or offer launch. Also: any time conversion rate drops or appointment-to-close ratio shifts unfavorably.

## Translation for a YouTube-as-front-door operator
Is the blockage in (1) channel growth, (2) audience-ICP mismatch, (3) offer-content fit, (4) call-booking funnel, (5) delivery capacity, or (6) on-camera operator presence? Each blockage has a fundamentally different treatment.

## Why it works
Theory of constraints applied to a personal-brand pipeline. The throughput of the entire system is bounded by the single weakest link; improving any non-bottleneck link does not improve total system output.

## Variants observed
Some operators use a 5-stage funnel diagnostic (no operator-presence layer); others add a 7th "pricing" stage. The 6-stage form retained here is the minimum-set that explicitly includes the operator-presence dimension, which is uniquely critical for personal-brand businesses.
