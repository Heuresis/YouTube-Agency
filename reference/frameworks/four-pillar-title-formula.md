# The 4-Pillar Title Formula Split (40 / 30 / 20 / 10)

## Origin archetype
Educational-content creator running a 7-million-view framework for online coaches and info-product owners.

## What it solves
"What kind of videos should I make?" without an explicit allocation devolves into either all-tactical (boring) or all-aspirational (empty). The 40/30/20/10 split fixes the content mix.

## Structure — four title formula categories with allocation

| Pillar | Allocation | Title templates |
|---|---|---|
| **How-To** | 40% | "How to [Achieve Specific Result] in [Timeframe] (Even if [Common Obstacle])"; "The Complete [Topic] Guide for [Specific Audience] in [Year]"; "Step-by-Step: [Process] That Got Me [Specific Result]" |
| **Case Study** | 30% | "How [Person/Student] Went From [Starting Point] to [End Result]"; "Behind the Scenes: The [Strategy] That Made Me [Specific Result]"; "The [Failure/Mistake] That Actually Led to [Success]" |
| **Myth-Busting** | 20% | "Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"; "[Number] Lies About [Topic] That Are Keeping You [Negative Result]"; "The [Industry] Secret Nobody Wants You to Know" |
| **Trend / Analysis** | 10% | "This [New Thing] Will Make [Prediction] (Here's Why)"; "[Person/Company] Just Changed [Industry] Forever"; "Why [Recent Event] Proves [Your Main Point]" |

## Allocation rationale
- **How-To 40%** — feeds the search-driven, intent-rich audience. These are the predictable mid-tail wins.
- **Case Study 30%** — feeds the social-proof and capability-belief installation. Lower CTR but higher conversion when watched.
- **Myth-Busting 20%** — feeds the contrarian / pattern-interrupt audience. Highest CTR per video, lowest predictability.
- **Trend / Analysis 10%** — reactive content slot. Reserved for platform updates, industry news, algorithmic changes.

## When to use
When planning a 30–50 video content calendar. The allocation is a constraint, not a suggestion — staying inside it produces a balanced channel; deviating produces either burnout (too many myth-busts) or staleness (too many how-tos).

## Why it works
The four formulas serve four distinct viewer intents (learn / aspire / question-the-status-quo / stay-current). A channel that serves only one intent shrinks its reachable audience to the intersection of the topic and that intent. Serving all four expands the addressable audience to the union.

## Variants observed
Some operators use a 50/30/20 split (no trend slot). The 40/30/20/10 form retained here is more common in info-product / coaching channels because the trend slot generates the highest single-video CTR spikes when timed correctly.
