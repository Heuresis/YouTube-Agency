# The Five Core Emotions Strategic Sequence

## Origin archetype
Long-form-VSL specialist who codified the emotional arc that consistently outperformed pure-rational scripts.

## What it solves
Scripts that hit only one or two emotions feel flat. Scripts that hit all five in the right *order* compose into a complete emotional arc that mirrors the viewer's path from skeptical scroll to identity-aligned purchase.

## Structure — five emotions, each with focus and sample triggers

| Emotion | Focus | Example trigger pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Fear** | What they'll lose if they don't act | "While you're reading this, competitors are stealing market share"; "Every day you wait costs you $X in lost opportunities" |
| **Anger** | What's holding them back (external forces) | "The industry experts don't want you to know this"; "Big companies are rigging the game against you" |
| **Greed** | What they'll gain by taking action | "Double your revenue in 90 days"; "Join the top 1% of earners in your industry" |
| **Guilt** | What they should be doing but aren't | "Your family deserves better"; "Stop letting your potential go to waste" |
| **Pride** | Who they'll become after transformation | "Join the elite group of [achieved-state]"; "Become the leader your industry respects" |

## The strategic sequence
Fear (attention) → Anger (motivation, external villain) → Greed (desire, payoff vision) → Guilt (pressure, internal motivation) → Pride (identity / close).

## Hard rule
Every great script hits a minimum of 3 of the 5 emotions. Best ones weave all 5. Hitting only one (typically pure greed: "make $10k/month") flattens the script and reads as scammy.

## When to use
Every long-form persuasion script. The sequence maps to the 15-step VSL spine: Fear in Hook (Step 1), Anger in Problem Deep Dive (Step 4), Greed in Features and Benefits (Step 7), Guilt in Urgency Story (Step 11), Pride in Future Pace (Step 15).

## Why it works
The arc mirrors the viewer's authentic emotional progression from external threat ("am I losing?") through agency-restoration ("who's blocking me?") to vision ("what's possible?") to internal stakes ("am I letting my future down?") to identity claim ("who am I becoming?"). Bypassing any one of these breaks the arc.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach 7 emotions (adding hope and shame); others teach 4 (collapsing fear into anxiety). Canonical form retained: 5, because the strategic sequence requires exactly 5 distinct emotional gears.
