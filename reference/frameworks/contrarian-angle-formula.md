# The Contrarian Angle Formula

## Origin archetype
Title-craft specialist with roots in pattern-interrupt copywriting; particularly effective in saturated niches where audiences are desensitized to standard advice.

## What it solves
In a saturated niche, every video sounds the same. The contrarian angle is the single most reliable pattern-interrupt — a title that explicitly contradicts the common advice forces the viewer to stop scrolling.

## Structure — the formula

"Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong (And [Alternative])"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "Why Posting Daily on YouTube Is Wrong (And What Works Better)"
- "Why Most Sales Advice Is Wrong (And Why [Specific Counter-Type of Person] Sell Better)"
- "Why Niching Down Is Wrong (And What 7-Figure Creators Actually Do)"

## Three load-bearing components
1. **Common Belief** — must actually be a belief the audience holds. If the contrarian frame attacks something the audience doesn't believe, it has no leverage.
2. **"Is Wrong"** — explicit contradiction, not softened ("might not work," "isn't the only way"). The directness is the pattern-interrupt.
3. **Alternative** — must be specific and credibly different. "Wrong... and here's the better way" without a specific better way reads as click-bait.

## When to use
Saturated niches; pattern-interrupt slots; channels where the operator's positioning is contrarian. Less effective in unsaturated niches because there's no widely-held belief to contradict.

## Why it works
The brain has a strong response to contradicted beliefs — it must either reject the new claim (which requires effort) or update the belief (which requires watching the video). Either way, attention is captured.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach a softer "rethinking [common belief]" version; others teach a harder "[common belief] is destroying your [outcome]" version. The "Is Wrong" form retained here is the highest-CTR variant; the harder version converts CTR to anger and can repel non-aligned viewers.
