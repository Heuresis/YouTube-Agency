# Six Principles of Influence (+ Unity)

## Origin archetype
Social-psychology compliance research (the influence-principles literature, plus its later seventh addition) operationalized by direct-response funnel builders and recast for creator channels.

## What it solves
These principles are not tricks; they are the observable defaults of how a viewer decides under load — load being a feed of two hundred competing thumbnails, an inbox of pitches, and fifteen seconds of patience. Every video, community post, DM, sales call, and landing page is already running these levers, well or badly, whether the creator knows their names or not. Naming them lets the creator audit every surface for absence (a missing lever that would have converted) and overuse (a lever pushed into manipulation, which breaks the trust contract — INV-16).

## Structure — the seven principles, mapped per surface

### 1. Reciprocity
**Default:** people who receive real value feel pulled to give attention, trust, or action back.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | Give the complete method, not the teaser — the free value IS the pitch for the paid depth |
| Community post | Drop a usable asset (template, checklist, breakdown) with no ask attached |
| DM | Answer the actual question first; link the resource before any mention of the offer |
| Sales call | Open with a genuine diagnosis of their situation before any pitch |
| Landing page | Lead magnet that solves one full problem, not a brochure |

**Overuse failure:** gift-stacking until the audience feels an obligation trap; low-value gift attached to a high-ticket ask (a PDF checklist does not earn a $5K pitch); reciprocity used as the *only* close — it accelerates belief layers, it does not replace them (`four-belief-layers`).

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
**Default:** after a small voluntary commitment, people act to stay consistent with it.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | Early agreement beats: state a premise the target viewer already holds, get the internal "yes" before the contrarian turn |
| Community post | Genuine micro-polls and stance questions ("which of these is your bottleneck?") — opinions, not bait |
| DM | One low-friction yes ("want the breakdown?") before any larger ask |
| Sales call | Verbal agreements early ("can we agree the bottleneck is X? then here's what that costs") |
| Landing page | Multi-step application — each completed field deepens the commitment |

**Overuse failure:** stacked commitment asks read as a script; manufactured "type YES if you're ready" prompts are engagement-bait and banned outright (INV-17). Genuine prompts pass; farming devices don't.

### 3. Social Proof
**Default:** under uncertainty, people copy the behavior of similar others. *Similar* is load-bearing — proof from an unmatched peer doesn't register.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | Matched-peer results shown on screen (`showing-not-telling`); specific, never aggregate-vague |
| Community post | Quote a member's win in their own words (with permission) |
| DM | One matched result, one sentence, no pile-on |
| Sales call | Two named-anonymized outcomes matching the prospect's stage and niche |
| Landing page | Tiered per `social-proof-hierarchy` — strongest matched proof above the fold |

**Overuse failure:** celebrity proof for an ordinary-viewer offer (disqualifies instead of persuading); a wall of same-sounding testimonials (pattern-matches as bought); cross-niche proof (wrong tribe); any fabricated or unverifiable proof — hard violation of INV-6 and the Truth Gate (INV-5).

### 4. Authority
**Default:** when the cost of being wrong is high, people defer to credible expertise signals.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | 2–3 lines of specific credential inside the first 90 seconds — receipts, not bio ("I've written N scripts in this niche; here's what holds") |
| Community post | One detail only a practitioner would know — the specific number, the named tool, the edge case |
| DM | Signature specificity, not titles ("I run the X system for Y-stage channels") |
| Sales call | One technical observation about *their* situation that proves real inspection |
| Landing page | Demonstrated mechanism (diagram, walkthrough) over badge walls |

**Overuse failure:** credential walls with no shipped outcomes; borrowed authority ("as seen in…") that sophisticated viewers discount; authority without warmth — pair it with Liking or it reads clinical.

### 5. Liking
**Default:** people say yes to people they like — driven by similarity, genuine attention, and cooperation far more than polish.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | The creator's real cadence and persona (per the parasocial-trust contract) — relatability beats production gloss |
| Community post | Reply in the member's vocabulary; remember and reference prior threads |
| DM | Open with a specific observation about *them*, not about the creator |
| Sales call | Mirror their pace and their words ("audience" if they say audience, not "list") |
| Landing page | Voice-faithful copy — reads like the videos sound |

**Overuse failure:** forced rapport and fake similarity (discovered once, trust is gone permanently — INV-16); over-familiarity with strangers; liking without authority, which reads as a friend asking for money.

### 6. Scarcity
**Default:** people value what is genuinely limited; loss registers roughly twice as hard as equivalent gain (see `system1-system2-persuasion`).

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | Cost-of-delay made arithmetic ("every month at this retention costs you X subscribers' worth of reach") |
| Community post | Real calendar announcements (cohort opens/closes) — dates that are true |
| DM | Real capacity statement with the structural reason ("I onboard N per month because I review every channel myself") |
| Sales call | Decision deadline with reason-why, never pressure theater |
| Landing page | Genuine caps and dates; remove the countdown widget if the count isn't real |

**Overuse failure:** fake timers, perpetual "2 spots left," deadline-extension habits — each detected instance poisons every future launch and violates INV-16. Scarcity without a stated reason-why triggers reactance in exactly the buyers worth having.

### 7. Unity
**Default:** shared identity — "we are the same kind of person" — outpulls mere similarity. Tribe, named in-group, shared standard.

| Surface | Operational move |
|---|---|
| Video | Speak to a named tribe ("for educators who refuse to chase trends"), not a demographic |
| Community post | Use and reward the community's own vocabulary (see `four-community-markers`) |
| DM | Same-tribe signal in the first line (shared niche, shared standard, shared enemy *approach*) |
| Sales call | The disqualification list ("we don't take X, Y, Z") creates membership for those who fit |
| Landing page | "Who this is for / not for" block — the negation builds the in-group |

**Overuse failure:** claimed unity without enforcement (taking every buyer dissolves the tribe); identity-exclusion theater with no substance behind it; unity without authority — a tribe needs a credible practitioner at its center. Out-groups must be categories of approach, never named people (`parasocial-ethical-guardrails`).

| Body, proof beats | Social Proof | Matched-peer artifacts on screen |
| Body, mechanism | Authority + Commitment | Demonstrated method; premise agreements before the turn |
| CTA | Reciprocity + Scarcity | Value already delivered; real next step with a true reason to act now |

## When to use
As an audit grid, not a recipe: run any high-stakes asset (pinned VSL, application page, launch email, sales-call script) against all seven and mark each *absent / present / overused*. Two or more "overused" marks = revise before ship; the asset has crossed from influence into pressure.

## Why it works
Each principle is a decision shortcut the brain runs when full evaluation is too expensive — and a viewer mid-scroll is always in that state. Surfaces that align with the shortcuts get processed; surfaces that fight them get skipped. The same shortcuts misfire when forced, which is why every overuse failure above reads to the audience as a specific flavor of "being sold at."

## Anti-patterns
- Running all seven at maximum on one surface — the asset reads as a compliance machine and sophisticated viewers bounce.
- Using the principles to compensate for a missing belief layer: influence levers accelerate decisions the audience is already structurally able to make; they cannot substitute for ownership, possibility, fit, or timing (`four-belief-layers`).
- Any deployment that fails the test "would this still work if the audience could see the label?" Ethical influence survives disclosure; manipulation doesn't (`parasocial-ethical-guardrails`).

## Variants observed
The original six-principle set predates the Unity addition; some operators also split Scarcity into scarcity-of-supply and loss-framing. Canonical form retained: 6 + Unity, because Unity is the dominant lever in community-driven channels and deserves first-class treatment.
