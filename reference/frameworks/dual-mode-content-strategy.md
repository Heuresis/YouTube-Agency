# Dual-Mode Content Strategy (Authority + Reach)

## Origin archetype
Personal-brand framework specialist + senior YouTube script consultant — the combined prescription for online-coach operators who need both audience growth *and* high-ticket conversion from the same channel.

## What it solves
A channel cannot be optimized for one mode (pure-authority or pure-reach) without sacrificing the other. The dual-mode strategy treats reach and authority as parallel modes that coexist in the channel's content calendar.

## Structure — two modes running in parallel

**Mode A — Authority / Niched (for the target ICP):**
- Awareness-level target: solution-aware, product-aware (Five Awareness Levels).
- Title formulas: how-to + case study (4-Pillar Title Formula, the 40% + 30% slots).
- Brand pillar: primary expertise focus (Brand Pillar Split, Pillar 1 dominant).
- Production cadence: deep, well-researched, longer-form (10–25 minutes).
- Function: convert existing audience into qualified leads, calls, customers.
- Belief installation: beliefs 5, 6, 7, 8 (mechanism, comparative superiority, trust in person, trust in expertise).

**Mode B — Broad-Appeal / Reach (for audience growth):**
- Awareness-level target: unaware, problem-aware (Five Awareness Levels).
- Title formulas: myth-busting + trend / contrarian + curiosity gap (4-Pillar Title Formula, the 20% + 10% slots; Curiosity Gap Formula, Contrarian Angle Formula).
- Brand pillar: philosophy / lessons (Brand Pillar Split, Pillar 4) and broad takes that reach beyond the niche.
- Production cadence: tighter, higher-energy, often shorter (8–15 minutes).
- Function: feed the channel new viewers; expand the addressable audience.
- Belief installation: beliefs 2, 3, 4 (must-fulfill-derivative-desire, capability, urgency).

## The two-mode allocation
- Approximately 60% authority-niched + 40% broad-appeal-reach for mature channels.
- Approximately 40% authority-niched + 60% broad-appeal-reach for new channels (need more reach to break threshold).
- Adjustable based on the operator's offer maturity, niche saturation, and current channel performance.

## Inter-mode interaction
Reach-mode videos contain explicit hooks and binge-loops to authority-mode videos. Authority-mode videos contain hard CTAs to the offer / lead magnet. The reach-to-authority handoff is the channel's internal funnel.

## When to use
Continuous channel-strategy decision. Every new video is either Mode A or Mode B; deciding which is the first question on the planning template.

## Why it works
The channel's audience-growth and conversion-rate functions are mathematically separate (audience growth is measured in subscribers and watch-time impressions, conversion is measured in lead-to-call rate). Optimizing for both simultaneously is impossible inside a single video; treating them as parallel modes lets the channel optimize for both across the calendar.

## Variants observed
Some operators advocate single-mode focus (typically pure authority for high-ticket coaching). The dual-mode form retained here is the strategically-correct prescription for online-coach / info-product creators who use YouTube as the front door to an owned offer — because both reach and conversion are required, and the offer's quality depends on disciplined separation between modes.
