# Contrast Narrative Formats (Structural Templates)

## Origin archetype
Title-craft specialist focused on identity-based hook structures (the "before vs after" archetype across multiple operator brands).

## What it solves
Three structural patterns that exploit the audience's auto-completion of the gap between two contrasting states. Each template is structurally distinct from the contrast hook (Four Hook Formulas, Formula C) — these are full-narrative-arc patterns, not single-sentence hooks.

## Structure — three templates

### Template A: 1-vs-9-5 (Identity Pivot)

**Pattern:** Operator reveals a specific moment when they made an unconventional choice that pivoted them away from a conventional path. The "1" is the unusual choice; the "9-5" is the conventional path rejected.

**Generic structural example:** "In [year], I quit my [conventional credential / job title] to start [unconventional venture]. Today, that same [unconventional venture] [specific present-day result]. [Reframe: what the conventional path would have produced is now framed as a worse outcome than the unconventional path]."

**Why it works:** The audience auto-completes the question "what happened in the gap between leaving the 9-5 and the present-day result?" — the operator's video then fills that gap, with the audience already invested in the answer.

### Template B: Before-vs-After (Personal Transformation)

**Pattern:** Operator establishes a vulnerable, low-status starting state, then reveals a high-status current state, with the gap between the two being the subject of the video.

**Generic structural example:** "[N months/years] ago, I was [vulnerable starting state — broke, in debt, confused, rejected]. Today, [high-status current state — quantified, specific, named]. Here's exactly what changed."

**Why it works:** The before-vs-after pattern activates the comparative-self mechanism at the narrative level. The audience auto-asks "could I make this transition?" — the operator's video answers with the path.

### Template C: Then-vs-Now (Industry / Market Shift)

**Pattern:** Operator establishes a past industry / market state, then contrasts it with the present state, with the implication being that the audience must adapt or fall behind.

**Generic structural example:** "Most people [are still doing the thing that worked in past-year], wondering why they can't get traction. But I'm about to show you EXACTLY what's working right now. Most people fail because they're trying to copy what worked in [past year]. They're following outdated playbooks. The truth about [current year] is..."

**Why it works:** The then-vs-now pattern activates the urgency belief. The audience auto-recognizes that they may be on the "then" side and must update — the operator's video provides the update path.

## When to use across the three templates
- **1-vs-9-5:** when the operator has a specific identity pivot (job-quit, school-quit, geography-shift) to anchor the narrative.
- **Before-vs-After:** when the operator has a specific personal transformation to anchor the narrative (financial, skill, status).
- **Then-vs-Now:** when the operator is reacting to an industry / market / platform shift.

## Why each works at the structural level
All three templates exploit the same underlying mechanism — the audience's automatic gap-filling — but anchored to three different identity axes (operator's identity pivot, operator's personal transformation, market / industry transformation). Different audiences respond to different anchors; the channel-level prescription is to use all three across the content calendar at different ratios depending on operator brand and audience composition.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach a 5-template extended version (adding generation-vs-generation and competitor-vs-self templates). The 3-template form retained here is the minimum-set that covers the dominant identity-pivot anchors observed across the source corpus.
