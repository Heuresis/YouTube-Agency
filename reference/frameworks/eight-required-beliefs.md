# The Eight Required Beliefs (Long-Form VSL Belief Installation)

## Origin archetype
High-ticket-info-product creator running 8-figure VSL funnels who codified the belief stack viewers must hold before any high-ticket purchase will close.

## What it solves
Most "value" content fails to convert because it teaches *how* to do something to viewers who don't yet believe they *can* do it, that *now* is the time, or that *the speaker* is credible. The belief stack tells you which content to make in which order.

## Structure — the 8 beliefs

1. **Reproduction belief** — they must believe they need to reproduce / replicate / exceed their current state (this is the baseline human drive that all marketing rests on).
2. **Derivative-desire belief** — they must believe they need to fulfill the specific derivative desire your offer satisfies (e.g., "I need to scale to 10K/month online").
3. **Capability belief** — they must believe they CAN fulfill that desire (i.e., "people like me have done this").
4. **Urgency belief** — they must believe they must do it NOW, not later.
5. **Mechanism belief** — they must believe YOUR offer/method/system fulfills the desire.
6. **Comparative-superiority belief** — they must believe your offer fulfills the desire BETTER and FASTER than alternatives.
7. **Trust-in-person belief** — they must trust YOU as a person (not a scammer, not flaky, has goodwill).
8. **Trust-in-expertise belief** — they must trust your expertise and the actual results your offer produces.

## Implementation
Every video in the operator's content calendar installs 1–2 of these beliefs. A 30-video content plan is structurally a 30-step belief-installation sequence — not a topic list. The mapping is deliberate and ordered.

## Content-to-belief mapping
- Belief 2 (must fulfill desire) → consequence-of-not content; paint the gap between current state and desired state.
- Belief 3 (CAN fulfill) → success stories of people like the viewer; transformation testimonials; "if they can do it, you can too."
- Belief 4 (urgency) → opportunity-cost content; time-sensitive market conditions; scarcity / sunset framing.
- Belief 5 (mechanism) → behind-the-scenes of your system; "here's how it works."
- Belief 6 (better than alternatives) → comparison content; "why other methods fail"; "the problem with [common alternative]."
- Belief 7 (trust in person) → personal moments; vulnerability; behind-the-scenes; day-in-the-life content.
- Belief 8 (trust in expertise) → results posts; proof artifacts; case studies.

## When to use
When mapping a creator's first 30–50 videos against the offer they will eventually pitch. The belief stack is the planning lens; specific topics are the surface output.

## Why it works
Purchase is a multi-step belief cascade. A viewer who fails any one of the eight will not buy regardless of how many testimonials or how strong the offer. Each belief is a gate; missing any gate stalls conversion.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach a 5-belief subset for attention (the ABBB rule); others teach the full 8 for purchase. Canonical form retained: the 8 for purchase, with an explicit cross-reference to the 5-belief attention set used for content gating.
