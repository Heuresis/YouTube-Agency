# The Three-Brain Decision Sequence

## Origin archetype
Long-form-VSL specialist working on 8-figure educational funnels for high-ticket info-product creators.

## What it solves
Most scripts speak only to the rational brain and lose the viewer in the first 3 seconds because they ignore the survival and emotional brains that gate attention.

## Structure

| Window | Brain region | The viewer's silent question | What the script must do |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–3 seconds | Reptilian / survival | "Is this safe? Is this real?" | Immediate credibility cue, specific proof, no scam-alarm triggers, professional-but-authentic delivery |
| 3–15 seconds | Limbic / emotional | "Do I want this?" | Desire creation, future-state visualization, status / pain-relief / belonging trigger, social proof from a relatable other — desire **before** education |
| 15+ seconds | Neocortex / rational | "Can I justify this?" | Logical frameworks, ROI math, risk-mitigation, step-by-step actionability |

**Hard rule:** Never deliver logic to a viewer whose survival brain is still scanning for "is this real." Specificity in the first 3 seconds (a single concrete number, a single named artifact) does the safety-check work that no abstract claim can do.

## When to use
Every long-form video opening (8-minute and up). The framework is the explicit structural answer to the question "what goes in the first 15 seconds and why."

## Why it works
Attention and purchase decisions resolve in roughly 200ms, sequentially down a phylogenetically older-to-newer brain stack. The viewer cannot evaluate logic until safety and desire have already cleared. Scripts that try to convince with reason in seconds 0–3 are filtered out before the rational brain ever reads them.

## Variants observed
Some operators frame this as a 3-second / 30-second / 3-minute hierarchy; others use the 0–3 / 3–15 / 15+ split. Canonical form retained: the 0–3 / 3–15 / 15+ second timecodes, because they map cleanly to the four hook formulas in the Four Hook Formulas framework.
