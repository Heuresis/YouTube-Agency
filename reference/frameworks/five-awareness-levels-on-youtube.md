# The Five Awareness Levels Applied to YouTube

## Origin archetype
Direct-response copywriting tradition (the awareness-spectrum author) ported to YouTube attention-economy by a senior script consultant.

## What it solves
Match the script's language, depth, and promises to the viewer's current awareness state. Wrong message at wrong awareness level kills the video — the right message at the wrong level reads either as patronizing (too basic) or as gibberish (too advanced).

## Structure — five awareness states

| Level | Viewer's mental state | Script approach | Language pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know they have a problem | Educate on the problem first; use stories that reveal hidden issues; never pitch solutions | "Did you know that [surprising fact about their situation]..." |
| **Problem-aware** | Knows the problem, doesn't know solutions exist | Agitate pain, then introduce solution categories | "If you're struggling with [specific problem], there's finally a way to..." |
| **Solution-aware** | Knows solutions exist, doesn't know YOUR solution | Differentiate your mechanism; explain why your approach is different | "Most people try [common solution]. But there's a different approach that..." |
| **Product-aware** | Knows your solution, hasn't taken action | Overcome specific objections; risk reversal, social proof, urgency | "You've probably seen [your solution] before. Here's why now is the time to..." |
| **Most-aware** | Ready to act, needs final push | Simple, clear CTA; remove friction; create urgency | "If you're ready to [outcome], here's exactly what to do next..." |

## The pyramid
At unaware sit the most people (broadest audience, lowest conversion). At most-aware sit the fewest (narrowest audience, highest conversion).

## Critical script-writing rule
When writing the script, tailor the language to the awareness level the **video** is meant to target — not the average level of the channel's audience. Different videos on the same channel target different levels.

## When to use
The framework lives at the title and hook layer. Title selection is implicitly an awareness-level commitment. The script must then deliver against that commitment.

## Why it works
Viewers self-select based on title, and they bring an internal model of their own awareness state. A title that reads as too-broad to a most-aware viewer reads as gibberish to an unaware viewer. The script must match what the title promised, and the title's awareness level is encoded in its specificity.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach 3 awareness levels (compressed); some teach 7 (extended). Canonical form retained: 5, because it maps cleanly to the 6-step product-purchase decision and the standard direct-response copywriting canon.
