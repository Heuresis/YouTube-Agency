# The Bound Promise Formula (Time-Bound)

## Origin archetype
Title-craft specialist focused on results-driven content for entrepreneurs and high-ticket creators.

## What it solves
Vague promises ("how to grow your business") have low CTR because the viewer cannot evaluate whether the time investment is worth it. A time-bound promise is concrete.

## Structure — the formula

"How I [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe] ([Qualifier])"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "How I Gained 100K Subscribers in 6 Months (Starting From Zero)"
- "How I Made $10K in 30 Days (With No Experience)"
- "How I Closed My First $5K Client in 90 Days (Without Cold Calls)"

## Three load-bearing components
1. **Achieved Result** — specific, quantified outcome.
2. **Timeframe** — bounded duration that the viewer can map against their own situation.
3. **Qualifier** — typically a relatability hook ("starting from zero," "with no experience") that lowers the bar and includes the viewer.

## When to use
Mid-tier title slot, especially when the operator has a specific case study or personal story to anchor the timeframe to. The qualifier is the critical element — without it, the title sounds like a humble-brag; with it, the title becomes "if they can, I can."

## Why it works
Time-bounded outcomes activate the comparative-self mechanism. The viewer auto-calculates: "Could I do this in [Timeframe]? With my [situation]?" — and the qualifier ("starting from zero") preempts the disqualifying objection.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach a result-only version ("How I Made $10K"); others teach a fully-bounded version with specific start-state, timeframe, and end-state. The 3-component form retained here is the most commonly successful CTR-driver in the source corpus.
