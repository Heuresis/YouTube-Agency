# The Re-Hook → Story → Lesson Per-Section Pattern

## Origin archetype
Long-form educational creator with 60%+ retention curves on 15+ minute videos.

## What it solves
Long-form videos have multiple drop-off zones (30s, 2min, 5min, mid-video). A single opening hook does not protect against later drop-offs. Each body section needs its own micro-hook to recapture attention.

## Structure — the per-section three-beat pattern

**Beat 1: Re-hook.** Recapture attention as viewers naturally fatigue.
- Formula: Problem → Agitate → Delay solution.
- Generic structural example: "Now, the average person trying to [goal] makes one critical mistake that guarantees [negative outcome]. This mistake has [amplify stakes]. And it's probably the same mistake you're about to make. But what exactly is this mistake? Well, to understand this, I first need to tell you about..."

**Beat 2: Story.** Create emotional connection, maintain engagement, make abstract concepts concrete.
- Framework: BUT-THEREFORE structure.
- Sub-structure: Arrival (set scene) → Adversity (introduce conflict) → Aftermath (reveal consequences and lessons).

**Beat 3: Lesson.** Deliver the promised value.
- Actually deliver what you teased in the re-hook.
- Make the solution as valuable as you hyped it.
- Break complex concepts into simple, actionable steps.
- Use specific examples and proof.
- Leave viewers feeling satisfied, inspired, and capable.

## Section-to-section bridging
After the lesson, use one of the seven transition types (Question Bridge, Problem Bridge, Tease Bridge, Callback Bridge, Contrast Bridge, Story Bridge, Summary Bridge) to link to the next re-hook.

## When to use
Every body section of every long-form video (8 minutes+). For an 8-section video, expect 8 instances of the pattern, with 8 transitions between them.

## Why it works
The pattern creates a periodic re-engagement structure that respects the brain's natural attention cycle (~3–5 minute fatigue interval). Each new re-hook resets the curiosity loop; each story creates emotional engagement; each lesson delivers a satisfaction hit that primes the viewer to continue rather than disengage.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach a 4-beat pattern (Re-hook → Story → Lesson → Application); others teach a 2-beat (Story → Lesson). Canonical form retained: 3 beats, because the re-hook is the load-bearing element and dropping it produces the "video meanders" failure mode.
