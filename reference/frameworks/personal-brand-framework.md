# Personal Brand Framework — 10-Section Template

## Origin archetype
Personal-brand framework specialist working with online coaches, info-product creators, and high-ticket service operators.

## What it solves
Most personal-brand exercises are vibes-based ("find your voice"). The 10-section template is a deterministic workbook that extracts a creator's positioning by forcing answers to ten specific structural questions.

## Structure — the 10 sections

1. **Your X-Factor Discovery** — background analysis, core motivation, contrarian beliefs, demographic resonance, geographic / cultural angle. Surface what makes the operator structurally different from the competition.
2. **Your Core Mission & Philosophy** — teaching approach, transformation goals, philosophy (teach-to-fish vs give-fish vs both), real-world comparison.
3. **Competitive Analysis** — per-competitor breakdown (what to adopt, why it works, how to adapt) + "your unique angle vs competitors" along Depth Level / Teaching Style / Outcome Focus.
4. **Your Expertise Breakdown** — 3–5 core technical-depth areas the operator can teach.
5. **Desired Perception** — Professional Identity Statement, Perception Strategy (Professional Layer / Deeper Layer / Content Balance), Value Proposition, Services laddered (Done-With-You / Done-For-You / Education/Courses).
6. **Content Strategy** — Tone & Style, Energy Level, Professional vs Casual ratio, Hook Style, Main Content Style, Visual Style, Production Quality, Editing Style, Color/Aesthetic.
7. **Your Unique Angles** — 5–8 personal-story angles, each with Core Message / Why it matters / 3 Content Ideas. Pre-defined angle slots include: open / Biggest Failure / Mentor & Wisdom / Professional or Educational Advantage / Cultural or Geographic Perspective / Investment or Risk Story / Current Situation or Urgency.
8. **Content Pillars** — 50/25/15/10 split, with each pillar specifying: What it covers / Positioning / Target audience / 3 sample hook ideas.
9. **Lead Generation Plan** — typically lead magnet + audience-research workflow (the section is implicitly defined by downstream lead-generation infrastructure).
10. **Brand Evolution Plan** — 6-month vision + 12-month vision; how positioning, depth-of-reveal, and audience perception should evolve.

## Two operating constraints
- All 10 sections must be filled in completely. Skipping a section produces cascading inconsistencies downstream.
- The 6-month and 12-month evolution plans are non-optional — without them, the operator's brand is locked in its starting state and cannot grow into authority.

## The sub-prompt for X-Factor Discovery (verbatim sample)
- "What's your background that differs from others in your space?"
- "What advantages/disadvantages shaped your path?"
- "What 'safe route' did you reject or could you reject?"
- "Why are you really doing this? (beyond money)"
- "What conventional wisdom do you reject?"
- "What does everyone else want that you couldn't care less about?"
- "Who shares your background/situation?"
- "Where are you from and how does that influence your perspective?"

## When to use
Initial creator-brand intake. Run the full 10-section workbook before any content is produced. The output of this workbook is the input to the 50/25/15/10 content-pillar plan and the 30–50 video belief-installation calendar.

## Why it works
The framework forces operators past surface-level positioning ("I help X do Y") into the differentiated, story-anchored, evolving brand that actually wins on a competitive YouTube channel. A surface-level brand cannot survive 30 videos; a fully-extracted brand has 30 videos worth of material before the calendar is built.

## Variants observed
Some operators use a 7-section workbook (no evolution plan); others use a 12-section extended version. Canonical form retained: 10 sections, because the missing sections in the 7-section form (X-factor and evolution plan) are the most load-bearing for differentiation and longevity.
