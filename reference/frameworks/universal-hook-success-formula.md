# The Universal Hook Success Formula (Educational Content)

## Origin archetype
Educational-content specialist running a 7-million-view framework for online coaches and info-product creators.

## What it solves
This is the meta-formula — the four cognitive ingredients every million-view educational hook combines. The Four Hook Formulas (PAST/AID/Contrast/Statistic) are formula-level applications; this framework is the cognitive recipe behind them.

## Structure — the four ingredients

1. **Specific Audience Problem** — call out the exact pain point the viewer is experiencing right now.
2. **Unique Solution Promise** — tease something the viewer hasn't heard before (not "I'll teach you the basics" — *new* information).
3. **Credibility Indicator** — prove you can deliver on the promise (specific number, named entity, on-camera artifact).
4. **Curiosity Gap** — create information tension that demands resolution (loop opening).

## Why each ingredient is non-optional
- Without specific audience problem: the hook reads as not-for-me.
- Without unique solution promise: the hook reads as redundant ("more of the same advice").
- Without credibility indicator: the hook reads as fantasy (not trustable).
- Without curiosity gap: the hook reads as already-resolved (no reason to keep watching).

## Why this works as a meta-formula
- Human brains are hardwired to focus on unsolved problems (survival mechanism).
- After problems are identified, brains crave solutions.
- Curiosity gaps create mental tension that demands resolution.
- Social proof / credibility triggers the "if they can do it, maybe I can too" response.

## When to use
Validation pass on every hook. Run the 4-ingredient checklist after writing a hook; rewrite if any ingredient is missing.

## Variants observed
Some operators teach 3-ingredient hooks (problem + promise + curiosity); others teach 5-ingredient (adding "future-pace"). The 4-ingredient form retained here is the minimum-viable hook that consistently produces million-view-level CTR in educational content.
