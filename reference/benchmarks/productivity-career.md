# Productivity & Career — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in productivity / career YouTube channels (knowledge-work systems, career-development, productivity tools, professional skill development). Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time.

## Niche scope

Productivity / career includes: productivity systems / tooling, knowledge-management workflows, career development, professional skill building, time management / focus, productivity-tool tutorials (Notion, Obsidian, Linear, etc.), creator-tooling, freelancer / contractor operations. Audience tilt: knowledge workers, founders, freelancers, career-progressors, system-loving (often optimizers).

## CTR ranges by length-band

Long-form (8–20 min):
- Floor band: 4–5%
- Typical band: 5–8%
- Upper band: 8–12%
- Outlier: 12%+

Short-long (4–8 min):
- Floor: 5–6%
- Typical: 6–10%
- Upper: 10–13%

Shorts:
- Typical: 7–12%
- Upper: 12–20%

## AVD ranges by length-band

10-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 45%
- Typical APV: 50–58%
- Upper APV: 60%+

20-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 38%
- Typical APV: 42–52%
- Upper APV: 55%+

System-build videos (15–30 min):
- Floor APV: 35%
- Typical APV: 40–50%

The productivity-systems-builder's "screen-recording slow burn" pattern is dominant. Steeper early drop (some viewers don't want screen-recording-heavy content) then flat through the demonstration.

## RPM bands

Productivity / career is mid CPM:

- Floor: $7
- Typical: $12–$25
- Upper: $25–$40
- Outlier: $40+ — typically when audience composition skews executive / high-income knowledge worker

Sub-niche variation:
- General productivity / time-management: $10–$18 RPM
- Knowledge-management / second-brain: $12–$22 RPM
- Career development / interview-prep: $10–$20 RPM
- Notion / Obsidian / specific-tool tutorials: $15–$28 RPM
- Freelancer / contractor operations: $15–$30 RPM

## Sponsor CPM bands

Sponsor CPM in productivity / career:

- Floor: $20 per CPM
- Typical: $35–$70 per CPM
- Upper: $70–$120 per CPM

Common sponsor categories: productivity tools (Notion, Obsidian, Linear, Asana), note-taking apps, focus / time-tracking tools, learning platforms, career-development platforms (LinkedIn-adjacent, interview-prep), freelancer-operation tools.

## Retention curve archetypes

- **The screen-recording slow burn** — dominant for productivity-systems-builder content.
- **The clean staircase** — for analytical-explainer-style productivity content.
- **The system-build curve** — when a system is built on screen, AVD spikes at each system-element addition.

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"My [tool] setup / system / vault / dashboard"** — system-walkthrough.
- **"How to [productivity skill]"** — tutorial; search-traffic-strong.
- **"X habits / systems / patterns of [audience]"** — listicle / framework.
- **"Why most [productivity advice] is wrong"** — contrarian frame.
- **"My weekly / daily routine"** — behind-the-scenes / story-driven.
- **"From [bad workflow] to [good workflow]"** — transformation / case-study.
- **"[Tool A] vs [Tool B]"** — contrast-format.
- **"Career advice for [stage]"** — career-tier content.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 22–45 typical primary; knowledge-worker tilt
- **Gender**: variable by sub-niche; tech-tooling skews male; career-development more balanced
- **Geography**: US / Europe / India / Latin America significantly represented; global-knowledge-worker audience
- **Income**: variable; career-stage spread
- **Education**: typically college-educated

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in productivity / career:

- **View-to-application** (coaching / cohort): 0.5–2% on pinned VSL
- **Course / template purchases**: 1–4% conversion from email funnel
- **Application-to-call**: 35–55%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 25–40%

LTV per email subscriber: typically $40–$150; can reach $300+ for executive-coaching or specialized cohort offerings.

## Regulatory considerations

Lower regulatory burden:

- **FTC**: #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate content
- **Earnings / career claims**: substantiated only ("our students average X" with data)
- **Tool / template performance claims**: substantiated by data

## Banned content patterns

- "Increase productivity 10x in a week" — over-promising
- Recycled productivity advice with stale tool versions
- Fake schedule / routine claims (audience detects this)
- Affiliate-link disclosures missed (INV-15)

## Calibration note

Productivity / career is a steady-build niche. Channels grow gradually but library compounds well — productivity tutorials and system walkthroughs have multi-year long-tail demand. The library compound is the lever; new channels in the floor band; mature channels with 100+ videos and strong tool-stack-curriculum architecture in the typical or upper band.
