# Personal Finance — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in personal-finance YouTube channels. Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time; what's documented here is the structural range a new channel can use as starting calibration. The bands are not promises — they are the typical-channel envelope.

## Niche scope

Personal finance includes: budgeting, debt elimination, investing education, retirement planning, real-estate investing, tax strategy, wealth-building, financial independence, FIRE-movement-adjacent content. Audience tilt: high loss-aversion (financial decisions activate it), credibility-gated (audience disqualifies operators without standing), multi-life-stage (different tiers — beginner / intermediate / advanced — with different needs).

## CTR ranges by length-band

Long-form (8–25 min):
- Floor band: 4–5%
- Typical band: 5–8%
- Upper band: 8–12%
- Outlier (above-band): 12%+ — usually reach-mode content with universal-appeal hooks

Short-long (4–8 min):
- Floor: 5–6%
- Typical: 6–10%
- Upper: 10–14%

Shorts (vertical):
- Typical: 8–14%
- Upper: 15–25%
- The vertical feed's relative-CTR-equivalent is significantly higher than long-form

## RPM bands

Personal finance is high-CPM. Typical RPM bands (revenue per 1,000 views, after platform share):

- Floor: $15
- Typical: $20–$35
- Upper: $35–$50
- Outlier: $50+ — typically channels with high investor / high-net-worth audience composition

Sub-niche variation:
- General personal finance: $20–$35 RPM
- Investing education: $25–$40 RPM
- Real estate: $25–$50 RPM
- Tax strategy: $30–$55 RPM
- Wealth-building / FIRE: $20–$40 RPM

## Sponsor CPM bands

Sponsor CPM (the rate sponsors pay per 1,000 views in negotiated integrations) typically tracks 1.5–2.5x the platform RPM:

- Floor: $25 per CPM (per integration)
- Typical: $40–$70 per CPM
- Upper: $70–$120 per CPM

Common sponsor categories: investment platforms, brokerages, tax software, real-estate-investment platforms, wealth-management services, finance-adjacent tools (budgeting apps, etc.).

## Retention curve archetypes

Personal-finance audiences typically produce:

- **The trough-recovery** — significant drop in the first 1–2 minutes (audience self-disqualifying based on credibility / regulatory feel), then stable retention. Channels that hit the floor APV typically show this pattern.
- **The clean staircase** — for analytical-explainer-style finance content. Steady decline; high info-density.
- **The cost-of-inaction curve** — for loss-aversion-heavy content. Strong opening retention (loss-aversion hook works), stable mid-section, drop at the close (typically when the offer-pitch or recap arrives).

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"How much should you have saved by [age]"** — life-stage benchmarking content. High evergreen demand.
- **"X mistakes [audience] makes with money"** — listicle format; high CTR.
- **"How to [tax / investment / retirement] strategy"** — how-to content; search-traffic-leaning; long-tail-strong.
- **"[Income / lifestyle] vs [income / lifestyle]"** — contrast format; reach-strong.
- **"Why most [advice] is wrong"** — contrarian-narrator pattern; high engagement.
- **Real-time market / news commentary** — timely; lower long-tail but high short-window views.
- **Case studies of clients / operators / families** — transformation content; conversion-strong.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 25–55 typical primary; 18–25 secondary (FIRE-movement-adjacent), 55+ tertiary (retirement-planning)
- **Gender**: roughly balanced; some sub-niches skew (FIRE skews male; budgeting skews female)
- **Geography**: US dominant; UK / Australia / Canada secondary; emerging-market growth in finance content (varies)
- **Income**: bimodal — pre-financial-stability (looking for advice) and post-financial-stability (looking for advanced strategies)
- **Education**: typically college-educated; sub-niches like investing skew higher

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in the personal-finance space:

- **View-to-application** (from pinned VSL): 0.5–2% in month 1; 1–3% mature
- **Application-to-call**: 35–55%
- **Call show rate**: 65–75%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 25–35% on $3K–5K offers; 15–25% on $10K+

LTV per email subscriber (in personal finance with a $5K coaching offer): typically $80–$200 calibrated; recalibrate per-channel from real data.

## Regulatory considerations

Personal finance is regulated. The channel must comply with:

- **US**: SEC investment-advice rules (registered vs unregistered); FTC #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate content
- **UK**: FCA finfluencer rules (effective 2024); FCA-authorised vs unauthorised promotional content
- **EU**: MiFID II implications; country-specific finfluencer rules
- **Australia**: ASIC finfluencer guidance
- **Canada**: provincial securities-regulator rules

The channel's compliance discipline is a structural feature (see `personal-finance-mentor-archetype.md` for archetype-specific compliance). Disclaimers in description, on-screen disclaimers in regulated content, no-guarantee language on outcomes — all standard.

## Banned content patterns

INV-15 + INV-5 + INV-6 + jurisdiction-specific:

- Specific stock / asset / investment recommendations without proper licensing
- Past-performance-as-future-promise framing
- Unsubstantiated income claims ("$10K/mo guaranteed")
- Affiliate links to regulated products without disclosure
- Personal client data / specific account numbers (privacy + regulatory)

## Calibration note

These bands recalibrate per-channel from real data over the channel's first 3–6 months. New channels typically land in the floor band; mature channels in the typical band; outlier channels (top 5%) in the upper band. The bands shift over time as the platform's algorithm and the niche's competitive density evolve.
