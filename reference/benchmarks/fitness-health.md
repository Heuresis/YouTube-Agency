# Fitness & Health — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in fitness / health YouTube channels (training, nutrition, body-composition transformation, athletic performance, wellness-adjacent). Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time.

## Niche scope

Fitness / health includes: strength training, cardio / endurance, hypertrophy / bodybuilding, weight-loss / fat-loss, nutrition science, athletic performance, mobility / flexibility, wellness-adjacent (sleep, recovery, supplements). Audience tilt: visual / transformation-driven, before-vs-after responsive, high parasocial trust with operators who demonstrate physical results.

## CTR ranges by length-band

Long-form (8–20 min):
- Floor band: 5–6%
- Typical band: 6–9%
- Upper band: 9–13%
- Outlier: 13%+ — typically transformation-content with strong visual contrast

CTR is structurally above some other authority niches because the visual nature of the niche supports stronger thumbnail design (before/after, body-composition contrast).

Short-long (3–6 min):
- Floor: 6–7%
- Typical: 7–11%
- Upper: 11–15%

Shorts:
- Typical: 8–14%
- Upper: 14–25%

## AVD ranges by length-band

8-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 45%
- Typical APV: 50–58%
- Upper APV: 60%+

15-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 40%
- Typical APV: 45–53%
- Upper APV: 56%+

25-minute videos (uncommon for fitness — typically tutorial / podcast content):
- Floor APV: 35%
- Typical APV: 40–48%

## RPM bands

Fitness / health is lower CPM than business / finance:

- Floor: $4
- Typical: $6–$12
- Upper: $12–$20
- Outlier: $20+ — typically when audience composition skews high-income / executive

Sub-niche variation:
- General fitness / training: $5–$10 RPM
- Bodybuilding / hypertrophy: $5–$10 RPM
- Nutrition / supplementation: $7–$15 RPM
- Athletic performance / endurance: $6–$12 RPM
- Wellness / recovery / sleep: $8–$15 RPM

The lower RPM band is offset by a much larger audience pool — fitness has broad reach.

## Sponsor CPM bands

Sponsor CPM in fitness / health:

- Floor: $15 per CPM
- Typical: $25–$50 per CPM
- Upper: $50–$90 per CPM

Common sponsor categories: protein / supplement brands, fitness-app subscriptions, training-equipment, wearables (fitness trackers, smart-rings), nutrition / meal-prep services, athletic-apparel.

## Retention curve archetypes

- **The transformation-narrative climb** — for before-vs-after / case-study content. Hook is the after-state; viewers stay to see the journey. Strong end-retention.
- **The contrast-stack flat** — for contrast-format-viral-specialist patterns applied to fitness (e.g., "cheap supplements vs expensive"). Steady AVD across the comparison.
- **The tutorial slow burn** — for instructional content (form videos, technique). Steeper early drop, then flat.

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"How I [transformed] in [time]"** — transformation-content; story-driven-creator overlap.
- **"X mistakes [audience] makes with [training / nutrition]"** — listicle; high CTR.
- **"Why [popular advice] is wrong"** — contrarian frame in this niche.
- **"The science of [topic]"** — analytical-explainer overlap; evergreen.
- **"How to [exercise / pattern] correctly"** — tutorial; search-traffic-strong.
- **"[Diet A] vs [Diet B]"** — comparison; contrast-format.
- **"What I eat in a day"** — lifestyle / behind-the-scenes; high engagement.
- **Workout / training plan walkthroughs** — system content.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 18–45 typical primary; varies significantly by sub-niche (bodybuilding skews 18–35; longevity / wellness skews 30–55)
- **Gender**: variable by sub-niche (bodybuilding skews male; pilates / yoga skews female; general-fitness more balanced)
- **Geography**: US / UK / Australia / Brazil heavily represented; global engagement
- **Income**: variable; the audience pool is broad
- **Education**: variable

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in fitness / health (coaching, courses, training programs):

- **View-to-application**: 0.5–2% on pinned VSL
- **Application-to-purchase** (low-ticket / course): 5–15% conversion through email funnel
- **Application-to-call** (high-ticket coaching): 35–50%
- **Call show rate**: 65–75%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 25–40% on coaching offers

LTV per email subscriber: typically $30–$120; lower than business/finance because offers are typically lower-ticket. Higher in specialized niches (athletic performance coaching, executive wellness) where offers reach $5K–$15K range.

## Regulatory considerations

Health / fitness is regulated:

- **FDA / equivalent**: no medical claims; supplement claims must be substantiated; weight-loss claims subject to FTC review
- **Medical-advice boundaries**: no diagnosis, no treatment claims; refer-to-professional language standard
- **Body-image considerations**: ethical content discipline (no "lose 30 lbs in 30 days" exploitative framing)
- **Sponsor disclosure**: INV-15 + supplement/fitness-specific FTC enhanced scrutiny

## Banned content patterns

- "Cure [condition]" or "treat [condition]" — medical-claim violations
- Unsubstantiated supplement claims
- Body-shaming content (audience attrition + platform-policy concerns)
- Before/after photos with deceptive framing (lighting, posing, time-elapsed undisclosed)
- Affiliate links without disclosure (INV-15)

## Calibration note

Fitness / health is a high-reach, lower-monetization niche by default. The lever for revenue is the back-end offer (coaching, programs) or sponsor relationships, not platform RPM. Channels that build a strong back-end offer can outperform their platform RPM by 5–10x in total revenue.
