# Real Estate — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in real-estate YouTube channels (residential investing, commercial real-estate, multi-family, BRRRR / flipping, real-estate education, market analysis). Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time.

## Niche scope

Real estate includes: residential rental investing, commercial real-estate, multi-family, BRRRR (Buy / Rehab / Rent / Refinance / Repeat) / flipping, REITs / passive investing, real-estate-market analysis, real-estate-coaching info products, mortgage / financing education. Audience tilt: high financial stakes, loss-averse, credibility-gated, often capital-rich and time-constrained.

## Retention curve archetypes

- **The case-study curve** — common for case-study-archivist real-estate channels (deal walkthroughs, BRRRR case studies). Strong opening, stable through the deal mechanics.
- **The cost-of-inaction climb** — common for personal-finance-mentor-archetype real-estate content. Loss-aversion-led; strong first half.
- **The pinned-VSL trough-and-recovery** — for high-ticket real-estate-coaching pinned VSLs.

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"How I bought [property] for $X"** — case-study; specific deal walkthrough.
- **"Why most [advice] about real estate is wrong"** — contrarian frame.
- **"The market is [up / down / shifting] — here's what to do"** — timely market commentary.
- **"How to analyze [property type] in [time]"** — tutorial / framework content.
- **"X mistakes new investors make"** — listicle.
- **"Real-estate vs [other investment]"** — contrast-format.
- **"From $0 to [portfolio size] in [time]"** — transformation / journey content.
- **Walkthroughs of actual deals (with permission / anonymization)** — case-study-archivist signature.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 25–55 typical primary; older skew than general business
- **Gender**: tilts male in active investing; more balanced in residential / first-time-buyer
- **Geography**: US heavily dominant (real-estate is highly geography-specific); UK / Australia / Canada secondary
- **Income**: typically middle-class to high-income; capital-rich often
- **Education**: variable; many self-made operators alongside MBAs

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in real estate:

- **View-to-application** (high-ticket coaching / mastermind): 0.5–2% on pinned VSL
- **Application-to-call**: 35–50%
- **Call show rate**: 65–75%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 25–40% on coaching offers ($3K–$15K typical price points)

LTV per email subscriber: typically $100–$300 in real estate with a $5K–$10K coaching offer.

## Regulatory considerations

Real estate is regulated:

- **License-specific advice**: real-estate-broker / agent licensing varies by jurisdiction; investment-advice in real-estate-securities (REITs, syndications) is SEC-regulated
- **Earnings / appreciation claims**: substantiated only; no guaranteed-returns framing
- **Affiliate disclosures**: INV-15 + real-estate-specific FTC scrutiny
- **Securities claims**: SEC scrutiny on syndication / fund-promotion content

## Banned content patterns

- Specific deal recommendations without proper licensing (in some jurisdictions)
- Past-performance-as-future-return framing
- Unsubstantiated cash-flow / appreciation claims
- "Make $10K/mo in real estate" — over-promising
- Affiliate-link / referral-fee disclosures missed (INV-15)

## Calibration note

Real estate is geography-sensitive. A channel calibrated for US-residential investing has different benchmarks than one calibrated for UK-buy-to-let or Australian-property-investing. The bands documented here are weighted toward US-residential as the platform's largest sub-niche; recalibrate for the channel's specific geography.
