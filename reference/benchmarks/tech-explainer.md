# Tech Explainer — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in tech-explainer YouTube channels (programming, AI / ML, dev tools, software architecture, B2B-SaaS analysis). Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time.

## Niche scope

Tech explainer includes: programming education, AI / ML explanation, dev-tools tutorials, software-architecture analysis, B2B-SaaS comparisons, infrastructure deep-dives, computer-science-applied content. Audience tilt: technical, analytical, pattern-matching, tolerates higher cognitive load per minute, often watches at 1.5–2x playback speed.

## CTR ranges by length-band

Long-form (10–30 min):
- Floor band: 3–4%
- Typical band: 4–6%
- Upper band: 6–9%
- Outlier: 9%+

CTR is structurally lower than general niches because the audience is narrower and the topics are specialized. The trade-off: the audience that does click is high-quality.

Short-long (4–8 min):
- Floor: 4–5%
- Typical: 5–7%
- Upper: 7–10%

Shorts:
- Typical: 5–10%
- Upper: 10–18%

## AVD ranges by length-band

10-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 45%
- Typical APV: 50–60%
- Upper APV: 62%+

20-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 40%
- Typical APV: 45–55%
- Upper APV: 58%+

30-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 35%
- Typical APV: 40–48%
- Upper APV: 50%+

The analytical-explainer's "clean staircase" retention curve is dominant in this niche. Information density holds the viewer; the staircase is steady but high.

## RPM bands

Tech explainer is mid-to-high CPM:

- Floor: $10
- Typical: $15–$30
- Upper: $30–$50
- Outlier: $50+ — typically when audience composition is enterprise-software / B2B-developer

Sub-niche variation:
- General programming / dev: $15–$25 RPM
- AI / ML: $20–$40 RPM (higher in 2025–2026 as the AI sponsor market grew)
- DevOps / infrastructure: $20–$35 RPM
- B2B-SaaS analysis: $25–$50 RPM
- Beginner / general-audience tech: $8–$18 RPM

## Sponsor CPM bands

Sponsor CPM in tech explainer:

- Floor: $25 per CPM
- Typical: $40–$80 per CPM
- Upper: $80–$150 per CPM

Common sponsor categories: dev tools, hosting platforms, cloud providers, AI tools, learning platforms, code-quality / observability tools, B2B-SaaS products, technical-recruiting platforms.

## Retention curve archetypes

- **The clean staircase** — dominant. Steady decline; high info-density; analytical-explainer pattern.
- **The screen-recording slow burn** — for tutorial / productivity-systems-builder-style tech content. Steeper early drop, then flat.
- **The diagram-build curve** — for explainer videos where a diagram is built over the video. AVD spikes at each diagram-element addition.

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"How [technology] works" deep-dives** — evergreen; analytical-explainer signature.
- **"Why [popular framework / tool] is [structural takedown]"** — contrarian frame in this niche.
- **"X explained in Y minutes"** — concise educational content.
- **"How to [build / deploy / architect / design] [thing]"** — tutorial; search-traffic-strong.
- **"[Tech A] vs [Tech B]"** — comparison content; contrast-format.
- **"My [tech] setup / stack / workflow"** — productivity-systems-builder overlap.
- **"What [tech] looks like inside a [company-type]"** — behind-the-scenes; insider-access positioning.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 18–45 typical primary; tech audiences are younger-skewed
- **Gender**: tilts male significantly in many sub-niches; some sub-niches (UX / design / general-tech) more balanced
- **Geography**: US / Western Europe / India / Eastern Europe / Latin America significantly represented; the niche is more globally distributed than most
- **Income**: variable; senior developers are high-income; students and bootcamp-grads lower
- **Education**: STEM-heavy; many self-taught alongside formally-credentialed

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in tech (consulting, course, mentorship):

- **View-to-application**: 0.5–2% on pinned VSL
- **Application-to-call**: 35–55%
- **Call show rate**: 65–75%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 20–35%

Tech audiences convert at slightly lower rates than business audiences because the offers are typically lower-touch (course / SaaS / consulting hours rather than mastermind / mentorship). LTV per email subscriber: $50–$200 typical; can reach $500+ for B2B-consulting or specialized course offerings.

## Regulatory considerations

Lower regulatory burden:

- **Earnings claims**: substantiated only ("our students average $X" with data)
- **Performance claims about tools**: substantiated by sponsor's data when sponsored
- **AI / ML claims**: as the field's regulatory environment evolves (2025–2026), evolving compliance considerations may emerge

## Banned content patterns

- "This will make you a [job-title] in 30 days" — over-promising
- Recycled tutorial content with stale tool versions and no update notes
- Fake performance benchmarks
- Affiliate-link disclosures missed (INV-15)

## Calibration note

Tech explainer is a long-tail niche. The channel's revenue is heavily weighted to the long tail — videos that gradually accumulate views over years rather than spike in week 1. Calibration improves as the library grows and the channel's evergreen videos compound. New channels in the floor band; libraries with 100+ videos and 3+ years of seasoning typically reach typical or upper band.
