# Business Education — Niche Benchmarks

> Typical structural performance bands observed in business-education YouTube channels (entrepreneurship, agency operations, B2B sales, info-product mentorship, consulting). Bands are calibrated per-channel from real data over time.

## Niche scope

Business education includes: entrepreneurship, agency-acquisition / agency-operations, B2B sales, consulting, info-product mentorship, business-systems coaching, growth strategy, marketing-as-business-education. Audience tilt: financially capable (operators with revenue), sophisticated (have bought info products before), skeptical (have been burned by recycled programs), high willingness-to-invest in mentorship.

## CTR ranges by length-band

Long-form (8–25 min):
- Floor band: 3.5–5%
- Typical band: 5–7%
- Upper band: 7–10%
- Outlier (above-band): 10%+

Short-long (4–8 min):
- Floor: 4–5%
- Typical: 5–8%
- Upper: 8–12%

Shorts:
- Typical: 6–12%
- Upper: 12–20%

CTR is structurally lower than entertainment niches because the audience is narrower. Don't expect entertainment-CTR; the lower CTR is offset by higher CPM and higher conversion.

## AVD ranges by length-band

10-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 42%
- Typical APV: 48–55%
- Upper APV: 58%+

20-minute videos:
- Floor APV: 38%
- Typical APV: 42–50%
- Upper APV: 53%+

Pinned VSL (35–60 minutes):
- Floor APV: 28%
- Typical APV: 33–40%
- Upper APV: 43%+

## RPM bands

Business education is the highest-CPM general niche. Typical bands:

- Floor: $20
- Typical: $30–$50
- Upper: $50–$80
- Outlier: $80+ — typically channels with high-ticket B2B audience and advertiser pool of SaaS / B2B services

Sub-niche variation:
- General entrepreneurship: $25–$45 RPM
- Agency operations / acquisition: $35–$60 RPM
- B2B sales: $40–$70 RPM
- Consulting: $30–$55 RPM
- Info-product mentorship / creator-economy-business: $25–$45 RPM

## Sponsor CPM bands

Sponsor CPM typically tracks 2x–3x platform RPM in business education:

- Floor: $40 per CPM
- Typical: $60–$100 per CPM
- Upper: $100–$180 per CPM

Common sponsor categories: SaaS for business operators, business-services tools (CRM, project management, accounting), mentorship platforms, course-hosting platforms, agency-tools, B2B-software.

## Retention curve archetypes

- **The objection-reversal staircase** — common for contrarian-narrator-style business content. AVD steps up at each objection-reversal.
- **The case-study-curve** — common for case-study-archivist business channels. Strong opening retention (social-proof-stack hook works), stable through the case body, slight drop at the lesson-extraction.
- **The pinned-VSL trough-and-recovery** — for pinned VSLs. Drop at price-anchoring, recovery through the close.

## Recurring topic categories that perform

- **"How to scale [business] from $X to $Y"** — stage-aware content; agency-acquisition operator archetype's bread-and-butter.
- **"Why most [business] [advice] is wrong"** — contrarian-narrator pattern; reach-strong.
- **"X frameworks every [audience] should know"** — listicle / framework-introduction; high engagement.
- **"How I built [thing] in [time]"** — case-study; build-in-public; story-driven-creator overlap.
- **"[Operator] vs [operator] — what's the structural difference"** — contrast-format; reach-strong (anonymized as archetype-vs-archetype).
- **"The X-Y-Z method explained"** — mechanism-first content; mechanism-first-educator pattern.
- **Behind-the-scenes business operations** — productivity-systems-builder overlap.

## Audience composition characteristics

- **Age**: 25–50 typical primary
- **Gender**: tilts male in some sub-niches (agency, B2B); more balanced in creator-economy-business
- **Geography**: US dominant; UK / Australia / Canada secondary; high engagement from Singapore / Dubai / Eastern Europe in some sub-niches
- **Income / scale**: typically operators with revenue ($10K/mo–$500K/mo MRR for the agency niche)
- **Education**: variable; the niche includes self-taught operators and credentialed MBAs alike

## Conversion benchmarks

For channels with a back-end offer in business education:

- **View-to-application**: 1–3% mature on pinned VSL
- **Application-to-call**: 35–55%
- **Call show rate**: 65–75%
- **Call-to-close rate**: 25–40% on $3K–5K offers; 20–30% on $5K–10K; 15–25% on $10K+

LTV per email subscriber: typically $150–$500 in business education with a $5K offer; can reach $1,000+ in agency-acquisition or B2B-coaching niches with offers in the $10K–$30K range.

## Regulatory considerations

Lower regulatory burden than personal finance, but:

- **FTC**: #ad disclosure on sponsored / affiliate content; income-claim substantiation required
- **Earnings claims**: substantiated only ("our average client" with documented data); never guaranteed; never inflated
- **Testimonials**: anonymized properly; verifiable; not fabricated (INV-5, INV-6)

## Banned content patterns

- "Make $10K/mo guaranteed" or any guaranteed-earnings framing
- Income claims without substantiation
- Recycled program-style content (engagement-bait, fake-urgency, manufactured-scarcity)
- Affiliate-link disclosures missed (INV-15)

## Calibration note

Business education benchmarks recalibrate per-channel; the niche's competitive density and the platform's algorithm both shift over time. New channels in 2026 starting in the niche typically land in the floor band; mature channels with strong differentiation in the typical band; flagship channels in the upper band.
