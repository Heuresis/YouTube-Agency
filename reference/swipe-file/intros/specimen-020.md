# Intro Specimen 020 — Operator-credibility intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Re-anchor the hook's promise. "I want to spend [N seconds] making sure you know I'm not just speculating — what I'll show you is what I'm actively running."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Specific operator credentials. "I've been a creator for [N years], built [generic descriptor of business] off it, and I've worked with [N clients] to do the same."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Audience callout. "This is for creators who already have audience but aren't yet making the income that audience implies."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Roadmap. "Three parts. The structural problem, the monetization mechanism, and the build-out."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:40): Pre-handle the "I don't have a big enough audience" objection. "Audience size is not the bottleneck. I'll show you the math."
- Beat 6 (1:40–1:50): End-tease. "Stick to the end — I'll share the exact [funnel / dashboard / spreadsheet]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Operator-credibility intros work in creator-economy niches because the audience is sophisticated and skeptical of theory-only educators. Beat 1's "what I'll show you is what I'm actively running" framing is the high-leverage credibility-anchor. The audience-size pre-handle (Beat 5) is critical because it's the universal first objection from sub-100K creators.
