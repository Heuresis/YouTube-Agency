# Intro Specimen 004 — Comparison-format reduce-defensiveness intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Acknowledge both sides of the comparison. "[Both options] are real. This isn't about saying [X] is bad."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Operator's specific result with the recommended option. "I've generated over [specific revenue] with [recommended option] in the last [timeframe], working just [low-hour-investment] hours per week."
- Beat 3 (40–60s): Promise the analytical decomposition. "Today I want to show you exactly why [recommended option] outperforms [alternative] across [N] meaningful metrics."
- Beat 4 (60–80s): Time-commitment statement framed as ROI. "Pay attention because this video may just be the difference between you making [low result] or [high result] per month."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The comparison-intro template reduces audience defensiveness via Beat 1's "both options are real" framing. Without that frame, audiences who are already engaged with the alternative will defend their choice and disengage. Beat 2's specific personal result establishes the operator's standing to make the comparison.

## When to use
Comparison-format videos. Especially effective in adjacent-niche overlaps where the audience is choosing between two legitimate paths.
