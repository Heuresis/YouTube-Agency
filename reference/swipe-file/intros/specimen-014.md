# Intro Specimen 014 — Charisma-first reach intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:20–0:35): Continue the hook's energy with no decompression. "Now what I'm about to show you, I've never broken down on YouTube before, and most of my paying clients have only seen this in private."
- Beat 2 (0:35–0:50): Single-line credential — no list. "I run [generic descriptor: the agency / the business / the operation] and we hit [specific outcome] last year."
- Beat 3 (0:50–1:05): Audience callout with personal touch. "If you're trying to [outcome] and feel like nothing's working, I get it. I was you [N years ago]. The mechanism I'll show you is the one that broke me out of it."
- Beat 4 (1:05–1:20): Roadmap kept tight. "Three parts. Mechanism, the worked example, and how to apply this week."
- Beat 5 (1:20–1:30): End-tease. "Stick around because at the end I'll show you the exact [artifact: doc / template / dashboard]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Charisma-first reach intros maintain hook energy through the intro window — the audience is already engaged and decompressing into a credential-stack would lose them. Beat 1's "never broken down on YouTube before" framing creates artificial scarcity that elevates perceived value. The personal-touch callout (Beat 3) is the trust-anchor — "I was you N years ago" outperforms credentials in reach-mode content.
