# Intro Specimen 022 — Framework-introduction intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Name the framework explicitly with a memorable handle. "What I'm about to show you, I call [Framework Name]. I'll explain why the name matters in a moment."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Authority through framework-application history. "I've used this with [N+ clients / scenarios] across [N+ years]. It's not theory."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Promise the structural advantage. "The reason this works when other approaches don't is because it operates on [meta-level distinction]."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Roadmap. "We'll cover the four components, the failure modes, and a worked example."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:40): Set the rigor expectation. "This is going to be more conceptually demanding than most YouTube content. Stay with me — the payoff is at the synthesis."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Framework-introduction intros earn senior-audience retention by raising the conceptual bar of the video — Beat 5's "more demanding than most YouTube content" framing is a reverse-bid that filters for serious viewers. Naming the framework with a specific handle (Beat 1) makes the content quotable and shareable, which has compounding distribution effects. The "operates on a meta-level" framing (Beat 3) signals depth.
