# Intro Specimen 025 — Contrast-format intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:20–0:35): Re-anchor the contrast from the hook. "I'm going to walk you through both sides — what I tried first, what I tried second, and what the difference actually was."
- Beat 2 (0:35–0:50): Set the comparison rules. "I'll show you the inputs, the outputs, and the variables I controlled for. This isn't anecdotal."
- Beat 3 (0:50–1:05): Audience callout. "If you're trying to figure out which approach to take with [topic], this video is the head-to-head you've been looking for."
- Beat 4 (1:05–1:20): Roadmap. "Side A. Side B. Comparison. Recommendation."
- Beat 5 (1:20–1:30): End-tease. "And at the end I'll share what I'd do if I were starting from scratch tomorrow."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Contrast-format intros earn retention because the comparison structure is itself the value proposition — viewers came for the head-to-head and the video confirms they're getting it. Beat 2's "this isn't anecdotal" framing pre-empts skepticism. The "what I'd do starting from scratch tomorrow" tease (Beat 5) is the load-bearing end-anchor; it converts the comparison into actionable advice.
