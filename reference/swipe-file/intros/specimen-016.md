# Intro Specimen 016 — Technical-authority intro stack

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:30–0:45): Re-state the technical claim with a specific stake. "What I said in the hook isn't a hot take — it's based on [N years] of building [specific stack / system] in production."
- Beat 2 (0:45–1:00): Demonstrate technical authority through specificity. "I've shipped [specific outcome] across [N+ implementations / clients]."
- Beat 3 (1:00–1:15): Audience callout. "This is for [specific role / function]. If you're [other role], the framing won't apply."
- Beat 4 (1:15–1:30): Roadmap with technical-detail signaling. "We'll cover the architecture, the failure modes, the migration path, and the cost model."
- Beat 5 (1:30–1:40): Pre-handle the "but my context is different" objection. "The framework adapts. The principles don't change."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Technical-authority intros earn retention by signaling depth in the first 90 seconds — the senior-engineer audience filters out generic content quickly. The "shipped X across N implementations" framing (Beat 2) outperforms title-based credentials because senior viewers care about scope of practice, not formal authority. Pre-handling the contextual-difference objection (Beat 5) is high-leverage because that's the first reflexive resistance from technical audiences.
