# Intro Specimen 019 — Identity-pivot intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Re-anchor the identity flag from the hook. "If the hook resonated, you already know what this video is about — but I want to be more specific so you know if it's worth your time."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Shared-context authority. "I'm not a productivity guru. I'm someone who used to be exactly where you are, found a way out, and have been refining it for [N years]."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Tribal callout. "If you're someone who reads, takes notes, tries new systems, and still feels behind — this is the video I wish I'd had."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Structured roadmap. "We'll cover the diagnosis, the underlying mechanism, the system change, and the failure modes."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:35): End-tease. "At the end I'll share the exact [tool / template / setup] I use myself."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Identity-pivot intros earn loyalty because the creator's stance ("not a productivity guru") inverts the dominant authority pattern in the niche, which the audience has been trained to distrust. Beat 3's tribal callout is precisely-targeted — the people who read, take notes, and try systems are the highest-LTV viewers in the productivity niche. The "video I wish I'd had" framing is a powerful empathy-anchor.
