# Intro Specimen 008 — Case-study front-loaded intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Expand the hook by introducing the specific student / case. "[Student type] came to me [N months/years] ago in [specific vulnerable starting state]."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Establish the timeline of transformation. "Over the next [specific timeframe], they [specific transformation milestones]."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Specific present outcome. "Today, [student type] is [specific impressive present state]."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): Generalize to the audience. "And this isn't a one-off. I've helped [N students] do similar things — [aggregate result]."
- Beat 5 (75–90s): Promise the structural decomposition. "In this video, I'll show you exactly the playbook we used — step by step — so you can apply it yourself."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The case-study intro front-loads social proof. The structural rule: the case must be specific enough to feel real (named student type, specific starting state, specific outcome) without being so specific it's not generalizable. Beat 4's "and this isn't a one-off" preempts the "but that was just luck" objection.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos where the operator's offer is education / coaching with documented student outcomes. Pairs naturally with the 15-step VSL spine where the case study is the hero story (Step 5).
