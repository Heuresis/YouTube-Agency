# Intro Specimen 027 — Soft-CTA at 90s placement intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:30–0:45): Re-anchor the hook with one new piece of info.
- Beat 2 (0:45–1:00): Authority anchor in one sentence.
- Beat 3 (1:00–1:15): Audience callout.
- Beat 4 (1:15–1:30): Roadmap. Three to four parts.
- Beat 5 (1:30–1:50): Soft CTA at the 90-second mark. "Before I go further — if you find this useful, the [free resource: PDF / community / first lesson of the course] is linked below. No email required for the [base tier]." No hard pitch.
- Beat 6 (1:50–2:00): Re-anchor and bridge to body. "OK — back to it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The 90-second soft-CTA placement is the highest-converting CTA position in long-form because the audience has just consumed the intro's value but hasn't yet hit the body's commitment threshold — they're warm and curious. Beat 5's "no email required for the base tier" is the load-bearing trust-element; it lowers friction on the opt-in. The "OK — back to it" bridge (Beat 6) prevents the CTA from breaking video momentum.
