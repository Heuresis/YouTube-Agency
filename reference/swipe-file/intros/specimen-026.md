# Intro Specimen 026 — Depth-promise intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:30–0:50): Set the depth expectation. "What I'll cover in the next [N] minutes goes deeper than the [N+1]-minute summaries you've seen on this. If you've watched those, this will feel different."
- Beat 2 (0:50–1:05): Authority through specificity. "I've spent [N hours / years] working through this and I've made every mistake worth making."
- Beat 3 (1:05–1:20): Audience callout. "If you're someone who reads the documentation rather than the cheat-sheet, you'll like the way this is structured."
- Beat 4 (1:20–1:40): Roadmap. "We'll go through the principles, the mechanisms, the failure modes, and the worked example. The order matters — don't skip ahead."
- Beat 5 (1:40–1:50): End-tease. "And at the end I'll share the [specific deep-dive resource] for people who want to go further."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Depth-promise intros earn long-form retention by explicitly contrasting against summary-style content — viewers who came for depth feel rewarded immediately. Beat 3's "documentation rather than cheat-sheet" callout is precisely-targeted at the high-LTV audience segment. The "don't skip ahead" framing (Beat 4) is structurally important — it signals the video has a load-bearing argument arc rather than independent tactics.
