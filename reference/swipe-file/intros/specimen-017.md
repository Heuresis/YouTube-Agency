# Intro Specimen 017 — Transformation-anchored reach intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Continue from hook with a personal-stake anchor. "I'm going to walk you through exactly what changed for me, and I'm not going to skip the boring parts because that's where it actually happened."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Single proof-point. "[Specific metric: pounds, body fat %, lift number] in [timeframe]. Sustained for [N+ years]."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Audience callout with explicit empathy. "If you've been training hard and seeing nothing change, this is going to feel familiar — and the answer is going to surprise you."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Three-part roadmap. "Three sections. The mistake almost everyone makes, the change I made, and how to set it up this week."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:35): End-tease. "At the end I'll share the [exact program / split / nutrition outline]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Transformation-anchored reach intros work because the proof is the credential — viewers don't need credentials when they have evidence. Beat 1's "not going to skip the boring parts" framing earns trust by signaling intellectual honesty, which is rare in fitness-influencer content. The "answer is going to surprise you" tease (Beat 3) keeps the loop open without overpromising.
