# Intro Specimen 010 — Reactive industry-shift intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Acknowledge the recent industry change. "[Specific named platform / algorithm / tool change] just happened. Most people don't understand what this means for [target audience's outcome]."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): State the operator's standing to comment. "I've been [activity] since before [the change], and I've already adapted my [strategy / system] to the new environment."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Tease the structural decomposition. "In this video, I'll show you (1) exactly what changed, (2) why the old approach is now broken, and (3) the specific adjustments to make right now."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): Time-sensitivity emphasis. "If you're [activity], you have [short timeframe] to adapt before [negative outcome]. I want to make sure you don't miss the window."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Reactive-content intros must front-load both the change-event (Beat 1) and the time-sensitivity (Beat 4). Beat 2's "I've already adapted" preempts the "you don't know either, you're just speculating" objection. The structural rule: the operator must produce this video within 24–72 hours of the change-event for maximum CTR.

## When to use
Reactive-content slot only. Loses effectiveness rapidly as the change-event recedes from audience awareness.
