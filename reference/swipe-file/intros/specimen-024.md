# Intro Specimen 024 — Opinion-led intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Re-state the contrarian opinion plainly. "I'm going to give you my honest opinion on [topic]. I'm not going to balance it. I'm not going to weasel-word it."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Authority through scope. "I've been operating in this space for [N years] and the take I'm about to share is the one I've come around to after [N reps / iterations]."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Audience callout. "If you want neutral consensus, this isn't for you. If you want one operator's high-conviction view, stick around."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Roadmap. "I'll lay out the position, the evidence, the counter-arguments, and my response to them."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:35): Pre-handle the "you're being arrogant" reaction. "I might be wrong. I'm telling you what I think and why. Disagree freely."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Opinion-led intros earn senior-audience retention because the audience is fatigued by hedge-everything content — strong views are cognitively refreshing. Beat 1's explicit "not going to weasel-word it" framing is the trust-anchor; it signals editorial integrity. The "I might be wrong" pre-empt (Beat 5) protects against backlash and paradoxically increases credibility.
