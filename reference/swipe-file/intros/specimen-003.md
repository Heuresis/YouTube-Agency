# Intro Specimen 003 — 90-second CTA insertion intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–60s): Standard 6-component intro stack (intro specimens 001 / 002 above).
- Beat 2 (60–75s): Casual, framed-as-resource CTA insertion. "Quick note — if you're serious about [goal], check out our [free resource] at [link in description]. Now, back to the strategy..."
- Beat 3 (75–90s): Resume content roadmap.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The 90-second CTA placement is counterintuitive: most creators bury the CTA at the end of the video, where ~30% of viewers see it. The 90-second placement reaches ~100% of viewers. The structural rule: the CTA must be casual, framed as a resource (not a pitch), and re-pivot back into content within 15 seconds. Long CTAs at the 90-second mark kill retention; short, casual ones don't.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos with a clear lead-magnet or VSL endpoint. Less suitable for pure-reach videos where the audience is brand-new and the offer is too far ahead in the funnel.
