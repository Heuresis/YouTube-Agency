# Intro Specimen 013 — Data-credibility intro stack

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:15–0:25): Expand the hook by previewing the first data point. "Before I get into the framework, let me show you the data that started this."
- Beat 2 (0:25–0:38): Authority through evidence trail. "I've been tracking [N+ datasets] from [source] for the past [N years]. The pattern we're going to talk about is statistically robust."
- Beat 3 (0:38–0:55): Audience callout with disqualifier. "If you're someone who treats personal finance as a hobby, this isn't for you. If you treat it as a system to optimize, you'll find this useful."
- Beat 4 (0:55–1:10): Roadmap. "We'll cover [section 1], [section 2], [section 3], and at the end I'll show you how to set this up in [single tool]."
- Beat 5 (1:10–1:25): Pre-handle the "but I don't make enough" objection. "This works at any income level — only the inputs change."
- Beat 6 (1:25–1:30): Tease the end. "And at the end I'll share the spreadsheet I use to run this myself."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The data-credibility intro front-loads source-citation as authority, which is the high-leverage move in personal-finance niches where audiences are skeptical of guru-bait. The disqualifier in Beat 3 paradoxically increases retention by raising perceived seriousness. The income-level pre-handle (Beat 5) is the load-bearing objection-clearing move; without it, lower-earning viewers drop off when they assume the content isn't for them.
