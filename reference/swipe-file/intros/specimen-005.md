# Intro Specimen 005 — Long-form blueprint / free-course intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): State the comprehensiveness of the video. "This is going to be a complete A-Z blueprint for [outcome] in [year], even if you're starting from scratch with [zero starting condition]."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Inventory the video's coverage. "This free course will cover everything: [item 1], [item 2], [item 3], and [item 4]."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Position the operator as the audience-from-the-future. "I'm giving you the exact strategies I wish I had when I started with [vulnerable starting state]."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): Direct retention command. "So grab a notepad, take good notes, and you might want to rewatch this a few times to catch everything."
- Beat 5 (75–90s): Final tease before content begins. "Let's get into it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The long-form-blueprint intro positions the video itself as a complete artifact (a "free course," "complete blueprint"). The structural rule: Beat 2's coverage inventory must accurately represent the body — promising 4 modules and delivering 2 produces audience backlash. Beat 4's "rewatch this" command is the load-bearing element that pre-frames the audience for high-intensity engagement.

## When to use
Long-form (45-minute+) educational videos where the operator wants to maximize watch-time and downstream conversion. Less suitable for shorter videos where the "complete" framing is overpromising.
