# Intro Specimen 007 — Listicle / countdown intro with #1-tease

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): State why the ranking matters. "Most people don't realize that [topic] has [N] critical levels — and the difference between [low] and [high] is what separates [outcome A] from [outcome B]."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Establish criteria for ranking. "I'm ranking these by [criterion 1] + [criterion 2] + [criterion 3]."
- Beat 3 (40–60s): Tease #1 specifically. "And the #1 [item] is one most people don't even know exists — but once you see it, you'll wonder how you've been operating without it."
- Beat 4 (60–80s): Final tease. "Stay until the end because the #1 reveal is the most counterintuitive thing I've ever shared on this channel."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The listicle-intro must front-load the #1-tease (Beat 3) to keep the audience watching through items 5–2. Without the #1 tease, audiences pattern-match the list as "5 generic items" and disengage by item 3. The criteria in Beat 2 establishes legitimacy — without it, the list reads as arbitrary opinion.

## When to use
Listicle / countdown / ranking videos. Less effective for non-ordinal lists ("5 things to know") because there's no #1 to tease.
