# Intro Specimen 021 — Documentary chapter-tease intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:30–0:50): Voice-over over cinematic B-roll. "This is a story about [specific subject], but the reason I'm telling it isn't what you think."
- Beat 2 (0:50–1:10): Tease the meta-question. "By the end you'll see how this connects to [larger pattern], and once you see it, the way you think about [domain] will change."
- Beat 3 (1:10–1:30): Chapter preview. On-screen text or graphic listing the chapters. "Chapter 1: the origin. Chapter 2: the breakthrough. Chapter 3: what came after. Chapter 4: what it means for you."
- Beat 4 (1:30–1:50): Set the stakes. "I traveled [where] / interviewed [generic descriptor] / spent [N months] on this. The investment is in the depth."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Documentary chapter-tease intros earn long-form retention by signaling structure and effort upfront — the viewer commits to the arc rather than evaluating each minute individually. Beat 2's "the reason I'm telling it isn't what you think" framing is a classic open-loop that promises intellectual payoff. The chapter graphic (Beat 3) is the load-bearing structural element; without it, the documentary feels meandering.
