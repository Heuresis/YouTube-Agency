# Intro Specimen 023 — Vulnerability-led intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Acknowledge the discomfort of the topic. "I've been putting off making this video for [N months]. Talking about [topic] is uncomfortable for me, and I want you to know that going in."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Single specific credential, framed as lived experience. "I've been on the other side of [problem] for [N years]. The framework I'll share is the one I had to build for myself."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Audience callout with empathy. "If you're going through [problem] right now, I want this video to actually help — not be entertainment."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Roadmap kept simple. "Three sections. What I learned. What changed. What you can apply."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:35): Tone-setter. "I'm going to share things I haven't shared publicly. If that feels too personal, no judgment — but stick around if you can."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Vulnerability-led intros earn unusually deep audience trust because the creator forfeits the easy authority pose. Beat 1's "putting off making this video" framing converts the runtime into a deliberate, considered act, which the audience values. The "actually help, not be entertainment" framing (Beat 3) is the load-bearing tone-setter; it signals the rest of the video will be substantive.
