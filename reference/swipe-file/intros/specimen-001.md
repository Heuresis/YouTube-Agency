# Intro Specimen 001 — 6-component standard intro stack

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Expand the hook with more context.
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Authority establishment in 2–3 lines. "My name is [name]. I've [credential 1] for [time period]. My team [credential 2] and now we [current venture] which [specific result]."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Audience callout. "If you're [target characteristic], and you're struggling with [specific problem], in this video I'll reveal [specific solution] by showing you [specific method]."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): Content roadmap. Broad outline of what video covers; create expectation of value.
- Beat 5 (75–85s): Pre-handle 1–2 common objections. "You can start with [low requirement]. You don't need [common assumed barrier]."
- Beat 6 (85–90s): End-of-video tease. "Stay until the end because I'll share [specific bonus / insight / template]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The 6-component intro stack respects the 90-second window where retention is highest. Each component installs a different belief: authority → audience callout → content roadmap → objection pre-handle → end-tease. Skipping any component produces a predictable failure — e.g., skipping the objection pre-handle leads to mid-video drop-off when the assumed barrier surfaces.

## When to use
Every long-form (8 minute+) video. The 90-second window is the load-bearing slot for both authority establishment and the 90-second CTA placement (intro specimen 003).
