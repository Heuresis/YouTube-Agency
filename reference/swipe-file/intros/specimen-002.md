# Intro Specimen 002 — 6-beat case-study setup intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Expand on hook. Acknowledge the surprise / counterintuition embedded in the hook.
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Self-deprecating relatability. "I'm just a regular [generic descriptor] who figured this out through pure trial and error."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Promise of structural decomposition. "Today I'm breaking down exactly how I did it."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): Name the artifact / framework. "By the end of this video, you'll understand the [framework name] — the [N]-phase strategy that [outcome]."
- Beat 5 (75–85s): Universal applicability claim. "It works whether you have [low end of audience] or [high end of audience]."
- Beat 6 (85–90s): Specific rapid-result proof. "And it delivers fast — my last [event] using this strategy? [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This intro template is structurally a 6-beat case-study setup. Beat 2's "regular [generic descriptor]" is the relatability pivot; without it, the operator reads as guru and the audience disengages. Beat 4 names the artifact, which converts the video into a takeaway object. Beat 6's rapid-result proof is the credibility cue that justifies watching the rest.

## When to use
Reach-mode videos with a named framework / proprietary system. Most effective when the operator has a single peak-performance event (Beat 6) to anchor the credibility.
