# Intro Specimen 018 — Deal-citation authority intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:25–0:40): Re-anchor to the deal opened in the hook. "Let me give you the full picture of that deal so you understand what we're actually talking about."
- Beat 2 (0:40–0:55): Brief authority spec. "I've closed [N] deals across [N markets] over the last [N years]. I run [generic descriptor: a portfolio / a fund / a syndication]."
- Beat 3 (0:55–1:10): Audience callout. "If you're trying to break in or scale beyond [stage] and you're tired of cookie-cutter advice, this is for you."
- Beat 4 (1:10–1:25): Roadmap. "I'll show you the deal structure, the negotiation, the financing, and how to find these yourself."
- Beat 5 (1:25–1:40): Pre-handle the "this only works in your market" objection. "I'll show you how to identify analogues in your local market."
- Beat 6 (1:40–1:50): End-tease. "Stick to the end — I'll share the exact filter I use to source these."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Deal-citation intros are uniquely effective in real-estate niches because deals are concrete — the audience can validate them. The "tired of cookie-cutter advice" callout (Beat 3) flags the educated-investor audience that's filtering out generic content. Pre-handling the market-specific objection (Beat 5) is the load-bearing element; without it, viewers in other markets disengage.
