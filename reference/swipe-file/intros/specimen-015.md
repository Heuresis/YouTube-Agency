# Intro Specimen 015 — Philosophical-grounding intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:30–0:45): Re-anchor the hook to the philosophical frame. "Before we go anywhere, I want to ground this in a specific tradition because the rest of the video will only work if we share the same starting point."
- Beat 2 (0:45–1:00): Authority through study, not production. "I've spent [N years] inside this tradition — both reading the [primary sources] and applying it personally. I'll cite as we go."
- Beat 3 (1:00–1:15): Audience callout for thoughtful viewer. "If you're someone who finds most self-improvement content shallow, this is for you. If you want quick fixes, this won't work."
- Beat 4 (1:15–1:35): Roadmap. "We'll cover the principle, the mechanism, two applications, and the most common failure mode."
- Beat 5 (1:35–1:45): Tease the failure mode. "The failure mode is what I want you to remember most — and it's the part nobody covers."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Philosophical-grounding intros earn long-form retention because they explicitly raise the cognitive bar of the video — the audience self-selects to engage at depth. Beat 3's filtering disqualifier is a reverse-bid for attention; quick-fix viewers leave, serious viewers commit. Teasing the failure mode (Beat 5) is the structural innovation — most videos sell the success path, this one previews the failure path, which is more useful and more memorable.
