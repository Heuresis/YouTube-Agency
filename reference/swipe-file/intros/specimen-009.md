# Intro Specimen 009 — Operator-failure-as-audience-mirror intro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Set the vulnerable starting context. "Before I [achieved current state], I was making the same mistake every [target avatar] makes — trying to [false approach the audience also believes is correct]."
- Beat 2 (25–40s): Describe the failed loop. "I thought I needed to [false belief]. Every time I'd [attempt], I'd find out [setback]."
- Beat 3 (40–55s): Reveal the breakthrough moment. "That's when I found [mentor / book / quote / event] that completely flipped my perspective."
- Beat 4 (55–75s): The new framing as the structural takeaway. "This explains that [counter-framing] — and that overarching philosophy is the core of the [N] principles we're covering today."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This intro template uses the operator's failure as the audience's mirror. Beat 1's "every [target avatar] makes" generalizes the operator's failure to the audience's situation, creating identification. Beat 3's breakthrough moment must be specific (named mentor, named book, named quote) — generic "I had a realization" doesn't work.

## When to use
Storytelling-tilted reach videos, especially when the operator's transformation pivots on a specific external influence. Less effective for content built around the operator's solo discovery.
