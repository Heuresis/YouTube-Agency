# Intro Specimen 006 — Peak-event anchor intro with framework naming

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (15–25s): Counter-norm framing. "Most [operators / brands] spend an entire year trying to reach [specific revenue figure], or even worse never reach it."
- Beat 2 (25–35s): Operator's contrasting outcome. "But I was able to do it in [unbelievably-short timeframe] with my latest [specific event]."
- Beat 3 (35–55s): Promise structural decomposition + multi-step strategy. "In this video, I'll not only show you exactly how I did it, but teach you a [N]-step strategy that I and all the [credentialed peer group] use to [outcome]."
- Beat 4 (55–80s): Name the framework. "It's called [framework name] and there are [N] parts to it: [step 1], [step 2], [step 3], [step 4]."
- Beat 5 (80–90s): Direct retention command tied to ICP. "If results like these are what you want to see in your business one day, lock in for the next [X] minutes."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This intro template anchors the entire video to a specific peak-performance event. Beat 4's framework-naming with steps creates the video's structural skeleton on screen — the audience now has a 4-bucket mental model they're filling in across the body. The structural rule: the steps named in Beat 4 must match the body's section structure; mismatched names produce confusion.

## When to use
When the operator has a specific peak-performance event (a launch, a sale, a single high-conversion moment) to anchor the narrative. Less suitable for steady-state revenue claims.
