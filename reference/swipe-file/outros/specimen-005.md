# Outro Specimen 005 — Three-options crossroads outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Recap the video's central thesis. "Look — at the end of the day, this all comes down to [central principle]. If you're not [taking specific action], you're not [achieving outcome]."
- Beat 2 (30–60s): Three explicit options. "You have three options."
- Beat 3 (60–90s): Option 1 — the path of inaction. "Option 1: You can click on the next video and never [achieve goal]."
- Beat 4 (90–120s): Option 2 — the DIY path. "Option 2: You can try this by yourself. You'll have to figure out everything on your own, spend hours [activity], waste money on [activity], and have no one to turn to when you hit roadblocks."
- Beat 5 (120–180s): Option 3 — the operator's offer. "Option 3: You can join [specific community / program]. You get [specific benefits]."
- Beat 6 (180–200s): Disqualifier. "But I want to make it clear — I do not work with everybody. If you don't want to take [outcome] seriously, do not join."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The option-presenting outro is the structural form of the Crossroads Close (15-step VSL spine, Step 12). Beats 3–5 force the audience into one of three paths; Beat 6's disqualifier converts the operator's offer into a perceived-scarce option. The structural rule: Option 1 (inaction) must be genuinely framed as the worst path, not a viable choice.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos at the bottom of funnel with a specific paid offer. Most effective when the operator's offer is high-ticket; less effective for low-ticket digital products.
