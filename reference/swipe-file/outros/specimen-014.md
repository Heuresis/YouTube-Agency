# Outro Specimen 014 — Documentary-resolution outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–60s): Voice-over reflection over cinematic B-roll. "When I started this story, I thought it was about [original frame]. By the end, I realized it was about [larger frame]."
- Beat 2 (60–105s): The lesson, distilled. "The thing I want to leave you with is [single principle]. It applies to [topic of video], but it's bigger than that — it applies to [larger domain]."
- Beat 3 (105–135s): Soft CTA tied to depth. "If you want to go deeper on this, [paid newsletter / book / community] is where I'm working through these ideas. Link below."
- Beat 4 (135–155s): Credits / acknowledgment. Often includes "thanks to the people who made this possible."
- Beat 5 (155–170s): Sign-off, often poetic. "Until next time."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Documentary-resolution outros earn long-term audience loyalty because they elevate the video from content to art — viewers feel they've been on a journey, which they remember. Beat 1's "I thought it was about X — it was actually about Y" framing is the load-bearing element; it converts the video from a topic-explainer to a thinking-out-loud, which is structurally more memorable. The credits beat (Beat 4) signals production effort that audiences reciprocate with attention.
