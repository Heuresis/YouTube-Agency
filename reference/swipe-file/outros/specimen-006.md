# Outro Specimen 006 — Future-pace transformation outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Recap the structural principles delivered. "We covered [principle 1], [principle 2], and [principle 3]."
- Beat 2 (30–90s): Future-pace the audience's outcome. "Imagine [specific timeframe — 6 months, 1 year] from now. You've [implemented these principles]. Your [specific outcome — channel, business, life] looks like [specific transformed state]. That's not a fantasy — it's the predictable result of [consistent action on these principles]."
- Beat 3 (90–120s): Light CTA. "If you want to make sure you stay on track, [specific subscribe / follow action]. I'll be sharing [specific upcoming content] that builds on this."
- Beat 4 (120–140s): Sign-off. "See you in the next one."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The future-pace outro uses "once not if" language to place the audience in the post-implementation future. Beat 2's specific transformed state must be vivid and specific — generic "you'll be successful" doesn't pace the future; specific "your channel will be at 50K subscribers, you'll have a backlog of qualified leads, you'll be making your offer" does.

## When to use
Reach-mode videos targeting audiences early in their journey. Especially effective when the operator wants to build long-term audience-relationship rather than convert immediately.
