# Outro Specimen 010 — Binge-loop soft-CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–20s): Brief recap. One sentence per major idea. "We covered [A], [B], and [C]."
- Beat 2 (20–40s): Soft CTA — low-friction, no high-stakes ask. "If you found this useful, the [free PDF / community / newsletter] is linked below. No email required for the basic version."
- Beat 3 (40–60s): Bridge to next video. "If you want to go deeper on [Topic A], I made [next video] which goes into [specific subset]. I'll link it on screen."
- Beat 4 (60–75s): On-screen end card. Two videos shown side by side — the natural next watch and a related deep-dive.
- Beat 5 (75–85s): Sign-off. "I'll see you there."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Binge-loop soft-CTA outros are the highest-watchtime-multiplier outro pattern because they maintain session momentum rather than asking for a hard action. Beat 2's "no email required for the basic version" framing is the load-bearing trust-element; it earns goodwill that compounds across the channel. The on-screen end card (Beat 4) is structurally critical — viewers click these at high rates if the cards are well-designed.
