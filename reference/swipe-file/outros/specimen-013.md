# Outro Specimen 013 — Cohort-launch CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Recap. "We covered [framework]. Most people will watch this and not act on it — that's fine. This outro is for the people who do want to act."
- Beat 2 (30–75s): Cohort overview. "I run a cohort [N times per year] called [name]. We work through [specific process] over [N weeks] together. Cohort [N] starts [date]."
- Beat 3 (75–105s): Outcome description. "By the end, members have [specific outcome A], [specific outcome B], and a [specific tangible asset]."
- Beat 4 (105–130s): Scarcity, honestly framed. "I cap the cohort at [N] members because [structural reason — not artificial scarcity]. When it fills, it fills."
- Beat 5 (130–155s): Application step. "If you're interested, the link below has the full breakdown. There's an application — not because I'm gatekeeping, but because the cohort works best when [specific condition]."
- Beat 6 (155–170s): Sign-off. "Thanks for watching."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Cohort-launch CTA outros earn conversion because cohorts are inherently scarcity-shaped (limited seats, fixed start dates) and the scarcity doesn't feel manufactured. Beat 4's "structural reason — not artificial scarcity" framing is the load-bearing trust-element; explicitly naming and rejecting fake scarcity earns credibility. The "application — not gatekeeping" framing (Beat 5) inverts the usual luxury-positioning into a fit-positioning move.
