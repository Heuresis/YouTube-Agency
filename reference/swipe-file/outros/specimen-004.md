# Outro Specimen 004 — Community-driven subscriber outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–20s): Genuine acknowledgment of the audience. "I had so much fun making this and researching for this video, and I hope my delivery reflects that."
- Beat 2 (20–60s): Direct address to the audience's situation. "Whether you're a beginner or someone who's tried and failed multiple times, take these principles and run with them — I promise it will change the way you view [topic]."
- Beat 3 (60–90s): Light CTA. "If you want to dive deeper, I put together [specific resource] — completely for free — that covers [specific scope]. Check that out, it'll pop up on screen."
- Beat 4 (90–120s): Channel-continuity sign-off. "Thanks for watching, see you in the next one."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The community-driven outro prioritizes channel relationship over conversion. Beat 1's genuine-engagement framing builds long-term audience trust; Beat 2's both-beginner-and-experienced address ensures no audience segment feels excluded. Less direct conversion than outro specimen 001, but stronger long-term audience retention.

## When to use
Reach-mode videos where the operator is building audience rather than converting. Pairs naturally with broad-appeal content where conversion is downstream.
