# Outro Specimen 012 — Community-CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–25s): Recap with shared-stake framing. "We covered [topic]. If this resonated, you're not alone — there are people working through this every day."
- Beat 2 (25–50s): Community introduction. "I run a [free community / Discord / forum] where [N+] people are working on [topic]. The link is in the description."
- Beat 3 (50–75s): What happens in the community. "We share [specific things] there. Recent threads include [examples]. It's the place where the real conversations happen."
- Beat 4 (75–90s): Low-friction join. "It's free. No application. Just click the link and you're in."
- Beat 5 (90–105s): Sign-off. "Looking forward to seeing you over there. Until then — see you in the next video."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Community-CTA outros work because community joins are low-friction asks that build long-term audience equity — viewers who join are pre-warmed for any future paid offer. Beat 3's "real conversations happen there" framing is the load-bearing element; it positions the community as the depth-tier the video can't provide. The "no application, just click" framing (Beat 4) maximizes opt-in rate.
