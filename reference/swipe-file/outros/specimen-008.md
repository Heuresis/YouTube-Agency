# Outro Specimen 008 — Cliff-hanger next-video outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–20s): Recap with specific cliff-hanger. "I covered [topics] in this video. But there's one thing I deliberately didn't cover: [specific topic]."
- Beat 2 (20–60s): Build curiosity for the next video. "[Specific topic] is actually the most important piece of all of this — without it, [previous topics] don't fully work."
- Beat 3 (60–90s): Direct redirect to the next video. "Click this video right here [visual cue]. I show you exactly how to [specific deliverable for next video]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The cliff-hanger outro deliberately withholds a key piece of the puzzle to drive next-video click. Beat 1's "deliberately didn't cover" is the load-bearing element — it converts a content gap from a flaw into a feature. The structural rule: the cliff-hanger topic must be genuinely critical to the previous video's content, not arbitrary.

## When to use
Reach-mode video series where the operator wants to build channel-binge behavior. Less effective for stand-alone videos that need to be self-sufficient.
