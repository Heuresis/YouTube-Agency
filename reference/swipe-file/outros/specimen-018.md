# Outro Specimen 018 — Assignment-CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–25s): Recap with action-orientation. "OK — you have the framework. Watching it isn't enough. Here's the specific assignment for this week."
- Beat 2 (25–55s): The assignment. "Before next [day / week], do these three things: [1, specific action]. [2, specific action]. [3, specific action]."
- Beat 3 (55–80s): Why each step matters. "Step 1 forces you to [specific cognitive shift]. Step 2 generates [specific evidence]. Step 3 [specific outcome]."
- Beat 4 (80–105s): Soft CTA tied to follow-up. "If you do all three and want feedback, drop a comment with [specific format]. I respond to ones that follow the format."
- Beat 5 (105–125s): Optional next-step pointer. "If you want a structured way to apply this beyond the assignment, [course / cohort / advisory] is where the next layer lives."
- Beat 6 (125–135s): Sign-off. "Do the work. See you next week."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Assignment-CTA outros earn unusual implementation rates because they convert passive viewers into active participants — the assignment is the bridge between watching and doing. Beat 4's "drop a comment with [specific format]" is the load-bearing engagement mechanism; the format requirement filters for serious viewers and creates social-proof artifacts in the comments. The "do the work" close (Beat 6) is uncompromising and earns respect.
