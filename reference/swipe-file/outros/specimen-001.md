# Outro Specimen 001 — Hard-CTA outro with disqualifier

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (Outro, 0–30s): Recap the video's value. "So that's [framework name] — the exact strategy I've used to [specific outcome]."
- Beat 2 (Reality check, 30–60s): Acknowledge the limit of the video. "But here's the reality — while this [strategy] is powerful, it's just one piece of [building the larger outcome]. There's still [specific other components], and trying to figure all of this out alone can be overwhelming."
- Beat 3 (Hard CTA, 60–120s): Specific offer + qualification. "Look, if you want to skip the painful trial and error and get direct help with ALL of these challenges, click the [specific link location]. You'll be connected to [specific community / coaching / training] where I'll [specific deliverable]."
- Beat 4 (Disqualifier, 120–140s): "But I want to make it clear I do not work with everybody. If you don't want to take [outcome] seriously, do not [click / apply / join]."
- Beat 5 (Sign-off, 140–150s): "Thank you for watching, I'll see you in the next one."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The hard-CTA outro structure includes the "do not work with everybody" disqualifier (Beat 4) — a counterintuitive psychological move that increases conversion by raising perceived exclusivity. The structural rule: Beat 2's "reality check" must be honest — overstating what the video left out makes the CTA feel manipulative.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos at the bottom of funnel where the operator has a specific high-ticket offer. Less suitable for top-of-funnel reach videos where the audience isn't yet ready for the offer.
