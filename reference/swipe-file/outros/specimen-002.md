# Outro Specimen 002 — Binge-loop next-video outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (Link back, 0–15s): "And what I love about this framework is it proves [validation of video content]."
- Beat 2 (New problem, 15–45s): "BUT here's the thing — [new related problem the audience now faces]. That's where most people struggle. [Specific friction in solving the new problem]."
- Beat 3 (Next-video promise, 45–90s): "How do you solve that? That's exactly what I cover in this video right here, where I show you [specific promise]. [Visual cue pointing to next video tile]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The binge-loop outro keeps viewers on the channel by linking the current video's content to a new related problem solved in the next video. Beat 2's "BUT here's the thing" is the load-bearing pivot — it converts the satisfaction of the current video into urgency for the next video.

## When to use
Every video that's part of a connected channel content series. The new-problem framing must be genuinely related to what was just delivered, not arbitrary cross-promotion.
