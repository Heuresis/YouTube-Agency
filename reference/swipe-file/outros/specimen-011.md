# Outro Specimen 011 — Lead-magnet CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Recap with implication. "So now you understand [framework]. The question is — how do you apply this without missing pieces?"
- Beat 2 (30–60s): Lead-magnet introduction. "I made a [specific artifact: cheat sheet / template / checklist] that gives you the version of this you can apply this week. It's free."
- Beat 3 (60–80s): Why the lead magnet exists. "I built this for [specific persona] because the framework only works if you have [the specific structural element the magnet provides]."
- Beat 4 (80–100s): Specific call-out for the link. "The link is in the description and pinned in the comments. Drop your email and you'll have it in your inbox in [N seconds / minutes]."
- Beat 5 (100–115s): Sign-off with continuity. "Thanks for watching. If you grab the [magnet], you'll also get my weekly email which goes deeper on [topic category]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Lead-magnet CTA outros work because the lead magnet itself extends the value of the video — the audience perceives the magnet as the missing piece rather than as a price for entry. Beat 3's "I built this for [persona] because..." framing is the load-bearing element; it positions the magnet as built for them rather than as marketing collateral. The "drop your email" plain-language ask (Beat 4) earns higher conversion than coy phrasing.
