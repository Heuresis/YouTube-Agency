# Outro Specimen 009 — VSL-style hard-CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Recap with stakes. "Let me recap what we covered. Now you understand [Concept A], [Concept B], and [Concept C]. The question is what you do with this."
- Beat 2 (30–60s): Two paths frame. "From here, you have two paths. Path one — try to figure out [the implementation] on your own. Path two — work with us directly."
- Beat 3 (60–120s): Path 2 specifics. "Path two is [specific offer]. We help [specific audience] do [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]. Here's what's included: [N specifics]."
- Beat 4 (120–150s): Disqualifier. "This isn't for everyone. It's not for [type A]. It's not for [type B]. It's specifically for [target type]."
- Beat 5 (150–180s): Application step. "If you think you might be a fit, click the link below. You'll fill out a short application. We'll either invite you to a call or tell you we're not the right fit."
- Beat 6 (180–195s): Sign-off. "Either way, thank you for watching. See you in the next one."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
VSL-style hard-CTA outros earn conversion by explicitly framing the path-fork — the audience must choose between status quo and action. The disqualifier (Beat 4) is the highest-leverage move; counterintuitively, naming who it's not for raises perceived exclusivity and increases application rate. The "we'll either invite you or tell you we're not the right fit" framing (Beat 5) earns trust by signaling the creator isn't desperate.
