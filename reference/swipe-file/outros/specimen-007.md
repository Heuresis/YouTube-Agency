# Outro Specimen 007 — Peer-validation case-study outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–30s): Reference specific student / peer outcomes. "I've taught this exact framework to [N students]. [Student type 1] made [specific result]. [Student type 2] made [specific result]. [Student type 3] made [specific result]."
- Beat 2 (30–60s): Generalize to the audience. "These aren't unicorns. These are people who started where you are right now."
- Beat 3 (60–90s): Direct CTA. "If you want the same outcome, [specific link / action]. If not, that's fine — but don't say I didn't show you the path."
- Beat 4 (90–120s): Sign-off. "Until next time."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The peer-validation outro stacks specific case studies as the conversion driver. Beat 2's "started where you are right now" preempts the "but those people are different" objection. Beat 3's "don't say I didn't show you the path" is a soft challenge that increases conversion among action-oriented viewers.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos where the operator has multiple specific student outcomes to reference. Most effective when the case studies span counter-stereotype demographics (older, younger, less-experienced, etc.).
