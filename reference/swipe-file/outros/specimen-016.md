# Outro Specimen 016 — Tiered-CTA outro

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–25s): Recap. "OK — we covered [framework]. The question is what level of help you want from here."
- Beat 2 (25–50s): Tier 1 — free. "If you want to keep learning for free, [free PDF / community / newsletter] is the next step. Link below."
- Beat 3 (50–80s): Tier 2 — mid-priced. "If you want a structured path, [course / cohort / membership] takes you through [specific outcome] for [price tier descriptor]. Link below."
- Beat 4 (80–115s): Tier 3 — high-touch. "If you want hands-on help with [specific problem], the [coaching / advisory / mastermind] is the highest level. Application required."
- Beat 5 (115–135s): Self-selection guidance. "Most viewers should start at Tier 1. Tier 2 is for [specific signal]. Tier 3 is for [specific signal]. Don't skip ahead unless the signals match."
- Beat 6 (135–150s): Sign-off. "Whatever you choose — see you in the next one."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Tiered-CTA outros earn conversion across the entire funnel by giving every viewer a next step matched to their commitment level. Beat 5's self-selection guidance is the load-bearing element; without it, viewers default to either the free tier (low LTV) or feel overwhelmed by the high-tier offer. Naming the signals that suggest Tier 2 vs Tier 3 inverts the usual upsell pressure into a fit-positioning move.
