# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 063 — Social pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Direct address frequency: every 90–120 seconds. "Now you might be thinking..." or "Here's the thing for you specifically..."
- Viewer questions: posed every 2–3 minutes. "Make sense so far?" or "Are you with me?"
- Second-person framing: "imagine you're..." rather than "imagine someone..."
- Call-and-response moments: implicit invitations for the viewer to nod / shake their head.
- Acknowledgment of attention cost: "I know this is a lot — stay with me."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Social pattern-interrupt grammar engages the parasocial circuit — the audience feels addressed rather than instructed. The acknowledgment of attention cost (Beat: acknowledgment of attention cost) is the load-bearing element; it converts the relationship from broadcaster-audience to companion-companion, which has higher retention. Without acknowledgment, the audience feels their patience is being taken for granted.
