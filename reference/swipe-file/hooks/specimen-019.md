# Hook Specimen 019 — Specific-dollar peak-event hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Specific impossible-sounding dollar+timeframe combination. "Most [operators / brands] spend an entire year trying to reach [specific revenue figure], or even worse, never reach it."
- Beat 2 (4–8s): Operator's contrasting outcome. "But I was able to do it in [unbelievably-short timeframe]."
- Beat 3 (8–18s): Promise the structural mechanism. "And in this video, I'm not only going to show you exactly how I did it, but teach you a [N]-step strategy that I and all the [credentialed peer group] use to [outcome]."
- Beat 4 (18–25s): Implicit exclusivity hook. "I promise no one's telling you this for free."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The structural pattern is dollar-figure-as-impossible-result + unbelievably-short-timeframe. Beat 4's implicit exclusivity ("no one's telling you this for free") activates information-asymmetry curiosity. The structural rule: the dollar figure and timeframe must be defensible in the body — the hook can't claim "$100K in 30 minutes" if the body reveals it took years of buildup.

## When to use
When the operator has a peak-performance event (a launch, a sale, a single high-conversion event) that can credibly anchor the dollar+timeframe combination. Less effective for steady-state revenue claims.
