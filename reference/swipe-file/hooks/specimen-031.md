# Hook Specimen 031 — Tech-stakes statistic opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Lead with a high-stakes industry statistic. ("[N%] of [tech-category companies] will be obsolete within [short timeframe].")
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Cite the source visibly. On-screen graphic of report cover or analyst firm logo.
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:20): Personal-stake bridge. "I've been tracking this category for [N years]. The pattern is clear and most operators are missing it."
- Beat 4 (0:20–0:28): Layered tease. "I'll walk through the [N] structural shifts driving this — and the [M] decisions you can make this quarter to stay on the right side."
- Beat 5 (0:28–0:35): Audience callout. "This is for anyone running [specific role / function] at a [specific company stage]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
B2B / SaaS audiences are trained to discount unsourced claims — the visible source citation (Beat 2) bypasses that filter immediately. The "structural shifts" framing (Beat 4) signals the video is strategic, not tactical, which is the high-leverage positioning for senior-IC and founder audiences. Layering the tease (N shifts + M decisions) establishes value density without overpromising.
