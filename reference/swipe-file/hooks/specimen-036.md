# Hook Specimen 036 — Asset-class contrast opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Direct comparison frame. "If you're investing in [popular asset class], you might be making [N x] less than you could be in [overlooked asset class]."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:14): Specific numerical comparison. "Last year my [overlooked-asset deals] returned [Y%]. The [popular asset class] I track returned [X%]."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:24): Mechanism-tease — why the asymmetry exists. "It's not that the market is irrational. It's that [structural reason most people don't see]."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:35): Bound-promise. "In this video I'll walk you through the asset class, how it works, the risks, and how I'd start today."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Asset-class contrast openers work in real estate because the audience is already implicitly comparing options — making the comparison explicit accelerates the cognitive evaluation the viewer was going to do anyway. The "it's not irrational, it's structural" framing (Beat 3) is the load-bearing element; without it, the contrast reads as cherry-picked. Naming risks in the bound-promise (Beat 4) earns credibility and counter-intuitively increases conversion.
