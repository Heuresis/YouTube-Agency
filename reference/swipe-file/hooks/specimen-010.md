# Hook Specimen 010 — Borrowed-authority + appropriation hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Reference the iconic outcome of an established brand archetype. "[Established brand archetype] built a [N-figure] empire with one strategy almost nobody talks about."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Pivot to operator's appropriation. "I stole it, applied it to my [niche-specific brand], and now generate [specific dollar amount] every single month."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Vulnerable starting state. "When I first started, I was lucky to make [low amount] on a good day."
- Beat 4 (18–30s): Promise the structural pattern. "By the end of this video, you'll understand the [proprietary blueprint name] — the [N-phase] strategy that creates [specific demand outcome]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This hook borrows authority from an established brand archetype, then transfers it to the operator's case via "I stole it." The vulnerable-starting-state in Beat 3 prevents the hook from reading as braggy and resets the audience's identification with the operator. Beat 4's "blueprint" framing converts the video into an artifact (a named system) the audience will want to extract.

## When to use
When the operator has a specific established figure / brand they can credibly cite as the source of their own approach. Less effective when the audience does not recognize the cited brand archetype.
