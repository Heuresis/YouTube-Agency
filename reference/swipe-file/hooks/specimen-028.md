# Hook Specimen 028 — PAST with mechanism-tease

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:06): Pain — name the specific failure pattern. ("If you're spending [$X/month] on ads and barely breaking even, you're stuck in a trap most operators don't see.")
- Beat 2 (0:06–0:12): Authority — single specific credential, no list. ("I've spent the last [N years] running profit-first audits on [N+ stores].")
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:20): Story bridge — a one-line micro-case. ("Last month I helped a brand go from -[X%] ROAS to +[Y%] in 17 days.")
- Beat 4 (0:20–0:28): Tease the mechanism, not the tactic. "It's not a new ad strategy — it's an accounting reframe most operators have never heard."
- Beat 5 (0:28–0:35): Bound-promise close. "Stick with me for the next [X] minutes and I'll show you exactly how it works."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The PAST formula's variant here is the mechanism-tease in Beat 4 — by promising not a tactic but a "reframe," the creator signals the video will be conceptually load-bearing, not a tip-list. This filters for serious operators and elevates perceived authority. The single-credential approach (Beat 2) outperforms credential-stacks because it avoids triggering the "selling-too-hard" sensor.
