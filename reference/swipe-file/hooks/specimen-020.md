# Hook Specimen 020 — "DON'T do X" reactive-content hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–3s): Direct command not to do the common action. "DON'T [start common-practice activity], watch this first."
- Beat 2 (3–10s): Tease the cost of doing it wrong. "If you [do common-practice activity] without [specific knowledge], you'll [specific bad outcome]."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Promise the alternative. "Here's what to do instead — and why [specific recent industry change] makes it more important than ever."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The "DON'T do X" hook is the most direct form of pattern interrupt. The structural rule: it must be paired with a specific recent industry / platform / algorithm change in Beat 3, otherwise it reads as generic clickbait. With a specific change, it reads as urgent advisory.

## When to use
Reactive-content slot, especially when the audience is rushing into a recently-changed market or platform. Not effective for evergreen content.
