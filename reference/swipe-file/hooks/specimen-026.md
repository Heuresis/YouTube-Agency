# Hook Specimen 026 — Contrarian-against-guru opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:04): Name the dominant convention in the niche, neutrally. ("Every personal-finance channel tells you to [common advice].")
- Beat 2 (0:04–0:09): Position the contrarian frame as evidence-backed, not opinion. ("The data says the opposite.")
- Beat 3 (0:09–0:18): One concrete example of the dominant advice failing — specific numbers, specific timeframe.
- Beat 4 (0:18–0:25): Promise the alternative framework. "There's a different way that [N% of self-made people] actually use, and almost no one teaches it on YouTube."
- Beat 5 (0:25–0:35): Pre-empt the objection. "And before you say 'this is just clickbait' — I'll show you the math, the source, and how to apply it yourself."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The contrarian-against-guru opener works in saturated niches where the audience has already heard every standard piece of advice. By naming the convention neutrally rather than mockingly, the creator avoids triggering tribal defenses. Beat 5's pre-emption of the "clickbait" objection earns trust by acknowledging the viewer's likely first reaction.

- mode: authority

- creator persona compatibility: contrarian / analytical / data-driven
- channel stage: 100K–1M (requires established credibility to avoid feeling like attention-bait)
