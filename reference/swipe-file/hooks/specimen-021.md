# Hook Specimen 021 — Mentor-Reframe authority transfer hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–5s): Visual reference to a mentor figure. "[Open with a clip / image of mentor figure]."
- Beat 2 (5–12s): Mentor framing. "If you clicked on this video I'm sure you know who that is. For those unfamiliar, [mentor figure] is [credentials]."
- Beat 3 (12–20s): Mentor's relevance to operator. "More importantly, they're a source of inspiration for anyone who's ever dreamed of [outcome]. They're one of the main reasons I was able to [specific result]."
- Beat 4 (20–30s): Promise the structural mechanism the mentor provided. "You don't need [false barrier 1] or [false barrier 2]. You just need [mentor]'s [N] principles to creating [outcome]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The mentor-reframe hook borrows the mentor's authority via association, then transfers it to the operator's specific results. The structural rule: the mentor must be specific and well-known to the audience; vague "many people inspire me" doesn't work. The mentor's principles in Beat 4 become the body's structure.

## When to use
When the operator can credibly cite a specific well-known mentor figure whose principles they've actually applied. Most effective when the mentor is in the same niche as the operator.
