# Hook Specimen 043 — Self-imposed-challenge opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:04): State the challenge in one sentence. "I gave myself [N days / hours] to [achieve specific outcome] starting from [zero state]."
- Beat 2 (0:04–0:10): Set the stakes. "If I failed, I'd have to [public-stakes consequence]. If I succeeded, [reward]."
- Beat 3 (0:10–0:18): Tease the result without revealing it. "What happened was completely different from what I expected."
- Beat 4 (0:18–0:28): Promise the process. "I'm going to show you every step, every mistake, and what I'd do differently."
- Beat 5 (0:28–0:35): Identity-flag. "This is for anyone who's tired of theory and wants to see what actually happens."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The self-imposed-challenge format leverages high social-proof of action — the creator has put themselves on the line, which the viewer respects regardless of outcome. Beat 2's stakes structure activates loss-aversion empathy in the viewer ("I want to see if they make it"). The "completely different from what I expected" tease (Beat 3) is the load-bearing curiosity hook; if the viewer can predict the outcome, they leave.
