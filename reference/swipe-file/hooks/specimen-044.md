# Hook Specimen 044 — Three-part bound-promise opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Direct promise to the viewer's specific outcome. "By the end of this video, you'll understand [Outcome A], [Outcome B], and [Outcome C]."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Authority anchor as one sentence. "I've spent [N years] working on this and the framework I'm about to share is the one I use [specific frequency]."
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:22): Pre-empt the "this is too good" objection. "This isn't theory or a quick hack. It's a thought process you can use [scope of applicability]."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:32): Roadmap the structure. "First we'll cover [section 1], then [section 2], then [section 3], and at the end I'll show you how to put it together."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The three-part bound-promise opener is a clean, low-overhead structure that earns retention through clarity — the viewer knows exactly what they're getting and how the video is structured. The pre-empt in Beat 3 disarms the skepticism that often accompanies multi-part promises. The explicit roadmap (Beat 4) reduces drop-off at section transitions because viewers are already mentally tracking progress.
