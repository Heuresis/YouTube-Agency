# Loop-Architecture Specimen 064 — Mid-video tease architecture

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Mid-video tease at ~50% mark: "I'm about to show you the part of this that almost no one talks about. But before I do, you need to understand [bridge concept]."
- Bridge concept (60–90 seconds): The prerequisite framing.
- Big reveal (60–90 seconds): The promised content.
- Mid-video tease #2 at ~75% mark: "Now there's one more thing I haven't told you, and it's the part that makes everything we've discussed actually work."
- Final reveal in last 90 seconds: The capstone insight.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Mid-video tease architecture solves the structural problem of mid-video drop-off (the 50–60% mark is where retention historically collapses). The tease at 50% recharges curiosity at the exact point retention would otherwise dip. The tease-then-bridge-then-deliver discipline (Beat: bridge concept) is load-bearing; without the bridge, the reveal feels rushed and underwhelming. The second tease at 75% extends the architecture to the final stretch.
