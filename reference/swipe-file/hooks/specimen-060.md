# Loop-Architecture Specimen 060 — Callback-cascade architecture

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Opening element planted at 0:00–0:15: A specific phrase, image, or claim that will be referenced later.
- First callback at ~30% through video: "Remember when I said [opener element]? Here's why that matters."
- Second callback at ~60% through video: "OK — so [opener element] connects to this..."
- Third callback at ~90% through video: "And that brings us back to what I said at the start..."
- Final callback in the close: "Now you understand why I started with [opener element]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Callback-cascade architecture extends the master-loop's lifespan across the full runtime — each callback recharges the audience's investment in the resolution. The discipline of three to four callbacks (rather than one or two) is structurally important; under-callback loses the architectural benefit, over-callback feels mechanical. The final callback in the close is the load-bearing element; it converts the entire video into a closed loop that feels resolved.
