# Hook Specimen 023 — Failure-as-asset personal transformation hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Specific failure described in the past tense. "[N months/years] ago, I had [specific failure / setback]."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): The failure produced an unexpected pivot. "That same [failure] led me to [unexpected discovery / business / opportunity]."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Specific present outcome. "Today, that [discovery / business / opportunity] [specific impressive result]."
- Beat 4 (18–25s): Implicit lesson framing. "Here's exactly how a [failure] became the foundation of [outcome]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The failure-as-asset hook reframes loss into causation. The structural rule: Beat 2's "led me to" must show a real causal chain — generic "everything happens for a reason" reads as platitude; specific "the rejection made me cold-DM 100 people in a week, and the 14th person became my first client" reads as causal.

## When to use
When the operator has a real failure-to-pivot story. Less effective for operators whose path was linear.
