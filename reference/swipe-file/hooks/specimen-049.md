# Hook Specimen 049 — Failure-as-asset opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:08): Open with a specific personal failure. "I lost [significant resource / status] in [short timeframe]. It was completely my fault and the lessons cost me [specific number]."
- Beat 2 (0:08–0:18): Reframe the failure as proprietary knowledge. "What I learned from that loss is now the framework I use to advise [N] [audience descriptor]."
- Beat 3 (0:18–0:26): Tease the framework. "It comes down to [N] decisions, and almost every one of them is the opposite of what 'normal' advice tells you to do."
- Beat 4 (0:26–0:35): Bound-promise. "I'll walk through each of them, what failed for me, and how to avoid the same mistakes."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The failure-as-asset opener earns trust by inverting the success-led credential pattern that dominates the niche — vulnerability is differentiation. Beat 1's specific quantification ("lost X in Y timeframe") prevents the failure from sounding rehearsed; concrete numbers feel real. The "opposite of normal advice" framing (Beat 3) is the load-bearing curiosity hook because it positions the framework as hard-won and counterintuitive.
