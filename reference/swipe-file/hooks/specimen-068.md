# Hook Specimen 068 — Paradox-anchor opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): State a specific paradox the audience can recognize. "The people who [counter-intuitive behavior] are the ones who [counter-intuitive outcome]."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Validate the paradox is real, not a rhetorical trick. "I know that sounds wrong. I thought it was wrong too — until I started looking at the data."
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:22): Tease the explanation. "There's a specific reason this happens, and once you see it, the way you approach [domain] changes."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:32): Bound-promise. "I'll walk you through the paradox, the mechanism, and what to do about it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Paradox-anchor openers earn retention because the apparent contradiction creates cognitive disequilibrium that demands resolution. Beat 2's "I thought it was wrong too" framing is load-bearing; it earns trust by acknowledging the viewer's likely first reaction. The explanation tease (Beat 3) closes the loop only at the framework level — the specific tactical implications are reserved for the body.
