# Hook Specimen 030 — Mentor-reframe opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:08): Direct identification of the viewer's struggle, with empathy. ("You've tried [common solution]. You've read [common resource]. And it isn't working.")
- Beat 2 (0:08–0:16): Reframe the struggle as a known problem, not a personal failing. "What you're experiencing has a name. [Historical thinker / framework] called it [term].")
- Beat 3 (0:16–0:24): Position yourself as the guide, not the hero. "I spent [N years] trying to outwork it. Here's what finally let me put it down."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:32): Bound-promise close with a humility marker. "I'm not going to pretend this is the only path. I'm going to show you the one that worked for me."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The mentor-reframe earns trust by lowering the creator's status — the explicit "I'm not the hero" stance (Beat 3) inverts the standard guru-bait pattern. Naming the viewer's struggle with a canonical term (Beat 2) provides cognitive relief and signals the creator has actually studied the domain. The humility marker in Beat 4 protects against backlash from skeptical viewers.
