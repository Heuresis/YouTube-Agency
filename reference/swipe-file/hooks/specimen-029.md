# Hook Specimen 029 — Existential-question opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Open with a near-universal observation phrased as a question. ("Why does it feel like the people who care the least always seem to win?")
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:14): Validate the observation as real, not paranoid. "You're not imagining this. There's a structural reason it happens."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:22): Tease the philosophical frame, not the answer. "It comes down to a [N]-thousand-year-old idea that almost no one in your life has explained to you."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:30): Promise integration. "Once you see it, you can't un-see it — and your relationship to [domain] changes permanently."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The existential-question opener works because the question itself carries social proof — the viewer feels seen the moment the question lands. By invoking "an N-thousand-year-old idea," the creator borrows the gravity of canonical knowledge without needing to demonstrate originality. The promise of permanent change (Beat 4) sets the value bar high enough to justify the runtime.
