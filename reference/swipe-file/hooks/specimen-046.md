# Hook Specimen 046 — Framework-against-tactic opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Contrast tactics vs frameworks. "Tactics will make you marginally better. Frameworks will make you 10x better. Here's why almost everyone is stuck on tactics."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:14): Authority anchor. "I've spent [N years] developing the framework I'm about to walk through, and it has produced [specific outcomes] across [N+ applications]."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:24): Tease the structural advantage. "It works because it operates at the level above what most creators teach — at the level of [meta-decision]."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:32): Bound-promise. "I'll walk you through the framework, the components, and a worked example."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The framework-vs-tactic positioning earns immediate authority by claiming a higher level of abstraction than the dominant content in the niche. Beat 1's "marginally better vs 10x better" specificity is the load-bearing element — generic "frameworks beat tactics" doesn't trigger the response. The "meta-decision" framing (Beat 3) signals to senior viewers that the video is at their level, which filters for high-quality audience.
