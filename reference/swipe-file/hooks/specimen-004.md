# Hook Specimen 004 — Aggregation-surprise statistic hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): List of small relatable monthly expenditures, each with specific dollar amount ("If you spend $X on [item], $Y on [item], and $Z on [item] every month...")
- Beat 2 (4–8s): Aggregate annual total revealed as shocking. "...that's [shocking annual total] per year disappearing."
- Beat 3 (8–15s): Generalize to the audience's experience. "This is the reality for [target avatar] — money / time / energy just vanishes."
- Beat 4 (15–25s): Implicit promise that the video will resolve the pattern.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The statistic hook exploits aggregation surprise. The conscious mind tracks small individual costs but does not track them cumulatively. The aggregate reveal converts familiar small-numbers into an unfamiliar big-number, producing the "wait, what?" pattern interrupt. Each individual cost in Beat 1 must be specific and named — "$5 on coffee" not "money on coffee."

## When to use
Personal-finance, productivity, lifestyle, and decision-cost content. Especially powerful when the audience has a vague sense their resources are leaking but cannot quantify the leak.
