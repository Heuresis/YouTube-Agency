# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 058 — Verbal pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Pace shifts: alternating between rapid (4–6 words/second) and slow (2–3 words/second). Slow segments are reserved for emphasis.
- Sentence-length variance: long sentences (15+ words) interleaved with very short ones (3–5 words).
- Intonation: rising at curiosity-points, falling at conclusions. Avoid monotone for more than 10 seconds.
- Filler words: minimized but not eliminated entirely (intentional "OK" and "so" are pattern-anchors).
- Pause discipline: 1–2 second pauses before key takeaways. Pauses are rare and intentional.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Verbal pattern-interrupt grammar solves retention at the audio-processing layer — even with eyes off the screen, the audience stays attentive because the audio keeps changing. Pause discipline (Beat: pause discipline) is the load-bearing element; pauses signal "this matters" without verbal emphasis. The technique works only with variance; consistent rapid-fire or consistent slow-pace both fatigue the listener.
