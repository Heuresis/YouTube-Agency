# Hook Specimen 040 — Creator-monetization PAST opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:06): Pain — name the trap. "If you're a creator with [N+] followers but barely making [low monthly revenue], you're stuck in the audience trap."
- Beat 2 (0:06–0:14): Authority — single specific credential. "I built a [revenue range] business off a [counterintuitively-small audience], and I've helped [N] creators do the same."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:22): Story — micro-case example. "One client of mine, [generic descriptor], went from [low revenue] to [higher revenue] in [short timeframe] without growing their audience by a single follower."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:32): Tease + Bound-promise. "It comes down to [counterintuitive shift], and I'll show you exactly what that means and how to apply it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The creator-monetization PAST opener directly addresses the "audience size doesn't equal revenue" frustration that most mid-tier creators feel. Beat 2's "counterintuitively small audience" framing immediately differentiates from the volume-led growth advice that dominates the niche. The single-client micro-case in Beat 3 outperforms generic claims because it makes the promise concrete and replicable.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: authority

- creator persona compatibility: business-strategist / pragmatic / first-person
- channel stage: 100K–1M
