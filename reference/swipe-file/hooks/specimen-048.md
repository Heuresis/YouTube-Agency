# Hook Specimen 048 — Claim-stack opener (3-claim bullet)

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Three rapid claims, each with on-screen text overlay. "By the end of this video, you'll know [Claim 1]. You'll know [Claim 2]. And you'll know [Claim 3]."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:10): Authority anchor. "I'm able to tell you this because [single specific credential, no list]."
- Beat 3 (0:10–0:18): Tease the unique angle. "And the reason this is different from everything else you've seen on this is [specific structural difference]."
- Beat 4 (0:18–0:28): Set production expectation. "I edited this down from [N hours of footage] to give you the highest-density version of this I could make."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The claim-stack opener with on-screen text creates triple cognitive debt within the first five seconds — three things the viewer wants by the end. Beat 4's "edited down from N hours" framing is a potent value-density signal that increases perceived stakes and earns continued attention. The structural-difference tease (Beat 3) prevents the video from feeling like a re-tread of common content.
