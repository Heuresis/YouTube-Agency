# Hook Specimen 045 — Rapid-cut pattern-interrupt opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:03): Short declarative claim while moving — kitchen, walking, in-car. ("I just figured out [outcome] and it took me [counterintuitively short timeframe].")
- Beat 2 (0:03–0:06): Hard cut to different location, different angle, same speaker. New sentence continues the thread.
- Beat 3 (0:06–0:09): Hard cut to whiteboard or visual. "Here's why everyone gets this wrong."
- Beat 4 (0:09–0:14): Cut back to talking head. Fast pace, short sentences.
- Beat 5 (0:14–0:25): Bound-promise close. Camera stabilizes for first time.
- Beat 6 (0:25–0:35): Identity-flag. "This is for anyone who [specific characteristic]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Rapid-cut openers compress retention pressure by introducing a pattern-interrupt every 3–5 seconds — the viewer's reticular activating system stays engaged through novelty. The location-switching (talking head → kitchen → whiteboard) signals high production density and high information density before the viewer has consciously evaluated the content. The first stable shot (Beat 5) feels like landing after turbulence, which the brain rewards with attention.
