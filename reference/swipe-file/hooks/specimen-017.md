# Hook Specimen 017 — 1-vs-9-5 job-quit contrast hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Specific year + the job-quit decision. "In [year], I quit my [conventional credential] job to go all in on a business that looked even MORE [dismissive descriptor, e.g., boring] than [the conventional job]."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Specific present-day result framed against the conventional path. "And [N years later], that same [unconventional venture] is projected to do over [specific revenue] in revenue."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Reframe the conventional credential. "As someone who was sold the dream of [conventional success path], it didn't take long to realize this 'dream' was exactly that, BUT, what this job did do is set me up for an opportunity..."
- Beat 4 (18–25s): Specific lifestyle stake. "[time with family / college fully paid / specific status outcome conventional path couldn't provide]."
- Beat 5 (25–30s): Identity statement. "My name is [name], and this is my story."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The 1-vs-9-5 hook leverages the job-quit decision as the focal moment. Beat 3's "BUT, what this job did do..." is the load-bearing rhetorical move: it converts the conventional credential from a self-aggrandizing brag into a foundation that justifies the pivot. The structural rule: the lifestyle stake in Beat 4 must speak to a specific desire of the ICP, not generic "freedom" — generic stakes don't differentiate.

## When to use
When the operator has a real job-quit / school-quit / geography-pivot moment to anchor the narrative. Less effective without a specific identity-pivot.
