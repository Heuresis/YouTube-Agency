# Hook Specimen 034 — Anti-bro-science contrarian opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Name the dominant fitness-influencer claim. ("Every fitness channel says you need to [common claim] to build muscle.")
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Position the contrarian frame as evidence-led. "The published research says the opposite — and most of these creators have either never read the studies or are misrepresenting them."
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:22): Cite the meta-analysis or specific study by visible reference. On-screen graphic of paper title.
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:30): Promise the corrected framework. "I'll walk through what the evidence actually says, and how to apply it to your training this week."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The anti-bro-science positioning carves out a specific authority lane in a noisy niche — it filters for serious viewers who are skeptical of influencer claims. Beat 3's visible study citation is the credibility-anchor that prevents the contrarian frame from feeling like another guru taking shots. Naming the misrepresentation rather than the people maintains professionalism and avoids drama-bait.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: authority

- creator persona compatibility: scientific / evidence-based / measured
- channel stage: 10K–100K, 100K–1M
