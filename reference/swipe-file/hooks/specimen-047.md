# Hook Specimen 047 — Documentary cold-open

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:08): Cold-open visual sequence — no narration. Cinematic B-roll, ambient sound, mood-establishing.
- Beat 2 (0:08–0:14): Title card or text overlay names the central question of the video.
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:25): Voice-over begins. Calm, measured, often first-person. "I've spent [N] [time period] looking into this and what I found challenges almost everything I thought I knew."
- Beat 4 (0:25–0:35): Promise the journey. "Over the next [X] minutes I'll walk you through what I learned and why it matters."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The documentary cold-open earns retention by signaling production-tier — the viewer recognizes within seconds that this is a "premium" piece of content and self-selects to invest more attention. The title card (Beat 2) is the structural anchor that converts the cinematic mood into a specific cognitive question. The voice-over's calm tone (Beat 3) inverts the high-energy norm of the niche and creates differentiation through restraint.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: reach
- audience awareness: unaware viewers in browse / discovery state
- creator persona compatibility: documentarian / measured / cinematic-eye
- channel stage: 100K–1M, 1M+
- back-end offer fit: paid newsletter, premium course, community

## Production notes
- Length range: 15–25 minutes
- Production complexity: high (cinematic shoot, archival footage, color grading)
- B-roll dependency: high
- Talking-head dependency: optional
