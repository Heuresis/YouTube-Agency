# Hook Specimen 003 — Contrast hook with identity-pivot reframe

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Specific year + unexpected past action. "In [year], I quit my [high-status conventional job] to start [unconventional venture that looked even worse than the job]."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Time elapsed + specific present-day result. "[N years later], that same [unconventional venture] is [specific impressive financial outcome]."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Reframe the conventional path as a worse outcome. "As someone who was sold the dream of [conventional success path], it didn't take long to realize the dream was [reframe]."
- Beat 4 (18–30s): Specific lifestyle stake the conventional path could not provide ("more than [comparable role] while still being able to [family / lifestyle outcome]").
- Beat 5 (30–35s): Identity claim. "My name is [name], and this is my story."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The contrast hook's power is the gap between Beats 1 and 2 — the audience auto-asks "what happened in the gap?" and stays for the answer. Beat 3's reframe is the load-bearing rhetorical move: it converts a credential (the conventional job) from a humble-brag into a contextual asset that supports the unconventional venture's credibility.

## When to use
When the operator has an actual identity-pivot story with high-status credentials they can downgrade. Less effective for operators who built their career exclusively in the unconventional space (no high-status credential to contrast with).
