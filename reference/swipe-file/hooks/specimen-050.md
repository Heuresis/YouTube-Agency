# Hook Specimen 050 — Counter-intuitive-claim cold open

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:03): Single counterintuitive statement, no preamble. ("[Common belief] is actually wrong.")
- Beat 2 (0:03–0:08): Visual evidence — chart, document, or artifact on screen.
- Beat 3 (0:08–0:16): Frame the implications. "If this is true — and the data says it is — then almost everything you've been told about [domain] needs reconsidering."
- Beat 4 (0:16–0:25): Bound-promise. "I'll show you the evidence, what it actually means, and what to do about it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The counter-intuitive-claim opener earns retention through cognitive disequilibrium — the viewer must resolve the contradiction. Beat 2's immediate visual evidence is the credibility-anchor that prevents the claim from being dismissed as click-bait. Beat 3's "almost everything you've been told needs reconsidering" frame raises stakes high enough that the viewer feels the cost of leaving.
