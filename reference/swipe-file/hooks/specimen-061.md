# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 061 — Kinesthetic pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Hand gestures: deliberate, varied, used to emphasize specific words.
- Body language shifts: leaning forward at high-stakes points, settling back at moments of synthesis.
- Eye direction: looking off-camera (thinking) before key statements, then locking on camera for the statement.
- Walking / standing variance: in less-formal styles, the creator changes physical position between sections.
- Object handling: occasional handling of relevant objects (book, document, prop) for tactile pattern-interrupt.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Kinesthetic pattern-interrupt grammar engages the social-evaluation circuit — the audience reads body language even when they aren't consciously paying attention. The eye-direction discipline (Beat: eye direction) is the load-bearing element; the look-away-then-lock-on pattern signals authentic thought rather than rehearsal, which earns trust. The technique requires deliberate practice; performative gestures underperform natural ones.
