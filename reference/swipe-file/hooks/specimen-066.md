# Loop-Architecture Specimen 066 — Progressive-disclosure architecture

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Layer 1 promised in opener: "I'm going to show you [Layer 1 thing]."
- Layer 1 delivered around the 25% mark.
- Layer 2 introduced: "But Layer 1 isn't actually the most important part. Let me tell you what is."
- Layer 2 delivered around the 50% mark.
- Layer 3 introduced: "Once you see Layer 2, the next question becomes [Layer 3]."
- Layer 3 delivered around the 75% mark.
- Final layer / synthesis at 90–100%: "Now you can see why the original question — Layer 1 — was just the entry point."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Progressive-disclosure architecture earns retention because each layer feels like a complete reward while opening a new layer's promise — the audience gets payoffs throughout but new curiosity at every payoff. The "Layer 1 was just the entry point" framing (final beat) is the load-bearing element; it retroactively elevates the entire video's runtime by reframing the journey as essential. Without it, the architecture reads as scope-creep rather than depth.
