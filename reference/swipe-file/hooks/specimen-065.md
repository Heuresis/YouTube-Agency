# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 065 — Musical / sound-design pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Music exists, kept low (background level).
- Music shifts at section transitions: different tracks for different sections, or musical-mood changes.
- Sound-design accents: subtle whoosh / impact / chime sounds at key moments (text appears, scene cuts).
- Silence as interrupt: occasionally cutting all music for 5–10 seconds at the most important takeaway. The silence itself becomes the emphasis.
- Volume dynamics: music swells slightly at curiosity-hook moments, ducks during dialogue.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Musical / sound-design pattern-interrupt grammar engages the audio-emotional channel — viewers don't consciously notice music changes but their attention rides them. Silence as interrupt (Beat: silence as interrupt) is the most counterintuitive but most effective; cutting music creates a subconscious "something important is about to happen" signal. The technique requires audio engineering; weak sound design underperforms careful sound design even with identical content.
