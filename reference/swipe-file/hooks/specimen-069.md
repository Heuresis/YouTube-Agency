# Hook Specimen 069 — Conviction-anchor opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:06): State a high-conviction position with a deliberate marker of confidence. "After [N years] of doing this, I'm willing to bet on this: [specific claim]."
- Beat 2 (0:06–0:14): Pre-empt skepticism by acknowledging it. "If that sounds extreme, I get it. Most people in this space hedge. I won't."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:24): Promise the evidence trail. "I'll walk you through the framework, the data, and what I've actually seen across [N+ engagements / years / cases]."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:32): Filter the audience. "If you want consensus, this isn't for you. If you want one operator's high-conviction view backed by real reps, stay with me."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Conviction-anchor openers earn senior-audience retention because the audience is fatigued by hedge-everything content — strong views are cognitively refreshing. Beat 2's explicit "most people hedge, I won't" is the load-bearing differentiator; it converts the conviction from arrogance to editorial stance. The explicit audience-filter (Beat 4) is a reverse-bid for attention that increases perceived seriousness.
