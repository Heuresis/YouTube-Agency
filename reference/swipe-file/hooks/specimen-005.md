# Hook Specimen 005 — Direct-contradiction contrarian hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–3s): Direct contradiction of common belief. "Everyone tells you to [common conventional advice]."
- Beat 2 (3–8s): Specific personal counter-evidence. "But I [achieved counter-result] by doing the opposite."
- Beat 3 (8–15s): Frame the conventional advice as actively harmful. "Here's why everything you've been told about [topic] is keeping you [negative outcome]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The contrarian hook's power is the directness in Beat 1 — softer phrasings ("most people think...") have lower CTR than direct contradiction ("everyone tells you... [pause] ... they're wrong"). Beat 3's "actively harmful" frame is the escalation that prevents the viewer from compromising ("maybe both approaches work?") — the operator is committing to a fully contrarian position, which raises the stakes of staying to find out why.

## When to use
Saturated niches where the audience has heard the standard advice many times. Less effective in unsaturated niches because there's no widely-held belief to contradict.
