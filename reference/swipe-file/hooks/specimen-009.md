# Hook Specimen 009 — Validate-then-pivot comparative hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Acknowledge the trending opportunity the audience has heard about. "What if I told you there's a [opportunity / side hustle / business model] everyone's going crazy about, but 99% of people are missing a way better opportunity right in front of them?"
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Validate the trending option as legitimate. "[Trending option] is real — many people are making money."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Pivot to the alternative. "But here's what nobody's talking about: if you're putting in the effort to [skill the trending option requires], there's a MUCH more profitable way to monetize those exact same skills."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The comparative-analysis hook avoids the trap of dismissing the audience's existing interest. Beat 2's validation ("the trending option is real") preserves trust; the pivot in Beat 3 then reads as a recommendation, not a rejection. The structural insight: validate before contradicting, otherwise the audience defends their existing choice and disengages.

## When to use
When the operator wants to redirect audience attention from a trending alternative to a less-discussed but higher-ROI option in adjacent territory. Most effective when the operator can demonstrate both options share a core skill set.
