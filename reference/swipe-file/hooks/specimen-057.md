# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 057 — Visual pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Cut frequency: 3–5 seconds maximum between hard cuts.
- Cut types alternate: angle change, location change, B-roll, on-screen text, archival footage, demonstration.
- Color grading shifts between segments — different scene colors prevent visual fatigue.
- Framing varies — wide shot to close-up to product / artifact insert.
- The exception: one or two intentional "stable" segments where the camera rests (typically before key takeaways) that feel like landing.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Visual pattern-interrupt grammar solves the retention-decay problem at the cellular level — every 3–5 seconds the visual processor resets. The "stable" segments before takeaways are structurally critical; without them, the high-cut grammar feels exhausting and viewers fatigue out. The pattern works only with alternation; same-type cuts in succession (all B-roll, all close-ups) lose the pattern-interrupt benefit because the brain detects the pattern.
