# Hook Specimen 018 — Anti-complexity "it's all bullshit" hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Direct dismissal of complex positioning. "Everyone's out here making [activity] seem complicated when it's actually really simple."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): List the false barriers. ['You need [false barrier 1].' 'You need [false barrier 2].' 'You need [false barrier 3].']
- Beat 3 (10–14s): Direct dismissal. "It's all bullshit."
- Beat 4 (14–25s): Counter-evidence from the operator's own start. "I started [the venture] without [false barrier 1], with minimal [false barrier 2], and definitely not [false barrier 3]."
- Beat 5 (25–35s): Specific result. "Now we're doing [specific result]..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This hook works in niches where the audience feels gatekept by perceived prerequisites. The "It's all bullshit" pattern interrupt at Beat 3 is severe — softer phrasings ("not really true") have lower CTR. The structural rule: the false barriers in Beat 2 must be the actual barriers the audience believes; if they're strawmen, the hook reads as gaslighting.

## When to use
Reach-mode videos targeting audiences gatekept by perceived prerequisites (degrees, capital, experience, gear). Less effective in niches where the prerequisites are real.
