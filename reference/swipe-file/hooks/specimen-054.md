# Loop-Architecture Specimen 054 — Triple-nested loop architecture

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Loop A (opens 0:00–0:08, closes at end of video): The largest promise — "By the end you'll understand X."
- Loop B (opens 0:25–0:40, closes at ~70% through video): A specific story or case-study setup whose payoff sits 10+ minutes later.
- Loop C (opens 0:55–1:10, closes at ~30% through video): A tactical question the early section answers.
- Mini-loops (every 60–90s): Small "I'll show you in a minute" promises that close within 1–2 minutes.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Triple-nested loops solve the retention problem at three timescales simultaneously — Loop C closes early enough to reward the viewer with a payoff before they leave, Loop B closes mid-video to maintain interest into the second half, Loop A pulls them to the end. The staggered closure pattern is what differentiates this from single-loop structures; the viewer is constantly being rewarded with closure while new loops keep opening.
