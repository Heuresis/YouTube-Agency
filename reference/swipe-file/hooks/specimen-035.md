# Hook Specimen 035 — Deal-as-proof PAST opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Pain — name the gatekeeping belief. ("Everyone tells you that you need a huge down payment and perfect credit to start in real estate. They're wrong.")
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Authority — micro-proof, single deal. "I just closed on a [property type] with [counterintuitively-low resource]."
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:22): Story — preview the strategy without naming the mechanism. "I used a strategy that's completely legal, takes [short timeframe] to set up, and almost nobody is teaching."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:30): Tease + Bound promise. "I'll show you exactly how the deal worked, what it required, and how you can replicate it."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The deal-as-proof structure is the real-estate-niche-specific authority anchor — a single specific transaction outweighs years of generic advice. The "almost nobody is teaching" framing (Beat 3) creates scarcity-of-knowledge perception, which is high-leverage in a niche where viewers are saturated with similar-sounding gurus. Beat 1's gatekeeping-belief naming triggers tribal alignment with the viewer's existing frustration.
