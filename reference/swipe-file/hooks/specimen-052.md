# Hook Specimen 052 — Mystery-explainer opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Pose a question the viewer didn't know they had. "Why does [common product / phenomenon] work the way it does, and why is almost no one talking about how strange it actually is?"
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:14): Validate the question by surfacing the strangeness. "Most people interact with this every day and never think about it. But once you see what's really happening, you can't unsee it."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:24): Promise the explanation chain. "I'll walk you through the technology, the history, the economics, and the implications — in that order, because each one only makes sense once you understand the previous."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:35): Set tone. "This is going to be technical in places, but I'll keep it approachable."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The mystery-explainer opener works because it surfaces a question the viewer's brain hadn't formulated but recognizes as valid the moment it's asked. Beat 3's structured roadmap (technology → history → economics → implications) is a load-bearing element — it signals depth and prevents the video from feeling like a surface explainer. The "can't unsee it" framing (Beat 2) is high-leverage curiosity bait.
