# Hook Specimen 041 — In-medias-res narrative opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Drop the viewer into the middle of a story. "It was [day / time] and I was sitting across from [generic descriptor of a person] when they said something that changed how I think about [domain] forever."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Withhold the line. "I'll tell you what they said in a minute. But first you need to understand the context, because without it, the line just sounds like a cliché."
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:22): Brief context — who, where, why this conversation mattered.
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:32): Re-tease the closure. "By the end of this video, you'll understand the full story, the lesson, and how I've used it [N times] since."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The in-medias-res structure is one of the most retention-stable openers known — it activates the brain's narrative-completion drive within the first 5 seconds. Beat 2's explicit withholding ("I'll tell you what they said in a minute") is the signature move; it converts the opener into an open loop the viewer cannot easily abandon. Beat 4's "I've used it N times since" frames the lesson as proven-in-practice, not theoretical.
