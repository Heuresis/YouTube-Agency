# Hook Specimen 013 — Famous-Figure Reframe hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Famous-figure reference + magnitude promise. "[Famous figure] is building a [N-figure] empire right in front of you."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Contrarian "but-not-where-you-think." "But it is NOT with [their famous public venture]."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Reveal the lesser-known venture. "Their latest venture is [specific lesser-known thing] and very few people understand just how [significant outcome] this can actually get them."
- Beat 4 (18–25s): Operator's tangentially-related credential. "Coming from someone who's already [tangentially-relevant achievement], this video is going to dive deep into [the lesser-known venture]."
- Beat 5 (25–30s): Explicit retention command. "I want you to stick with me through this entire thing because there's a lot of different pieces you can copy on your own journey."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This is the "Famous-Figure Reframe Hook" — the operator borrows the SEO / discovery gravity of a famous figure while building their own authority on the side. The contrarian "but-not-where-you-think" in Beat 2 is the load-bearing element: it preempts the audience's assumption that this is just a generic commentary video. Beat 4's tangential credential lets the operator earn the right to comment without claiming to be on the famous figure's level.

## When to use
When the operator wants to leverage a famous figure's gravity for SEO / discovery while establishing their own brand. Less effective if the operator's tangential credential is too thin (audience perceives the commentary as parasitic).
