# Hook Specimen 001 — PAST formula opener with delay step

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–3s): Direct address to the viewer's specific pain point in one sentence ("If you're struggling to [achieve outcome] and feeling like maybe you don't have what it takes...")
- Beat 2 (3–8s): Reversal — "I have news" — pivots from pain to promise.
- Beat 3 (8–15s): Specific numerical proof embedded in the promise (specific result, specific timeframe, specific differentiator).
- Beat 4 (15–25s): Time-commitment statement ("In the next [X] minutes, I'll break it down step-by-step").
- Beat 5 (25–35s): Tease delivery via "But first, let me show you why [common conventional advice] is actually [hidden cost]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The PAST formula's power comes from the explicit delay step (Beat 5). The viewer's brain gets the pain-acknowledgment, the promise, and the time-bounded commitment — but is then redirected to "first, you need to understand why your current approach is failing." That redirect opens a curiosity loop the body will close.

## When to use
Educational long-form videos targeting solution-aware audiences where the viewer arrived with a known problem and is filtering through promised solutions. Most effective when the operator has a specific, defensible numerical credential to embed in Beat 3.
