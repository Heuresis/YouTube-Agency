# Hook Specimen 002 — AID formula with dismissal-then-reframe

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–3s): Open with a curiosity-inducing unanswered question that contradicts the audience's assumption ("Have you ever wondered why some [things in space] [achieve impossible outcome] while [perfectly executed alternatives] [fail]?")
- Beat 2 (3–8s): Dismiss the conventional explanation ("You've probably heard it's [common explanation].")
- Beat 3 (8–15s): Reveal the contrarian frame ("But what if it has nothing to do with [common explanation] and everything to do with [contrarian truth]?")
- Beat 4 (15–25s): Promise specific outcome ("Once you understand this, [transformation will follow]")
- Beat 5 (25–35s): Delay tactic — "But before I reveal it, I need to explain why [setup belief]"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The AID formula's load-bearing element is the dismissal in Beat 2 — the operator preempts the viewer's existing mental model before introducing the new one. Without the dismissal, the contrarian frame in Beat 3 reads as another competing claim; with the dismissal, it reads as the resolution to a contradiction the viewer was already carrying.

## When to use
Niches where the audience has been over-exposed to standard advice and is starting to suspect the standard advice doesn't work. Less effective in fresh / unsaturated niches where the conventional explanation hasn't yet been heard.
