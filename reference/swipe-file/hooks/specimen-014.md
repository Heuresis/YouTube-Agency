# Hook Specimen 014 — Vulnerable-starting-state personal transformation hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Vulnerable starting state with specific time-and-financial markers. "[N years/months] ago, I was [dead broke / living in parents' basement / rejected from dream job]."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Specific impressive present outcome. "Today, that same [failure / setback] led me to build a business that's on track to do [specific revenue]."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Implicit promise of the path. "Here's exactly what changed."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
This hook activates the comparative-self mechanism through extreme vulnerability in Beat 1. The starting state must be lower than the audience's current state — only then does the audience auto-conclude "if they could come back from that, I can come back from this." Generic "I started from nothing" doesn't work; specific "I was rejected from my dream job and living in my parents' basement" does.

## When to use
When the operator has a real low-point story to anchor the narrative. Less effective for operators whose career has been linear (no real low-point to contrast with).
