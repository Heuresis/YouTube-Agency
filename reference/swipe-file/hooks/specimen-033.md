# Hook Specimen 033 — Visual-contrast physique opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:04): Open on a vulnerable verbal admission, paired with a "before" visual. ("Three years ago, I looked like this and nothing I did was working.")
- Beat 2 (0:04–0:08): Visual cut to current physique, no words, music shift.
- Beat 3 (0:08–0:16): Numerical specificity around the change. "[N pounds / X% body fat] in [Y months]. No drugs, no extreme diets, no genetic luck."
- Beat 4 (0:16–0:25): Mechanism-tease. "What changed wasn't the workout. It was [N] decisions I made about [counterintuitive variable]."
- Beat 5 (0:25–0:35): Bound-promise. "In the next [X] minutes I'll walk you through all of them, in the order I'd do it again."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The visual-contrast opener delivers proof before claim — the physique transformation is the credential. Beat 2's silent reveal (no voice-over) is structurally important; voice-over during the reveal flattens the impact. The "decisions about counterintuitive variable" tease (Beat 4) outperforms tactic-promises ("here are 5 exercises") because it positions the video as conceptually load-bearing.
