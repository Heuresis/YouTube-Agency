# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 067 — Narrative pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Micro-story interrupts: every 2–3 minutes, the speaker breaks teaching with a short anecdote (30–60s) that illustrates the point.
- Personal-stake asides: "I remember when I was first trying this..." Brief, specific.
- Counter-example asides: "Now you might be tempted to think [common interpretation]. Here's why that's wrong with a quick example..."
- Concession-then-redirect: "I want to be honest — this part of the framework I'm still figuring out. But here's what I'm pretty sure of..."
- Each interrupt returns explicitly: "OK — so back to what I was saying about [main thread]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Narrative pattern-interrupt grammar refreshes engagement at the cognitive layer — abstract teaching causes attention-fade, concrete story restores it. The "concession-then-redirect" beat (Beat: concession-then-redirect) is the load-bearing trust element; admitting incomplete knowledge in the middle of teaching is unusual and signals authenticity. The explicit return pattern ("back to what I was saying") prevents the asides from becoming digressions.
