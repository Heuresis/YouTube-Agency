# Hook Specimen 025 — Inverted-statistic shock opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:03): A counterintuitive numerical claim stated as flat fact, no preamble. ("Most people earning [bracket] retire with less than [low number].")
- Beat 2 (0:03–0:07): Visual cut to the statistic on-screen, large bold typography, single accent color. Voice maintains pace.
- Beat 3 (0:07–0:14): Fast contextualization — why the statistic is true, in one sentence, naming the cognitive trap.
- Beat 4 (0:14–0:22): Personal stake bridge. "I've spent [N years] watching this happen, and the pattern is always the same."
- Beat 5 (0:22–0:30): Bound-promise pivot. "In the next [X] minutes, I'll show you the [N] decisions that separate the people who escape this from the people who don't."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The inverted-statistic opener bypasses the reflexive skepticism that greets most finance content. By leading with a number that contradicts the viewer's assumption, the creator triggers a cognitive disequilibrium that demands resolution. The fast contextualization (Beat 3) provides just enough framing to feel credible without satisfying the curiosity — leaving the loop open through the bound-promise close.
