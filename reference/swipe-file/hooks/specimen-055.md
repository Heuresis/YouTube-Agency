# Loop-Architecture Specimen 055 — Single-master-loop architecture

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Loop opens at 0:00–0:08: A specific question, story setup, or promise the entire video exists to resolve.
- Loop is referenced (but not closed) at every section transition: "Remember the [thing] I mentioned at the start? We're getting closer to it."
- Loop closes in the final 60 seconds: The promised resolution lands.
- The body of the video earns its runtime by being prerequisite-to-resolution: each section delivers context the resolution requires.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Single-master-loop architecture is the highest-retention long-form structure because the audience cannot leave without sacrificing the resolution they've invested in. The discipline required is structural; every body section must serve the master loop or the architecture collapses into "I forgot what they were going to tell me." References at section transitions are load-bearing — without them, the master loop fades from working memory.
