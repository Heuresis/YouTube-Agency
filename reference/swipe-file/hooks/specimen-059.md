# Pattern-Interrupt Specimen 059 — Textual pattern-interrupt grammar

## Structure (paraphrased)
- On-screen text frequency: every 30–60 seconds during dialogue-heavy segments.
- Text content: a single phrase from the speaker's sentence, isolated and emphasized. Not a full transcript.
- Color: text is always one of two accent colors that contrast with the background.
- Animation: text often "drops in" or "wipes" rather than fading — visible motion captures attention.
- Duration: text remains for 2–4 seconds, long enough to read, short enough not to compete with the speaker.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Textual pattern-interrupt grammar adds a third channel (visual text) on top of audio and video, increasing total information density without overwhelming the viewer. Single-phrase emphasis (Beat: text content) is the load-bearing discipline; full-transcript text overlays distract and underperform. The animation is what differentiates pattern-interrupt text from generic captions; static text gets ignored.
