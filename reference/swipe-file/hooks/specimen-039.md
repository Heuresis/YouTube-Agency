# Hook Specimen 039 — Creator-economy stats stack opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Two statistics in fast succession. "[N%] of creators never break [low subscriber bracket]. The ones that do almost all share one trait."
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:14): Authority anchor. "I've analyzed [M+ channels] over the last [N years] and the pattern is statistically obvious once you know what to look for."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:22): Tease the mechanism. "It isn't quality. It isn't consistency. It's [counterintuitive variable]."
- Beat 4 (0:22–0:32): Bound-promise. "I'll show you what it is, why almost nobody talks about it, and exactly how to apply it this week."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Stats-stack openers in creator-economy content work because the audience is intensely numerically curious about creator-success patterns — they want to know "what's the actual differentiator?" The "it isn't quality, it isn't consistency" elimination structure (Beat 3) is the load-bearing element; by ruling out the obvious answers, the creator forces the viewer to wait for the real mechanism. Beat 1's two-stat opener creates double cognitive debt.
