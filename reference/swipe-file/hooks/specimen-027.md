# Hook Specimen 027 — Double-loop curiosity stack opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:05): Open Loop A — a specific outcome with the mechanism withheld. ("I closed [N high-value clients] last month doing one thing that I'm about to show you.")
- Beat 2 (0:05–0:12): Open Loop B before closing Loop A. ("But the only reason it worked is because of a counterintuitive idea I learned from [vague reference: 'a 90-year-old playbook'].")
- Beat 3 (0:12–0:20): Tease both closures. "By the end of this video you'll understand both — what to do, and why it works when nothing else does."
- Beat 4 (0:20–0:30): Identity-flag the viewer. "If you're someone who's already trying [common approach] and getting nothing back, this is for you."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Stacking two open loops in the first 12 seconds doubles the cognitive debt the viewer feels — both loops must close before they can leave. The vague second reference (Beat 2) is deliberately undescribed to maximize curiosity density. The identity flag in Beat 4 captures the tribally-aligned viewer who feels personally addressed.
