# Hook Specimen 015 — Discovery-loop with research-aggregation hook

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–3s): Specific large research effort. "I analyzed [large number] of [things in space] and found something shocking."
- Beat 2 (3–10s): Hint at the contrarian finding without revealing it. "What I found contradicts everything we've been taught about [topic]."
- Beat 3 (10–20s): Promise the reveal in the body. "I'll show you exactly what the data says — and how to apply it to your own [domain]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The discovery loop pre-installs the credibility of the finding in Beat 1 — "I analyzed [large N]" implies methodology, not opinion. Beat 2's contrarian frame ("contradicts everything") is the curiosity bait. The structural rule: the [large number] must be specific (e.g., "1,000 viral videos," not "many videos") to maintain credibility.

## When to use
Research-based content, especially when the operator has actually done the analysis. Burns out fast if used as a frame without underlying analysis (audience eventually pattern-matches "claims to have analyzed N of X" as boilerplate).
