# Hook Specimen 038 — Career-decision AID opener

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0:00–0:06): Attention — name the specific career inflection point. "If you're stuck at [career stage] and watching less-talented people get promoted past you, this video is going to be uncomfortable."
- Beat 2 (0:06–0:14): Interest — the discomfort as evidence. "I've coached [N] executives through this exact moment, and the reason they're stuck is almost never the reason they think."
- Beat 3 (0:14–0:24): Desire — bound-promise the diagnosis. "I'll walk you through the [N] visibility patterns that separate the people who break through from the people who plateau."
- Beat 4 (0:24–0:35): Direction — set tone expectations. "This is direct. If you want platitudes about hard work, this isn't the video."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The career-decision AID opener earns retention by naming a high-stakes professional pain point with specificity — the "less-talented people getting promoted" frame is universal among ambitious mid-career viewers. Beat 4's tone-expectation setting is a high-leverage filter — it qualifies the audience and signals the creator won't soften hard truths. The "almost never the reason they think" framing (Beat 2) opens a powerful curiosity loop.

- channel stage: 10K–100K
