# Hook Specimen 006 — Curiosity stack with multiple improbable elements

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (0–4s): Specific named figure achieving impossible outcome. "There's a [age]-year-old who makes more money in [short timeframe] than most people make in [long timeframe]."
- Beat 2 (4–10s): Specific business model that audience considers dead/disreputable. "And they do it using a business model 99% of people think is dead."
- Beat 3 (10–18s): Stack of additional improbable elements. "What's even crazier is they've never [conventional necessary action], never [other conventional action], and spend less than [unbelievably-small effort]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The curiosity stack hook layers multiple individually-improbable elements into a single sentence cluster. Each layer adds a question the audience must stay to resolve. The mechanism: the brain can dismiss one improbable claim, but dismissing four simultaneously requires more effort than just watching the video.

## When to use
When the operator wants to maximize CTR at the expense of niche-specificity (broad-appeal mode). Especially effective for case-study videos where multiple counter-stereotypes are about to be unpacked.
