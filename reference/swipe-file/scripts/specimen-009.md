# Script Section Specimen 009 — Callback-driven continuity body

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (5–15s): Reference an earlier section. "Remember when I said [specific earlier point]? Now I'm going to show you why."
- Beat 2 (15–60s): Explain how the current section builds on the earlier point. "In Section [N], I talked about [topic]. That principle applies here because [specific connection]."
- Beat 3 (60–120s): Deliver the new content with the earlier point as foundation.
- Beat 4 (120–150s): Reinforce the connection. "This is why [earlier principle] matters — because it sets up [current insight]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The callback-driven section creates structural continuity across the video. Each callback rewards viewers who have been paying attention, while signaling to drifting viewers that they should re-engage. The structural rule: callbacks must reference specific earlier points, not vague "as I said before" — the specificity is what makes the callback feel earned.

## When to use
Long-form (12-minute+) videos where structural continuity is critical. Use 1 callback per 2–3 sections; more than that produces a "this is just repetition" feeling.
