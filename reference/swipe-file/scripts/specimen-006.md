# Script Section Specimen 006 — Tactical-walkthrough how-to body

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (10–20s): State the specific tactic. "Step [N]: [specific named action]."
- Beat 2 (20–40s): Why this step matters. "Most people skip this step because they think [false belief]. Here's why that's wrong: [counter-evidence]."
- Beat 3 (40–80s): How to actually do it. "Here's exactly what to do: [specific sub-step 1]. [Specific sub-step 2]. [Specific sub-step 3]."
- Beat 4 (80–120s): Common mistakes. "The mistake most people make here is [specific error]. To avoid this, [specific safeguard]."
- Beat 5 (120–160s): What you should expect to see. "When you do this correctly, you should see [specific observable indicator]. If you don't, [specific troubleshooting step]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The tactical-walkthrough section structure is built for execution. Beat 5's "what you should expect to see" is the load-bearing element — it converts the tactic from instructions into a closed-loop process the viewer can verify. Without Beat 5, viewers attempt the tactic, get unexpected results, and disengage.

## When to use
How-to / tutorial videos where the audience expects to be able to execute the tactic afterward. Less suitable for high-level strategy content where execution is downstream.
