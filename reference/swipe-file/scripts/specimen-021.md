# Script-Section Specimen 021 — Documentary chapter body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Chapter title card (5–10s): On-screen title — "Chapter [N]: [Theme]." Brief musical sting.
- Opening B-roll (15–30s): Cinematic visual sequence introducing the chapter's setting / subject.
- Narration begins (60–90s): First-person voice-over delivers the chapter's opening narrative beat.
- Evidence (60–120s): Documents, archival footage, or interview clips supporting the narrative beat.
- Reflection (45–60s): Voice-over reflects on what the evidence means. "What this tells us is [interpretation]."
- Bridge to next chapter (15–25s): "Which brings us to [next chapter's question]." Music shift signals transition.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Documentary chapter sections earn long-form retention by structuring the video as a narrative journey rather than a tactical breakdown. The chapter title cards (Beat 1) are the load-bearing structural element; without them, the video loses its sense of forward motion. The reflection beat (Beat 5) is what differentiates documentary from journalism — it tells the audience the meaning the creator drew from the evidence.
