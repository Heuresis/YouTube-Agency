# Script-Section Specimen 020 — Monetization-mechanism body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "Here's what most creators get wrong about the math of audience-to-revenue."
- Math walkthrough (60–90s): On-screen calculation. "If you have [audience size] and you assume [conversion rate], you get [revenue]. That math is wrong because [specific structural error]."
- Corrected math (60–90s): "The actual model is [different math]. Let me walk you through it: [specific calculation]."
- Lever identification (60–90s): "There are [N] levers in this model. Most creators only operate on [common lever]. The high-leverage levers are [different levers]."
- Tactical move (45–60s): Which lever to pull first. "Start with [specific lever] because [reasoning]."
- Transition (10–15s): "OK — that gives you the math. Now let's talk about how to actually build the offer."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Monetization-mechanism sections work because creator-economy audiences are intensely numerically curious — the on-screen math creates a visceral sense of opportunity. The "corrected math" structure (Beat 3) is the load-bearing element; by surfacing the structural error in common reasoning, the creator establishes superior insight. The lever-prioritization (Beat 4) converts insight into action.
