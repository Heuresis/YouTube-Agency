# Script-Section Specimen 036 — Phased-timeline body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "Let me give you the realistic timeline. I'm not going to compress this."
- Phase 1: 0–[N] weeks (60–90s): What to do, what to expect, what failures to expect.
- Phase 2: [N]–[M] weeks (60–90s): Same structure.
- Phase 3: [M]–[X] weeks (60–90s): Same structure.
- Phase 4: [X]+ weeks (60–90s): Same structure.
- Honesty about variance (45–60s): "Some people compress this. Some people take longer. The variables are [specific factors]."
- What success looks like in each phase (60–90s): "By Phase [N], you should see [specific signal]. If you're not seeing it, the issue is probably [specific diagnostic]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now you have the timeline. Let me show you the actual mechanics of Phase 1 in detail."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Phased-timeline body sections earn solution-aware-audience trust by explicitly resisting the compression-bias that dominates the niche — most content overpromises speed. Beat 6's "honesty about variance" is the load-bearing trust-element; it signals the creator isn't pretending the audience is homogenous. The "what success looks like" anchor (Beat 7) is what converts the timeline from intimidating to navigable.
