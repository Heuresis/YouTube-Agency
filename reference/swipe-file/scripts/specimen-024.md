# Script-Section Specimen 024 — Day-by-day challenge body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Day [N] header (5–10s): On-screen text — "Day [N]." Date or count.
- Goal (15–20s): What was the goal for this day?
- Action (60–90s): What actually happened. On-camera, vlog-style. Vulnerable moments included.
- Obstacle (30–45s): Something went wrong. Show it without polish.
- Pivot (45–60s): What the creator decided to do differently. "OK — so I tried [thing], it didn't work. Here's what I'm trying instead."
- End-of-day reflection (30–45s): Honest assessment. "Today was [honest descriptor]. Lessons: [N specific things]."
- Transition (5–10s): "On to Day [N+1]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Day-by-day challenge sections earn unusual retention by combining narrative arc with high-frequency pattern interrupts (each day is a mini-arc). The vulnerability of showing obstacles (Beat 4) is the load-bearing trust element; polished challenge content reads as fake. The end-of-day reflection (Beat 6) is the mechanism that converts entertainment into lesson — without it, the format is reality TV without payoff.
