# Script-Section Specimen 027 — Three-act-story body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Setup (45–75s): Establish character, setting, and stakes. "Picture [generic descriptor]. They're in [situation]. They want [goal]. The obstacle is [obstacle]."
- Inciting incident (30–45s): What disrupts the status quo. "Then [specific event happened]."
- Rising action (60–90s): Two or three escalating beats. "First, [event]. Then [escalation]. Then [further escalation]."
- Climax (30–45s): The decision moment. "At [moment], they had to choose between [option A] and [option B]."
- Resolution (45–60s): What they chose and what happened. "They chose [option]. And the result was [outcome]."
- Lesson (30–45s): What the story teaches. "The lesson here is [specific principle]."
- Application (30–45s): How the audience applies it. "In your context, this looks like [specific behavior]."
- Transition (10–15s): "OK — that's the story. Now let me show you the underlying structure."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Three-act-story sections leverage the brain's narrative-completion drive — once a story is started, the viewer commits to seeing it end. The lesson + application beats (6, 7) are what differentiate this from pure entertainment; without them, the story is forgettable. The "now let me show you the underlying structure" transition (Beat 8) elevates the section from anecdote to teaching.
