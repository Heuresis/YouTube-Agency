# Script Section Specimen 008 — Showing-not-telling with on-camera artifact

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (5–15s): Make a specific dollar / outcome claim. "[Specific number]: [outcome] in [timeframe]."
- Beat 2 (15–25s): Embedded production cue: hold up artifact to camera. *[hold up phone showing screenshot of [specific verifiable artifact]]*
- Beat 3 (25–60s): Walk through the artifact in detail. "Look at this: [specific data point]. [Specific data point]. This shows [specific implication]."
- Beat 4 (60–90s): Generalize from the specific case. "And this is just one of [N similar artifacts]. Today I help [target audience] generate triple that."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The showing-not-telling section converts abstract claims into on-camera evidence. The embedded production cue (Beat 2) is the load-bearing element — without the artifact, Beat 1's claim is just a claim; with the artifact, it's documented. The structural rule: the script must specify the artifact and its on-camera placement, not just the words.

## When to use
Any time the section makes a specific dollar / outcome / result claim. The on-camera artifact (phone screenshot, dashboard, contract, screenshot of bank account, screenshot of analytics) shifts the claim from "operator's word" to "operator's evidence" — a fundamentally different cognitive category.
