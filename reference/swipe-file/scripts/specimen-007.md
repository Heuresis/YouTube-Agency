# Script Section Specimen 007 — Belief-installation objection-handle section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (10–20s): Surface the audience's likely objection. "At this point, you're probably thinking '[specific objection in audience's voice].' That's a fair concern."
- Beat 2 (20–60s): Acknowledge what's true about the objection. "You're right that [specific concession]. I'm not going to tell you that's not real."
- Beat 3 (60–120s): Reveal the new information that resolves the objection. "But here's what most people miss: [specific reframe]. This changes [specific implication]."
- Beat 4 (120–180s): Specific evidence / case study supporting the resolution. "Here's a specific example: [specific worked case with detail]."
- Beat 5 (180–220s): Generalize back to the audience. "This is why [specific principle] applies to your situation, even if [specific edge case]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The belief-installation section explicitly handles objections rather than ignoring them. Beat 2's concession ("you're right that [concession]") is the load-bearing element — it disarms the audience's defensive posture. Without the concession, Beat 3's reframe reads as dismissal; with the concession, it reads as nuanced expertise.

## When to use
Authority-mode videos with conversion intent. Especially effective for high-ticket coaching content where audiences arrive with specific objections (cost, time, prerequisites, fit).
