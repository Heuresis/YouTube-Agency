# Script-Section Specimen 013 — Data-explanation body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (5–15s): Pose the section's question. "So why does [common assumption] not work in this context? The answer is in the data."
- Data anchor (60–120s): Walk the viewer through 1–3 specific data points. On-screen graphic per point. Each point gets a single sentence of explanation, not a paragraph.
- Synthesis (45–75s): Connect the points to the underlying principle. "These three things together tell us that [principle]. Here's why that matters for your decision."
- Application (60–90s): Translate the principle into a tactical move the viewer can make. Single-step clarity.
- Transition (5–15s): Bridge to next section. "Now that we understand [principle], the next question is [next section's question]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Data-explanation body sections earn retention because each on-screen graphic resets visual attention while delivering substantive content. The "single sentence per point" discipline (data anchor) is load-bearing — paragraph-length explanations of data points lose viewers who are processing the visual. The application step prevents the section from feeling academic.
