# Script-Section Specimen 034 — High-cut-density body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (5–8s): One-sentence section opener. Hard cut.
- Section structure: Each "beat" is 5–10s with a hard cut between beats.
- Cuts alternate between: talking-head, B-roll, on-screen text, archival footage, demonstration.
- Verbal pattern: short sentences, declarative voice, no hedging language.
- Mid-section question (every 60s): "Make sense? OK — here's the next layer."
- Section close (10–15s): Camera stabilizes, speaker delivers a single-sentence summary. "So that's [topic]. The takeaway: [single line]."
- Transition (5–10s): Hard cut to next section.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
High-cut-density body sections solve the retention problem at the cellular level — every 5–10 seconds the visual processor resets, preventing the slow drift of attention that kills mid-video retention. The "camera stabilizes for the section close" pattern (Beat 6) is the structural innovation; it signals "this is the takeaway, lock it in." Without the stabilization, the high-cut style feels exhausting rather than dense.
