# Script Section Specimen 012 — Rhetorical-question complexity-strip section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (5–15s): Rhetorical question that surfaces audience's likely confusion. "Confused? You're not alone."
- Beat 2 (15–45s): Acknowledge the complexity. "[Topic] gets complicated because [specific reason]. Most people get stuck at [specific stage]."
- Beat 3 (45–90s): Simplify with a specific framing. "Here's the simple version: [stripped-down framing]. That's it. Everything else is detail on top of that core idea."
- Beat 4 (90–120s): Build complexity back up. "Once you understand the core, you can layer in [specific advanced detail 1] and [specific advanced detail 2]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The rhetorical-question pattern interrupt uses the audience's own internal voice as the section opener. Beat 3's stripping-down to a simple framing is the load-bearing element — most educational content adds complexity to feel substantive; the highest-engagement educational content strips complexity down and then layers it back up.

## When to use
Sections where the audience is likely to feel overwhelmed or confused by the topic. Especially effective in technical / high-complexity niches where simplification is itself a differentiator.
