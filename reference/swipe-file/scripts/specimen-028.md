# Script-Section Specimen 028 — Objection-handling body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "I'm going to address the objections I know you're thinking right now."
- Objection 1 — Time (45–60s): "You might be thinking 'I don't have time for this.' Here's why that's the wrong way to think about it: [reframe]. The time investment is [specific amount] and the leverage is [specific multiplier]."
- Objection 2 — Money (45–60s): "You might think 'I can't afford this' or 'this is too expensive.' Let me address that directly: [reframe around cost-of-not-doing]."
- Objection 3 — Skill / capability (45–60s): "You might think 'this only works if you're [advanced trait].' Here's why that's wrong: [evidence that beginners can do this]."
- Objection 4 — Timing / market (45–60s): "You might think 'this won't work in my [market / context / situation].' Let me show you why it does: [adaptation logic]."
- Objection 5 — Trust (45–60s): "You might think 'this sounds too good to be true.' That's a healthy reflex. Here's how to test this on a small scale: [low-risk first step]."
- Transition (10–15s): "OK — objections handled. Let's get to the framework."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Objection-handling sections are the load-bearing conversion mechanism in authority videos because they neutralize the resistance that would otherwise block action. Naming the objection plainly (Beat 2's "you might be thinking...") is high-leverage — it shows the creator understands the audience and disarms hidden resistance. The "low-risk first step" close (Beat 6) converts skepticism into experimentation.
