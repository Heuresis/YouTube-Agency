# Script-Section Specimen 033 — Diagnostic-tree body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — here's the diagnostic I run when someone comes to me with [problem]. Let's walk through it together."
- Question 1 (45–60s): The first branch. "First question: [specific yes/no question]. If yes, you're dealing with [problem A]. If no, [different branch]."
- Question 2 (45–60s): Continue branching. "Assuming you said [answer to Q1], next: [specific question]. If yes, [diagnosis]. If no, [different diagnosis]."
- Question 3 (45–60s): Continue.
- Synthesis (45–60s): Tie back together. "The diagnostic gives you four possible profiles: [A, B, C, D]. Most people are [common profile], but the high-leverage cases are [different profile]."
- Application (45–60s): What to do based on diagnosis. "If you're profile [X], your move is [specific action]. If [Y], it's [different action]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now that you know which profile you are, the next layer is..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Diagnostic-tree body sections earn solution-aware-audience retention by treating the audience as cases rather than as a homogenous group — viewers self-diagnose along with the creator, which creates active engagement. The "high-leverage cases are [different profile]" framing (Beat 5) is the load-bearing element; it signals that the diagnostic isn't generic and there's a specific niche the creator is best at solving. Self-diagnosis is intrinsically retention-stable.
