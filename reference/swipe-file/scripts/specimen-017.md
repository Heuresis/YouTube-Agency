# Script-Section Specimen 017 — Study-citation body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — let's look at what the research actually says about this."
- Study setup (30–45s): Visible study citation on screen. "[Study type], published [year], [N participants], looked at [variable]."
- Findings (45–75s): Three specific findings. Each gets one sentence and one on-screen number. No paraphrasing of conclusions before the data.
- Limits (30–45s): Honest acknowledgment of what the study can't tell us. "What this study can't tell us is [specific limit]. So treat the conclusion as [specific qualifier]."
- Application (60–90s): Translate to training. "If you take this seriously, here's what it means for your weekly programming: [specific change]."
- Transition (10–15s): "There's one more piece we need before we can put it all together..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Study-citation sections earn evidence-led-audience retention by treating studies as evidence rather than authority — the explicit limits acknowledgment (Beat 4) is what separates this from the "studies show" rhetorical device. Showing limits paradoxically increases credibility because the audience trusts the creator's epistemic honesty. Translating to training (Beat 5) prevents the section from being academic.
