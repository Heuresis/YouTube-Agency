# Script-Section Specimen 029 — Emotional-arc body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — I want to slow down for a minute, because what I'm going to say next isn't tactical."
- Acknowledgment (45–60s): Validate the emotional state the audience is likely in. "If you're [N years] into this and not where you want to be, you're probably feeling [specific emotion]. That's normal. It doesn't mean you're broken."
- Reframe of the emotion (60–90s): "But here's what that emotion actually is: [reframe]. It's not [interpretation A]. It's [interpretation B]."
- Story of someone-like-you (60–90s): Brief anonymized example of someone who felt the same and got through. "I worked with [generic descriptor] last year. [N months] in, they were ready to quit. What changed for them was [specific shift]."
- Bridge back to tactical (30–45s): "I'm telling you this because the rest of the framework only works if you keep going. The emotional layer matters more than the tactics."
- Transition (10–15s): "OK — back to the build."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Emotional-arc sections are uniquely high-conversion because they meet the audience where they actually are — not in a tactical state, but in an emotional one. The "doesn't mean you're broken" framing (Beat 2) is the load-bearing empathy element. The story of someone-like-you (Beat 4) is what converts emotional acknowledgment into hope; without it, the section is just validation.
