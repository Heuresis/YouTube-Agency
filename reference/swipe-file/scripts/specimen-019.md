# Script-Section Specimen 019 — Visibility-pattern body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — here's the visibility pattern almost nobody is taught."
- Principle (30–45s): State the principle plainly. "Senior decision-makers don't promote based on [common assumption]. They promote based on [counterintuitive variable]."
- Mechanism (45–60s): Why the pattern exists. "From their seat, the calculus is [decision logic]. They're not being arbitrary — they're optimizing for [specific risk]."
- Tactical move (60–90s): What to do about it. "To shift this in your favor, you need to [specific change]. Here's what that looks like in practice."
- Common failure (30–45s): What people do wrong. "Most people respond to this advice by [common over-correction]. Don't do that. Do [more nuanced version]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now the next pattern stacks with this one — and you need both."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Visibility-pattern sections work in career content because they offer the audience access to insider perspective they can't get from public guidance. Beat 3's "from their seat, the calculus is..." framing is the load-bearing move — it positions the principle as the decision-maker's logic rather than abstract advice. The over-correction warning (Beat 5) prevents naive application.
