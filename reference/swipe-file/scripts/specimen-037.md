# Script-Section Specimen 037 — Delayed-reveal body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — I told you at the start I was going to show you [thing]. Now is the time."
- Delay step (30–45s): Add one more layer of context before the reveal. "But before I show you, you need to know [specific frame]. Without this, what you're about to see won't make sense."
- The reveal (30–60s): Show the thing. On screen, with adequate time for the visual to land. Speaker pauses.
- Reaction shot (15–20s): Speaker's authentic reaction or commentary. "When I first realized this, [reaction]."
- Implication (60–90s): Walk through what the reveal means. Specific implications, not generic.
- Action item (30–45s): What the viewer should do with this knowledge.
- Transition (10–15s): "And that brings us to the most important part of this whole video..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Delayed-reveal body sections work because they leverage anticipation — the loop opened in the hook is now closing, and the audience has waited specifically for this moment. The delay step (Beat 2) is counterintuitive but load-bearing; adding one more layer of context before the reveal increases the perceived weight of the reveal itself. The reaction shot (Beat 4) is the structural element that humanizes the reveal and makes it feel earned.
