# Script Section Specimen 003 — Logic-ladder analytical body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (10–20s): State the surprising claim. "Posting one good [content piece] earns more revenue than posting ten generic ones."
- Beat 2 (20–60s): Walk through the logic ladder step-by-step.
  - "When you post [content piece] with proper frameworks..."
  - "↓ You generate qualified traffic..."
  - "↓ Qualified traffic watches your content..."
  - "↓ They click your link in description..."
  - "↓ They land on a converting page..."
  - "↓ They book a call or buy."
- Beat 3 (60–80s): State the THEREFORE conclusion. "**THEREFORE**: posting one good [content piece] = real revenue. Posting ten generic ones = nothing."
- Beat 4 (80–100s): Apply the ladder to a specific worked example. "Look at [specific scenario]. Here's how the chain plays out: [walk through with real numbers]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The logic-ladder section converts an unbelievable claim into a chain of individually-plausible steps. The audience cannot dismiss the conclusion without dismissing every step in the chain — and chains of 5+ steps are nearly impossible to dismiss because each step is individually defensible. The worked example in Beat 4 anchors the abstract chain to a concrete case.

## When to use
Any time the section needs to establish a counterintuitive claim. Especially effective in business / financial / strategy content where audiences are primed to dismiss bold claims.
