# Script-Section Specimen 026 — Step-by-step tactical body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — let me walk you through exactly how I do this. Step by step."
- Setup (15–30s): What you need before you start. "You'll need [specific tools / resources]. If you don't have [item], use [alternative]."
- Step 1 (45–75s): Specific action. On-screen demonstration if possible. "First, [specific action]. The key here is [detail most people miss]."
- Step 2 (45–75s): Same structure. Each step's "key detail" beat is load-bearing.
- Step 3 (45–75s): Same structure.
- Step [N] (45–75s): Same structure for as many steps as needed.
- Common mistake (30–45s): "The most common mistake I see is [specific mistake]. Avoid that by [specific check]."
- Verification (30–45s): How to know you did it right. "You'll know this worked if [specific signal]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now you have the tactical layer. Let's stack it with the strategic layer."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Step-by-step tactical sections earn solution-aware-audience retention by being implementable — viewers can follow along. The "key detail most people miss" beat in each step is the load-bearing element; without it, the section reads as generic instruction rather than insider knowledge. The verification beat (Beat 8) closes the loop on each step's correctness and prevents post-video confusion.
