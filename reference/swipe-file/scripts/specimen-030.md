# Script-Section Specimen 030 — List-with-callback body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — here are the [N] things. I'm going to give you all of them, with examples."
- Item [N] structure (45–60s each): "[N]. [Item name]. [Brief explanation]. [Specific example]. [What makes this one matter most]."
- Mid-list callback (every 3–4 items, 15–20s): "Notice the pattern? Items [A], [B], [C] all share [common feature]. Hold on to that."
- Final item (60–90s): The most important / counterintuitive one gets extra time. Often the last item.
- Synthesis (30–45s): What the list adds up to. "These [N] together tell us [overarching principle]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now the question is — how do you actually start applying these?"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
List-with-callback sections solve the structural problem of list-based content (low retention because each item is independently dispensable) by introducing mid-list callbacks that link items into a meta-pattern. The callback (Beat 3) converts the list from a serial structure to a parallel one, increasing the cost of leaving. The synthesis at the end (Beat 5) elevates the list to a teaching.
