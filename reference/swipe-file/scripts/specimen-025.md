# Script-Section Specimen 025 — Belief-flip body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — here's the belief I need you to flip if any of this is going to work for you."
- Current belief (30–45s): Name the dominant belief explicitly. "Most people in [audience] believe [specific belief]. And they believe it because [reasonable origin]."
- Why it's wrong (60–90s): Walk through why the belief is structurally flawed. "But that belief breaks down when you look at [specific evidence]."
- New belief (30–45s): State the corrected belief. "What's actually true is [new belief]. Once you see this, you can't unsee it."
- Identity-check (45–60s): Connect the new belief to identity. "Believing this changes what kind of [audience descriptor] you are. It's not a tactic — it's a stance."
- Tactical implication (45–60s): What changes day-to-day. "When you operate from this belief, the tactics that follow are [specific]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now that we've flipped this, the next layer is..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Belief-flip sections are the highest-leverage authority pattern for high-ticket conversion because they install the cognitive infrastructure that makes the offer feel necessary. The "reasonable origin" framing (Beat 2) is the load-bearing empathy move; without it, the flip feels condescending. The identity-check (Beat 5) elevates the belief from intellectual position to self-concept, which is what drives conversion.
