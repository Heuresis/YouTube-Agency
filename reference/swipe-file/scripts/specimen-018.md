# Script-Section Specimen 018 — Deal-walkthrough body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "Let me walk you through this exact deal so you can see the mechanics."
- Deal spec (30–45s): On-screen — purchase price, financing structure, key terms. Single sentence each.
- Negotiation (60–90s): The specific moves made. "I asked for [specific concession]. Their counter was [response]. I responded with [next move]."
- Close (30–45s): How the deal closed. "We landed at [final terms]. Here's what made that possible: [specific factor]."
- Numbers (45–60s): The math. Cap rate, cash-on-cash, hold period, exit. On-screen calculation.
- Replication rules (30–45s): What conditions need to be true for the audience to do this. "You can replicate this if [conditions]. Don't try this if [contraindications]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now the question is — how do you find these in your market?"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Deal-walkthrough sections are the highest-trust authority asset in real-estate education because deals are concrete and verifiable. The "negotiation moves" detail (Beat 3) is the load-bearing element; without specific dialogue patterns, the walkthrough feels narrated rather than instructive. Replication rules (Beat 6) are critical — they prevent the section from feeling like a flex.
