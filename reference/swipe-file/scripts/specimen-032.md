# Script-Section Specimen 032 — Investigation-step body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — first thing I did was try to figure out [specific question]. Here's what I found."
- Method (30–45s): How the investigation step worked. "I [specific action: pulled the records, ran the math, talked to N people]."
- Finding (60–90s): What the step revealed. On-screen evidence.
- Implication (30–45s): What this finding meant for the larger question. "If this is true, then [specific implication]."
- New question (15–25s): What the finding raises next. "Which made me ask: [next investigation step]."
- Transition (10–15s): "So I went deeper..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Investigation-step body sections earn long-form retention by structuring the video as an unfolding mystery — each step opens the next. The "new question" beat (Beat 5) is the load-bearing element; without it, the section feels like a one-off finding rather than a mystery thread. The first-person "I went deeper" transition (Beat 6) maintains the investigative voice.
