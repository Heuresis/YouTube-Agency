# Script-Section Specimen 035 — Show-the-artifact body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "Let me actually show you the [artifact: dashboard / spreadsheet / contract / outline]."
- Artifact reveal (15–25s): On-screen, full-screen capture of the artifact. No talking-over. Let viewer absorb the visual for 5+ seconds.
- Walk-through (90–120s): Speaker narrates as cursor / overlay points to specific elements. "Here's [section]. This column is [purpose]. The reason this matters is [explanation]."
- Decision-points highlighted (60–90s): "When I'm using this, the high-leverage decisions are at [specific cells / lines]. Here's how I think through them."
- Common modification (45–60s): "Most operators will need to adapt this for [specific situation]. Here's what to change."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now you've seen the artifact. The next question is the underlying logic — let me walk you through that."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Show-the-artifact body sections earn unusually high authority because visible artifacts are the highest-trust evidence available in YouTube content — viewers can see the structure for themselves. The "absorb the visual for 5+ seconds" beat (Beat 2) is the load-bearing element; rushing past the artifact wastes its credibility weight. The common-modification beat (Beat 5) prevents the artifact from feeling proprietary and unattainable.
