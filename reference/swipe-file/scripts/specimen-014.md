# Script-Section Specimen 014 — Mini-case-study body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): Pose the diagnostic question. "How do you tell if [pattern] applies to your business? Let me show you with a real example."
- Setup (45–60s): Anonymized client setup. "[Generic descriptor]. Industry: [generic]. Stage: [stage]. Problem: [specific symptom]."
- Diagnosis (60–90s): Walk through what the operator saw. "When I looked at their [data / system / process], I found three things..."
- Intervention (60–90s): The change made. "We changed [specific thing] to [new state]. Specifically, [exact tactical move]."
- Result (30–45s): Outcome with numbers. "[N timeframe] later: [specific outcome]. The mechanism is what we'll cover next."
- Transition (10–15s): Bridge to mechanism explanation. "But this only works because of [underlying principle]. Let me explain..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Mini-case-study sections earn authority by grounding abstract teaching in concrete reality — a single specific case outweighs three paragraphs of generic principle. The "diagnosis → intervention → result" structure mirrors how the audience thinks about their own business problems, so the framework is implicitly portable. Anonymizing the client (Beat 1) is critical — it protects both the client and the creator from the "this only works for you" objection.
