# Script Section Specimen 010 — Five-emotion strategic-sequence body

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Beat 1 (Fear, 10–60s): "While you're [activity], your competitors are [taking advantage]. Every day you wait, [specific cost]."
- Beat 2 (Anger, 60–120s): "And here's what's worse — [external villain] doesn't want you to know this. They've been [specific manipulation] for [N years]."
- Beat 3 (Greed, 120–180s): "But here's the good news. Once you understand [mechanism], you can [specific positive outcome]. That's [specific quantified result]."
- Beat 4 (Guilt, 180–240s): "If you don't do this, you're letting [specific stake — family, future, potential] down. Your [stake holder] deserves better."
- Beat 5 (Pride, 240–300s): "But once you make this shift, you become the [specific identity — leader, expert, authority] in your space. People look to you for [specific authority outcome]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The 5-emotion arc maps directly to the strategic sequence (Fear → Anger → Greed → Guilt → Pride). Each beat serves a distinct cognitive function: Fear captures attention, Anger restores agency by externalizing the villain, Greed creates desire, Guilt creates internal pressure, Pride provides the identity claim that closes the arc.

## When to use
Long-form persuasion content (typically 10-minute+ videos with conversion intent). The arc spans the body of the video; each section approximately maps to one emotion. Less suitable for pure tactical content where the emotional arc would feel manipulative.
