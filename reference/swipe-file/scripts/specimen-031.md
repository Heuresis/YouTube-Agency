# Script-Section Specimen 031 — Wrong-vs-right body section

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (10–15s): "OK — let me show you the wrong way and the right way side-by-side. Once you see it, you can't unsee it."
- Wrong way (90–120s): Walk through the common approach. Don't strawman — describe it as someone using it would describe it. Specific actions, on-screen if possible.
- Why it fails (45–60s): Diagnose the structural failure. "This breaks down because [specific reason]. It can look like it's working short-term, but [longer-term failure mode]."
- Right way (90–120s): Walk through the corrected approach. Same structure as wrong way.
- Why it works (45–60s): Diagnose the structural success. "This works because [specific principle]. The key difference from the first approach is [specific delta]."
- Side-by-side recap (30–45s): Quick on-screen comparison. "Wrong way: [brief]. Right way: [brief]. The single most important difference: [delta]."
- Transition (10–15s): "Now you know the structure. Let me show you how to actually implement this."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Wrong-vs-right body sections work because they install the diagnostic capacity along with the right answer — viewers learn to recognize the failure pattern in their own behavior. The "don't strawman" discipline (Beat 2) is critical; if the wrong way is portrayed unfairly, viewers who currently use it disengage. The "single most important difference" recap (Beat 6) is the load-bearing memory anchor.
