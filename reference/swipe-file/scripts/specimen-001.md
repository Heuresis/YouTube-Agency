# Script Section Specimen 001 — Re-hook → Story → Lesson canonical pattern

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Re-hook (5–15s): Problem statement → Agitate → Delay solution. "Now, the average person trying to [goal] makes one critical mistake that guarantees [negative outcome]. This mistake has [amplify stakes]. And it's probably the same mistake you're about to make. But what exactly is this mistake? Well, to understand this, I first need to tell you about..."
- Story (45–90s): BUT-THEREFORE narrative structure — Arrival → Adversity → Aftermath. "[Character] started [activity] the same way everyone does — [generic starting state]. For [N timeframe], they did [generic effort]. Their results were [low outcome]. **BUT** they kept [activity] because [conventional advice]. **THEREFORE**, they [exhaustion / failure state]. **BUT** then [pivot moment]. What they discovered in [short timeframe of pivot] [specific transformation]."
- Lesson (60–120s): Deliver the promised value. "The lesson here is [specific principle]. Here's how to apply it to your own [activity]: Step 1 — [specific action]. Step 2 — [specific action]. Step 3 — [specific action]. The reason this works is [mechanism explanation]. Specific example: [worked case]."
- Transition (5–15s): Bridge to next section using one of the 7 transition types.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The re-hook → story → lesson pattern is the canonical body-section structure. Each beat serves a distinct cognitive function: re-hook recaptures attention, story creates emotional investment, lesson delivers value. The transition prevents momentum loss between sections.

## When to use
Every body section of every long-form (8-minute+) educational video. The pattern repeats 3–8 times per video depending on length. Skipping the re-hook produces predictable drop-off at section boundaries.
