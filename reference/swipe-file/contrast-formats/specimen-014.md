# Contrast-Format Specimen 014 — Same task, different methods, side-by-side

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I gave myself a task. I tried [N] completely different methods. The differences in time, quality, and effort were extreme."
- The task definition (45–60s): Specific outcome the audience can evaluate.
- Method 1 walkthrough (2–3 min): Approach, execution, result, time spent.
- Method 2 walkthrough (2–3 min): Same.
- Method 3 walkthrough (2–3 min): Same.
- Method N walkthrough (2–3 min): Same.
- Side-by-side comparison (1–2 min): Time, quality, effort matrix.
- The winner and why (1–2 min): "[Method X] won because [structural reason]."
- The surprising loser (1 min): "I expected [method] to be best — it was actually [worse than expected]."
- Decision rule (1 min): "When should you use [winning method]? When should you not?"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Same-task-different-methods contrast formats earn retention because each method-walkthrough is a mini-arc with its own resolution. The "surprising loser" beat (Beat: surprising loser) is the load-bearing curiosity element; without it, the format reads as a predictable winner-takes-all. Decision rules (final beat) convert the format from entertainment to applied teaching.
