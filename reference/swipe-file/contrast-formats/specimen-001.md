# Contrast-Format Specimen 001 — 1-vs-N "I tried both" head-to-head

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Setup (0:00–0:30): Frame the head-to-head. "I spent [N timeframe] doing [Side A]. Then I spent [equal timeframe] doing [Side B]. Here's what happened."
- Side A walkthrough (1:00–4:00): What was tried, how it was done, what the daily reality was.
- Side A results (30–60s): Specific outcome metrics.
- Side B walkthrough (1:00–4:00): Same structure, equal time.
- Side B results (30–60s): Specific outcome metrics.
- Direct comparison (1:00–2:00): Side-by-side numerical comparison. Visual graphic.
- Verdict + reasoning (1:00–2:00): "[Side X] won. The reason isn't what you think."
- What I'd do now (45–60s): "Knowing what I know, here's the move."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The 1-vs-N contrast format earns retention because the comparison is the value proposition — viewers stay to see the verdict. Equal time on both sides (Beats 2–5) is structurally critical; if one side gets short shrift, viewers detect bias and disengage. The "what I'd do now" close (Beat 8) converts the comparison from horse-race to actionable advice.
