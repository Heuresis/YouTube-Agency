# Contrast-Format Specimen 004 — How-rich-vs-poor-do-X format

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "Most people do [common behavior] one way. The [N%] who actually [outcome] do it completely differently. Let me show you the specific deltas."
- The "common" approach (2–3 min): Walk through what the average person does. Specific behaviors. Don't strawman.
- Why it doesn't compound (1–2 min): Diagnose the structural failure of the common approach.
- The "uncommon" approach (3–4 min): Walk through what the high-outcome cohort does differently. Specific behaviors. Often counterintuitive.
- Why it compounds (1–2 min): The structural advantage.
- The N specific behavioral deltas (2–3 min): Side-by-side list of what's different. Each delta gets one sentence.
- The cognitive shift (1–2 min): What stance / belief produces the uncommon approach.
- Tactical entry point (1–2 min): The lowest-friction way to start adopting the uncommon approach.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Stratification contrast formats work because they offer the audience access to the cohort they aspire to join — the structural deltas feel like insider knowledge. The "don't strawman the common approach" discipline (Beat 2) is critical; if the common cohort feels mocked, viewers using common tactics disengage. The cognitive-shift beat (Beat 7) is what differentiates this format from a tactic-list — it operates at the identity level.
