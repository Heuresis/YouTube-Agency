# Contrast-Format Specimen 013 — Market A vs Market B for [strategy] format

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I ran the same [strategy] in [Market A] and [Market B]. Same playbook, completely different outcomes. Here's why."
- The strategy held constant (1 min): What was the same in both deployments.
- Market A — characteristics (2 min): Demographics, supply, demand, regulatory environment.
- Market A — execution (2 min): What the deals actually looked like.
- Market A — outcomes (1–2 min): Specific numbers.
- Market B — characteristics (2 min): Same structure.
- Market B — execution (2 min): Same.
- Market B — outcomes (1–2 min): Specific numbers.
- The variables that mattered most (1–2 min): "It came down to [N] specific market characteristics."
- How to evaluate your market (1–2 min): "Here's the diagnostic to apply to your local market."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Market-comparison contrast formats work in real estate because the audience needs to translate examples to their own market — the diagnostic at the end (final beat) is the highest-value element. The "same playbook, different outcomes" framing (Beat 1) is the load-bearing curiosity-anchor; it forces the audience to wonder which market characteristics matter. Naming the specific variables (Beat: variables that mattered) converts the comparison from anecdote to teaching.
