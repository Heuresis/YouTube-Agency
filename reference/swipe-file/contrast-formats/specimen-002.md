# Contrast-Format Specimen 002 — Before-vs-after transformation arc

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Before snapshot (0:00–0:30): "Six months ago, I was [vulnerable starting state]. Here's what that actually looked like."
- Specific metrics on entry (30–60s): On-screen numbers / measurements / state-descriptors.
- The decision moment (45–60s): "Then [specific catalyst]. I decided to do [specific change]."
- The process — first phase (2–3 min): Day-to-day reality of weeks 1–4. Honest about boring / hard parts.
- The process — second phase (2–3 min): Weeks 5–12. Inflection point. Show the moment things started working.
- The process — third phase (2–3 min): Final phase. Compounding.
- After snapshot (1–2 min): Specific metrics on exit.
- The lesson (1–2 min): What the audience can extract. The principle that drove the transformation.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Before-vs-after contrast formats work because the audience projects themselves into the before-state and follows the arc emotionally. The honesty about boring / hard parts (Beat 4) is the load-bearing trust element; polished transformation arcs read as fake. The lesson at the end (Beat 8) converts entertainment into teaching, which is what makes the video re-shareable.
