# Contrast-Format Specimen 024 — Extreme protocol vs moderate protocol test

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I tried [extreme version] of [domain] for [timeframe]. Then I tried [moderate version] for the same time. The result calls into question what 'optimal' even means."
- Extreme phase (3–4 min): What it required, how it felt, what broke down.
- Extreme phase outcomes (1–2 min): Specific results, including hidden costs (sleep, mood, sustainability).
- Moderate phase (3–4 min): Same structure.
- Moderate phase outcomes (1–2 min): Same.
- The extreme paradox (1–2 min): "Extreme protocols look better in the short window I tested. But [hidden cost factor] makes them worse over [longer timeframe]."
- The sustainability metric (1–2 min): "If you ran each protocol for a year, the moderate one almost certainly wins on [outcome]."
- Recommendation by personality (1–2 min): "Some people thrive on extreme. Most don't. Here's how to know which you are."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Extreme-vs-moderate contrast formats earn retention because they engage the audience's curiosity about high-intensity protocols while delivering nuance most niche content lacks. The "extreme paradox" beat (Beat: extreme paradox) is the load-bearing element; it acknowledges the appeal of extreme while honestly costing it. Recommendation by personality (final beat) prevents the format from being prescriptive.
