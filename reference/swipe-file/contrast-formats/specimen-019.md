# Contrast-Format Specimen 019 — Generation-vs-generation format

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "What worked for the previous generation in [domain] doesn't work for ours. Here's why — and what does."
- Their context (2–3 min): The structural realities the previous generation operated in. Specific economic / technological / cultural variables.
- Their playbook (1–2 min): What they did. Why it worked given their context.
- Our context (2–3 min): What's structurally different now. Specific deltas.
- Why their playbook breaks (1–2 min): The mechanism of failure when their playbook is applied to our context.
- Our playbook (3–4 min): What actually works given the new structural realities.
- The continuity (1–2 min): What still applies from their generation, despite the structural shifts.
- Synthesis (1–2 min): "Don't reject everything they did. Update what doesn't apply, keep what does."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Generation-vs-generation contrast formats earn retention because the audience is intuitively aware their parents' / mentors' advice doesn't fully apply but lacks structured articulation of why. The "what still applies" beat (Beat: continuity) is the load-bearing element that distinguishes this format from generation-bashing; it shows respect for the prior generation's wisdom while updating the playbook. Synthesis prevents tribal alignment.
