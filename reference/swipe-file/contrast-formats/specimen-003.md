# Contrast-Format Specimen 003 — Then-vs-now era comparison

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame the era contrast (0:00–0:30): "[Domain] in [past year] vs [current year] is almost unrecognizable. Let me show you exactly what's changed."
- Then snapshot (2–3 min): What the past era looked like. Specific examples, archival imagery if possible.
- What worked then (1–2 min): The strategies / approaches that succeeded in that era.
- The shifts (3–4 min): What changed. Three to five specific structural shifts. Each gets its own segment.
- Now snapshot (2–3 min): What the current era looks like. Specific examples.
- What works now (1–2 min): The strategies that succeed today.
- The bridge (1–2 min): How to tell which era's strategies you're still operating from. Diagnostic.
- Adaptation guide (1–2 min): How to update your approach.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Then-vs-now era comparisons earn retention because the audience knows things have changed but lacks a structured account of the deltas. The diagnostic in Beat 7 ("which era's strategies are you still operating from?") is the load-bearing element; it converts the comparison from observation to self-assessment. The adaptation guide makes the video actionable rather than nostalgic.
