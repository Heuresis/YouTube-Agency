# Contrast-Format Specimen 020 — Macro view vs micro view comparison

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "Most operators only ever look at [domain] at one zoom level. The high performers move between two zoom levels. Here's both."
- Macro view (3–4 min): What the system / market / domain looks like from a strategic distance. Big picture.
- What macro reveals (1–2 min): The patterns visible only at this scale.
- Micro view (3–4 min): The same domain at the cell level. Day-to-day actions, specific decisions.
- What micro reveals (1–2 min): The patterns visible only at this scale.
- The mismatch (1–2 min): "What looks right at macro can look wrong at micro, and vice versa."
- The synthesis (1–2 min): How to operate at both levels simultaneously.
- Tactical application (1–2 min): "When to zoom out, when to zoom in, and what triggers the switch."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Zoom-out-vs-zoom-in contrast formats earn solution-aware-audience retention because they install a meta-cognitive skill — knowing which level to operate from. The "mismatch" beat (Beat: mismatch) is the load-bearing element; it names a tension the audience experiences without knowing how to articulate. The tactical application converts the meta-skill into operational behavior.
