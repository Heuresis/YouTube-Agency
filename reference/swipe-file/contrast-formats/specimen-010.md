# Contrast-Format Specimen 010 — Place A vs Place B comparison format

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I lived in [Place A] for [N timeframe] and [Place B] for [equal timeframe]. Same lifestyle, different city. Here's what surprised me."
- Place A overview (2–3 min): Day-to-day reality. Cost-of-living. Pace. Texture.
- Place A trade-offs (1–2 min): What you give up.
- Place B overview (2–3 min): Same structure.
- Place B trade-offs (1–2 min): What you give up.
- Direct comparison (1–2 min): Side-by-side cost / experience graphic.
- The unexpected variable (1–2 min): "The biggest difference wasn't [obvious variable]. It was [counterintuitive variable]."
- Recommendation by persona (1–2 min): "If you're [persona A], lean toward [Place X]. If you're [persona B], the opposite."
- The meta-lesson (45–60s): "What this taught me about choosing where to live..."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Place-comparison contrast formats earn retention because they tap into the universal "where should I live" question without preaching. The "biggest difference wasn't obvious" beat (Beat: unexpected variable) is the load-bearing curiosity-anchor; without it, the comparison reads as a tour. Recommendation by persona prevents the video from feeling prescriptive, which the audience resists.
