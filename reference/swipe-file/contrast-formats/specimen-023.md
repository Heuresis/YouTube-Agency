# Contrast-Format Specimen 023 — Free resources vs paid course test

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I tried to learn [domain] using only free resources for [N months]. Then I bought [paid course] and went through it. Here's what was actually different."
- Free-resource phase (3–4 min): What I consumed, how I tried to apply, where I got stuck.
- Free-resource outcomes (1–2 min): Specific skill levels reached.
- Paid-course phase (3–4 min): What was different, what was emphasized, what frameworks emerged.
- Paid-course outcomes (1–2 min): Specific skill levels reached.
- The structural difference (1–2 min): "It wasn't the information. It was [specific structural variable: sequence, accountability, application loop]."
- When free is enough (1–2 min): "If you're [conditions], free resources are sufficient."
- When paid is worth it (1–2 min): "If you're [conditions], paid courses are leveraged."
- The honest conclusion (1 min): "I wasted [N months] on free resources because of [common mistake]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Free-vs-paid contrast formats earn retention because the audience is constantly evaluating whether to invest in courses. The "I wasted N months" honest conclusion (final beat) is the load-bearing trust element; without it, the format reads as a paid-course advertisement. The "structural difference" beat (Beat: structural difference) is what differentiates this from a pro-paid screed — it names what specifically the paid course delivers beyond information.
