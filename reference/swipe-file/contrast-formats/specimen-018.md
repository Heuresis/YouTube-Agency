# Contrast-Format Specimen 018 — Solo vs team output comparison

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I ran [project type] solo for [N timeframe]. Then with a team of [size]. The output difference wasn't what I expected."
- Solo phase (3–4 min): What got built, how, what the trade-offs were.
- Solo phase metrics (1 min): Output, hours, revenue, sustainability.
- Team phase (3–4 min): Same structure with the team in place.
- Team phase metrics (1 min): Output, hours, revenue, overhead.
- The unexpected delta (1–2 min): "What I didn't expect was [counterintuitive variable that mattered most]."
- The breakeven analysis (1–2 min): When does adding the team actually pay off, and when does it not?
- The hidden cost of either (1–2 min): "Solo costs you [hidden cost]. Team costs you [hidden cost]. Choose based on which you'd rather pay."
- Decision rule (1 min): "Stay solo if [conditions]. Hire if [conditions]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Solo-vs-team contrast formats work for operator audiences because the hire-or-not decision is universal. The "hidden cost of either" beat (Beat: hidden costs) is the load-bearing element; honest admission that both paths have costs paradoxically increases credibility. The breakeven analysis is what differentiates this format from generic "you should hire" content.
