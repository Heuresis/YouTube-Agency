# Contrast-Format Specimen 006 — Beginner-vs-pro mistake format

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "Here are [N] mistakes beginners make in [domain] — and what pros do instead."
- Mistake 1 — Beginner (1 min): What beginners do. Show or describe specifically.
- Mistake 1 — Pro (1 min): What pros do instead. Show / describe.
- Why the delta matters (45–60s): "This single change separates [common outcome] from [pro outcome]."
- Repeat for mistakes 2 through N (depending on length).
- Synthesis (1–2 min): "Notice the meta-pattern — beginners optimize for [variable A], pros optimize for [variable B]."
- Self-diagnostic (1 min): "Which of these are you currently doing? Be honest."
- Tactical priority (1 min): "If you fix one, fix [most leveraged one] first."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Beginner-vs-pro contrast formats earn retention because the audience self-diagnoses along with the content — every mistake is an opportunity for self-recognition. The synthesis beat that names the meta-pattern (Beat: synthesis) is what differentiates this format from a tactic-list. The self-diagnostic ("which of these are you doing? Be honest.") converts watchers into participants.
