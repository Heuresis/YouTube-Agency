# Contrast-Format Specimen 015 — Constraint test: with vs without [variable]

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Frame (0:00–0:30): "I removed [common variable] from my [domain] for [N timeframe]. Then I added it back. The difference was bigger than I expected."
- The setup (1–2 min): What the variable was, why it was removed, the test conditions.
- Phase 1 — without (2–3 min): Daily reality. How it felt. What outcomes shifted. On-camera reactions.
- Phase 1 — measured outcomes (1–2 min): Specific data.
- Phase 2 — with (2–3 min): Same structure.
- Phase 2 — measured outcomes (1–2 min): Specific data.
- Direct comparison (1–2 min): Side-by-side data graphic.
- The mechanism (1–2 min): "What I think is actually happening when [variable] is in the system is [explanation]."
- Recommendation (1–2 min): "Should you remove it / keep it? Depends on [conditions]."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Constraint-test contrast formats earn retention because removing a variable is more dramatic than adding one — the audience watches to see what breaks. The on-camera reactions during the without-phase (Beat: phase 1 without) are the load-bearing emotional element; clean voice-over removes the visceral reality of constraint-living. The mechanism beat (Beat: mechanism) elevates the format from stunt to teaching.
