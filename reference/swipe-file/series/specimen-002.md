# Series-Structure Specimen 002 — 4-video cohort launch series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Video 1 (released 4 weeks before cohort opens) — The Problem-Anchor: Deep dive on the specific painful problem the cohort solves. No mention of cohort.
- Video 2 (3 weeks out) — The Mechanism: The framework / process you use. Demonstrates the cohort's intellectual depth without selling.
- Video 3 (2 weeks out) — The Worked Example: A case study of someone (anonymized) who used the framework. Hints at cohort.
- Video 4 (1 week out) — The Open: Full cohort launch. Includes recap of the framework, what the cohort is, who it's for, who it's not for, application process.
- Companion email sequence: Synchronized with each video release. Adds depth, addresses objections by segment.
- Final 48-hour close: Email-only urgency push.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
The 4-video cohort launch series compresses the standard 8-belief sequence into a tighter window because cohort scarcity creates natural urgency. Video 1's "no mention of cohort" discipline is structurally critical; if the launch is named too early, the audience reads the entire series as a sales sequence and disengages. The email companion sequence handles segment-specific objections that would slow the videos' pacing.
