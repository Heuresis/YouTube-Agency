# Series-Structure Specimen 006 — Client-transformation documentation series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Video 1 — The starting state: Anonymized client introduction. Their specific problem, their specific stuckness, what we agreed to try.
- Video 2 — Phase 1 work: First [N weeks]. What we did, what shifted, what didn't.
- Video 3 — The breakthrough: The moment things started working. What changed structurally.
- Video 4 — Scaling the win: How we expanded the breakthrough.
- Video 5 — The honest retrospective: What worked, what we'd do differently, what the client says about it now.
- Inter-video callbacks: Each video references the client's starting state and previous episode's progress.
- Anonymization discipline: No identifying info; client is described by archetype and stage.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Client-transformation series earn high-ticket-audience trust because they prove the creator can produce results in a longitudinal, non-cherry-picked frame. The "what we'd do differently" beat in the retrospective (Video 5) is the load-bearing trust element; pretending the engagement was perfect undermines credibility. Anonymization protects the client and prevents the series from feeling like marketing.
