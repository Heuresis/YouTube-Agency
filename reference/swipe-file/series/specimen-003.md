# Series-Structure Specimen 003 — Multi-part documentary series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Episode 1 — The setup: Introduce the central question / mystery / story. Establish the world and the stakes.
- Episode 2 — Expansion: Go deeper into the world. Introduce the second layer of complexity.
- Episode 3 — Complication: Something unexpected emerges. The original frame turns out to be incomplete.
- Episode 4 — Confrontation: The audience is brought face-to-face with the most uncomfortable truth in the series.
- Episode 5 — Resolution: Synthesis. What it all means. What the audience should take away.
- Inter-episode callbacks: Each episode references the previous; the synthesis episode references all four.
- Visual continuity: Shared color grading, music, title-card style across all episodes.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Multi-part documentary series earn cumulative engagement that single videos can't — the audience commits to the full arc, which compounds attention across releases. Visual continuity (shared grading, music) is the load-bearing element; without it, episodes feel disconnected. The "complication" episode (Episode 3) is structurally critical; without an unexpected turn, the series feels predictable and viewers don't return for Episodes 4 and 5.
