# Series-Structure Specimen 009 — "Things you've been told that are wrong" series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Video 1 — Belief 1 corrected: Name a dominant belief in the niche, walk through why it's wrong, replace with the corrected belief.
- Video 2 — Belief 2 corrected: Same structure.
- Video N — Belief N corrected: Same.
- Final video — The synthesis: "Notice that all of these beliefs share a common error pattern. Here's what's actually going on at the meta-level."
- Cross-video continuity: Each video references the previous corrections; the synthesis references all.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Belief-correction series earn solution-aware-audience loyalty by systematically dismantling the dominant content patterns in the niche — each video is a differentiation event. The synthesis episode (final video) is the load-bearing structural move; it elevates the series from a list of contrarian takes to a coherent worldview, which is conversion-driving. The "common error pattern" framing is what installs the creator's frame as the new default.
