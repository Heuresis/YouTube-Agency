# Series-Structure Specimen 015 — Reach-to-authority funnel series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Phase 1 — Reach (Videos 1–3): Three high-CTR reach videos that pull broad audience. No back-end mention. Pure value.
- Phase 2 — Bridge (Video 4): A video that names the deeper question the reach videos hinted at. First soft mention of next step.
- Phase 3 — Authority (Videos 5–7): Three authority videos that go deep on the framework. Soft CTAs to the back-end.
- Phase 4 — Conversion (Video 8): The full open. Recap, mechanism, offer, application.
- Cross-video continuity: Each video references the previous; Video 8 references all.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Reach-to-authority funnel series solve the channel-architecture problem of needing both broad reach and conversion-driving depth — the series structures the migration from one to the other. Phase 1's "no back-end mention" discipline is structurally critical; if the reach videos sell, they fail at reach. The bridge video (Video 4) is the load-bearing transition; without it, the audience that came for reach feels bait-and-switched when authority content appears.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: both (reach in Phase 1, authority in Phases 2–4)
- audience awareness: unaware (Phase 1) to product-aware (Phase 4)
- creator persona compatibility: any (the structure is style-agnostic)
- channel stage: 100K–1M
- back-end offer fit: any (especially for channels that want both reach and back-end conversion)

## Production notes
- Length per video: variable (8–14 min for reach, 14–22 min for authority)
- Total series length: 8 videos over 6–8 weeks
- Production complexity: medium-high (consistent visual identity across mode-shift)
- Release cadence: weekly
