# Series-Structure Specimen 004 — Framework-decomposition series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Video 1 — The framework overview: Introduce the full framework at high altitude. Name all components.
- Video 2 — Component 1 deep-dive: Single component, complete depth. Worked examples.
- Video 3 — Component 2 deep-dive: Same.
- Video N — Component N deep-dive: Same.
- Final video — The synthesis: How the components interact. Common failure modes when components are missed. The integration practice.
- Inter-video callbacks: Each component-video references the framework overview. The synthesis references all components.
- Companion artifact: Often a one-page framework summary (lead magnet) that ties the series together.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Framework-decomposition series earn solution-aware-audience loyalty by treating each framework component as worth its own video — depth is the differentiation. The synthesis episode (final video) is the load-bearing structural element; without it, the series feels like disconnected component-explainers. The companion lead magnet is what converts the series into a list-building asset.

## Production notes
- Length per video: 14–22 minutes
- Total series length: 4–7 videos depending on framework complexity
- Production complexity: medium-high (consistent visual identity)
- Sequencing: viewer should be able to watch in any order, but recommended order should be clear
