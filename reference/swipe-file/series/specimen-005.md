# Series-Structure Specimen 005 — Multi-week challenge documentation series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Episode 0 — The setup: I'm starting a [specific challenge] for [N weeks]. Here's why and what success / failure looks like.
- Episode 1 — Week 1: What happened. Honest about hard parts. Mid-week footage included.
- Episode 2 — Week 2: Same structure. Pattern emerging.
- Episode N — Week N: Same.
- Final episode — The retrospective: Aggregate outcomes. What I learned. What I'd do differently. How others can run a similar experiment.
- Companion live thread: Often a live community post or pinned comment for real-time updates.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Multi-week challenge series earn cumulative engagement because the audience commits to seeing the resolution — they want to know if the creator succeeds. Episode 0's "what failure looks like" framing (Beat: Episode 0) is the load-bearing element; defining failure conditions in advance increases stakes and earns trust. The retrospective episode converts the challenge from entertainment to teaching.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: reach
- audience awareness: unaware
- creator persona compatibility: experimental / vulnerable / first-person
- channel stage: 10K–100K, 100K–1M
- back-end offer fit: any (top-of-funnel format)

## Production notes
- Length per episode: 8–15 minutes
- Total series length: 5–9 episodes (Episode 0 + N week episodes + retrospective)
- Production complexity: very high (multi-week shoot)
- Release cadence: weekly to mirror challenge timeline
