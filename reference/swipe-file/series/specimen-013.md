# Series-Structure Specimen 013 — Comparative-test series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Episode 1 — Test 1: Two approaches compared on [variable A].
- Episode 2 — Test 2: Two approaches compared on [variable B].
- Episode 3 — Test 3: Two approaches compared on [variable C].
- Each episode follows the same internal contrast structure.
- Final episode — The meta-pattern: "Across all [N] tests, here's what we kept seeing."
- Recommendation by use-case (final episode): "Given everything we tested, here's how to choose for your situation."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Comparative-test series earn engagement because each episode is an independent comparison while the series adds up to a meta-conclusion the single episodes can't deliver. The final episode's "meta-pattern" (Beat: meta-pattern) is the load-bearing element; without it, the series feels like disconnected comparisons. Recommendation by use-case prevents the final episode from being prescriptive across contexts where it shouldn't be.
