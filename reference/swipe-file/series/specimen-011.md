# Series-Structure Specimen 011 — Investigative reveal series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Episode 1 — The question opens: Pose the central investigative question. Establish what's known, what's not, why it matters.
- Episode 2 — First findings: The initial investigation step reveals X. New questions emerge.
- Episode 3 — The complication: A finding that contradicts the working hypothesis.
- Episode 4 — Going deeper: New methodology / source / interview that breaks the problem open.
- Final episode — The reveal: What we learned. The honest synthesis.
- Each-episode close: Cliffhanger that names the next investigation step.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Investigative reveal series earn unusual cumulative engagement because each episode opens new questions while closing some — the audience must watch the next to resolve the current. The cliffhanger discipline (each-episode close) is load-bearing; without it, episodes feel self-contained and the series doesn't compound. The honest synthesis at the end (final episode) protects the series from reading as drama-bait.
