# Series-Structure Specimen 014 — Implementation walkthrough series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Video 1 — Setup: What you need to start. Tools, accounts, foundational decisions.
- Video 2 — Phase 1 implementation: Specific actions for the first [timeframe].
- Video 3 — Phase 2 implementation: Specific actions for the next phase.
- Video N — Phase N implementation: Same.
- Final video — Optimization: Once it's working, here's how to refine and scale.
- Companion artifacts: Templates, checklists, or workspace files released alongside each video.
- Cross-video continuity: Each video references previous phases and previews the next.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Implementation walkthrough series earn solution-aware-audience completion rates because they remove the cognitive overhead of figuring out "what do I do next?" — the series itself is the playbook. Companion artifacts (Beat: companion artifacts) are load-bearing; without them, the implementation series leaves the audience to recreate the artifacts the creator is using, which collapses completion rates. Cross-video continuity is what creates curriculum-feel.
