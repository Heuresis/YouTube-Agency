# Series-Structure Specimen 008 — Build-in-public series

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Episode 0 — The concept: What I'm building, why, what success looks like.
- Episode 1 — Foundation laid: First [N weeks] of work. What got built, what didn't.
- Episode 2 — First feedback: Early signal. What's resonating, what's not.
- Episode 3 — The pivot or persist moment: Decision point. Show the deliberation.
- Episode 4+ — Iteration cycles: Each subsequent episode shows another iteration.
- Episode N — The launch / outcome: What we shipped, what the response was, what's next.
- Companion changelog: Ongoing pinned post or community thread for real-time updates.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson — 2–4 sentences)
Build-in-public series earn cumulative engagement because the audience invests in the creator's project alongside them — each episode is both content and progress report. The "pivot or persist" episode (Episode 3) is the load-bearing dramatic element; without a real decision moment, the series feels rehearsed. The honest outcome (Episode N) is essential — softening or hyping the launch breaks the trust the series accumulated.

## When this specimen is in the candidate pool
- mode: reach
- audience awareness: unaware
- creator persona compatibility: vulnerable / first-person / experimental
- channel stage: 10K–100K, 100K–1M
- back-end offer fit: any (the build itself becomes the offer)

## Production notes
- Length per episode: 10–18 minutes
- Total series length: variable (typically 6–12 episodes)
- Production complexity: high (longitudinal documentation)
- Release cadence: bi-weekly to monthly
