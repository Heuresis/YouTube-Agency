# Thumbnail Specimen 011 — Face-plus-chart split

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Left half — operator's face with skeptical / concerned expression. Right half — simple bar or line chart with a single dramatic data point highlighted (red or yellow).
- Text overlay: 2–4 words that name the shocking statistic. Bold sans-serif, often white with dark outline.
- Color: 2–3 colors max — usually warm-tone face vs cold-tone chart for contrast.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Face-plus-chart thumbnails activate two cognitive channels at once — the face triggers the social-evaluation circuit (am I seeing concern or confidence?), and the chart triggers numerical curiosity (what is that number?). The face's expression is load-bearing; neutral expressions underperform clearly readable emotional states. Single-data-point highlighting outperforms full-chart visibility because it directs attention.
