# Thumbnail Specimen 001 — Before-vs-after split-frame

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Split-frame thumbnail. Left side shows the operator (or a representative figure) in a vulnerable / failed / low-status state. Right side shows the operator in a confident / successful / high-status state. Optional: divider in the middle with bold text.
- Text overlay: 2–4 words that anchor the contrast (e.g., "BEFORE / AFTER," "$0 → $100K," "FROM CONFUSED TO CLARITY").

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The before-vs-after thumbnail activates the comparative-self mechanism at the visual layer. The viewer auto-calculates "could I make this transition?" before the title or video has had a chance to deliver evidence. The split-frame is the load-bearing structural element — without the explicit visual divider, the contrast is lost.

## When to use
Personal-transformation videos, case-study videos, identity-pivot videos. Less effective for tactical / advisory content where the visual contrast doesn't make sense.
