# Thumbnail Specimen 020 — Day-counter experiment thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Operator's face with shocked / surprised expression dominates left two-thirds. Right third shows a calendar grid or large day-count number ("DAY 30").
- Text overlay: 2–4 words reveal-tease ("THE RESULTS" or "DIDN'T EXPECT THIS"). Bold, sans-serif.
- Color: 2–3 colors max, with high contrast between face and counter.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Day-counter experiment thumbnails work because the day-count signals duration and commitment, while the face signals an unexpected outcome. The combination triggers two cognitive responses simultaneously — "they did this for N days?" and "and the result was unexpected enough to surprise them?" Both compound into high-CTR.

- mode: reach
- audience awareness: unaware

- channel stage: 10K–100K, 100K–1M
