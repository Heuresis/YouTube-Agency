# Thumbnail Specimen 014 — Split-frame logo verdict thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Two technology / tool logos side-by-side, divided by a vertical line or large "VS" text.
- Verdict element: A bright-color callout (often green checkmark on one side, red X or muted gray on the other).
- Text overlay: 3–5 words featuring the verdict ("[Tool A] WON" or "I SWITCHED").

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Split-frame logo thumbnails work for tech-vs-tech content because the audience is comparing the same options the title and thumbnail name. The visible verdict element is the load-bearing structural choice — without it, the thumbnail looks like another neutral comparison. The audience will click specifically to see why the creator chose the side they did.

## When to use
- mode: authority
- audience awareness: solution-aware
- pairs with: tech-vs-tech opinionated title, AID-formula hook
- channel stage: 10K–100K
