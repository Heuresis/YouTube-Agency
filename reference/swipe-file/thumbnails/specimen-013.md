# Thumbnail Specimen 013 — Minimalist-text thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Single-color background (often warm neutral — cream, sand, soft black). No face, no person.
- Text: 4–7 words in large sans-serif, centered or left-aligned. Typography is the design.
- Color: 1–2 colors max (background + text, occasional accent).

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Minimalist-text thumbnails work in philosophical / essayist niches because they signal cognitive depth and stand out in a feed dominated by maximalist designs. The absence of a face is counterintuitive but high-leverage; it filters for the audience that doesn't need parasocial cues. The typography itself becomes the differentiator — the audience associates the visual style with the channel.

## When to use
- mode: reach
- audience awareness: unaware

- channel stage: 100K–1M, 1M+
- best for: philosophical / essayist creators with strong visual identity
