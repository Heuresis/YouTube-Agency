# Thumbnail Specimen 006 — Arrow-and-question

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: The operator's face on one side. An arrow pointing from their face to a specific element (a screen, a product, an artifact) on the other side.
- Text overlay: A short question ("HOW?", "WHY?", "WHAT IS THIS?") sized to dominate the remaining frame.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The arrow-and-question thumbnail explicitly invites the viewer to seek the answer. The visual flow (face → arrow → artifact → question) creates a directional narrative the eye traces in 1–2 seconds. The question must be the simplest possible form; longer questions reduce CTR.

## When to use
Curiosity-gap titles where the visual artifact is intriguing on its own. Less effective when the artifact is unexciting visually.
