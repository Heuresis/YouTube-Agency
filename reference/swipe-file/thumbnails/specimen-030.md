# Thumbnail Specimen 030 — Pointing-to-callout thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Operator pointing toward (or looking at) a specific element — usually a number, chart fragment, or visual evidence. Pointing arm visible.
- Callout element: A red circle or yellow arrow drawn around the specific element being pointed at.
- Text overlay: 3–5 words. Bold, often white with thick outline. Often above or beside the callout.
- Color: 2–3 colors, with the callout-color being highest contrast.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Pointing-to-callout thumbnails work because they direct visual attention precisely — the eye follows the pointing finger to the highlighted element. The red circle is the load-bearing element; without it, the viewer's eye scatters. The combination is one of the highest-CTR thumbnail patterns across nearly every niche because it compresses attention-direction and curiosity-density into one frame.
