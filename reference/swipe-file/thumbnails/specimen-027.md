# Thumbnail Specimen 027 — Then-and-now split thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Two visuals split vertically. Left: visual representation of past era (faded color, older imagery). Right: present-day visual (vibrant, current).
- Text overlay: Years marked above each side. "[YEAR A]" and "[YEAR B]".
- Color: Left side desaturated / sepia. Right side full color. Single accent color for text.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Then-and-now split thumbnails work because the temporal contrast is immediately legible — the audience knows they'll get a comparison without reading the title. The desaturation of the left side is load-bearing; without it, both halves look like the same era and the contrast is lost. Year markers make the comparison specific rather than abstract.

- mode: reach
