# Thumbnail Specimen 025 — Warning-tone thumbnail

## Structure (paraphrased)
- Visual: Operator's face with concerned / urgent expression. Visible warning element — stop sign, red exclamation triangle, hand-raised gesture.
- Text overlay: 3–5 words with warning framing ("STOP" "DON'T DO THIS" "BEFORE YOU"). Bold, often red or yellow.
- Color: Red and yellow accents on neutral background.

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Warning-tone thumbnails work because they activate loss-aversion — the audience clicks to avoid mistakes, which is a stronger driver than seeking gains. The visible warning iconography is load-bearing; without it, the warning framing in text alone reads as clickbait. The concerned-face expression authenticates the urgency — the operator looks like they actually care.
