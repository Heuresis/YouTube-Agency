# Title Specimen 015 — Failure-as-asset title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"The [Failure / Mistake] That Actually Led to [Success]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "The [Failed Launch] That Actually Led to [My First $100K Month]"
- "The [Embarrassing Cold Email] That Actually Got Me My First Client"
- "The [Job Rejection] That Built My Million-Dollar Company"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The failure-as-asset title reframes loss into causation. Audiences are drawn to the structural surprise of "failure → success" because it contradicts the "success → success" narrative dominant in the niche. The structural rule: the failure must be specific and verifiable, not a humble-brag.

## When to use
When the operator has a real failure-to-pivot story. Pairs naturally with vulnerability-tilted intros (hook specimens 014, 023).
