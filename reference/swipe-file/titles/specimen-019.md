# Title Specimen 019 — Why-question philosophical title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"Why [Universal Observation That Feels True But Is Rarely Explained]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "Why The People Who [Common Behavior] Always Seem To [Outcome]"
- "Why [Universal Pain] Is Actually [Counterintuitive Cause]"
- "Why You [Common Self-Defeating Behavior] (And How To Stop)"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Why-question titles work because the question itself contains social-proof — the viewer feels the question is valid the moment they read it. Philosophical-tone titles outperform tactical-tone titles in the unaware audience because the audience isn't yet looking for a solution; they're looking for a frame. Keeping the title to 5–9 words is structurally important; longer philosophical titles read as essayistic and lose CTR.
