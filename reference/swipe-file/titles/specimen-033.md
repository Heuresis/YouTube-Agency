# Title Specimen 033 — Client-case title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"How [Generic Client Descriptor] Went From [Start State] to [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "How A [Generic Client] Went From [Stuck State] to [Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
- "[Client Type] to [Outcome State]: The [N]-Step Process We Used"
- "From [Painful Starting Point] to [Result] in [Period]: The Case Study"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Client-case titles work because they offer parasocial transformation — the audience projects themselves into the case-study's starting state. The generic descriptor (instead of a name) maintains the projection while implying real specificity. "We used" is load-bearing; it signals the creator is the architect of the transformation, not just the narrator.
