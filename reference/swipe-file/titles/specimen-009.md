# Title Specimen 009 — Anti-complexity "Simple, Actually" title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"Starting [Activity] in [Year] Is Simple, Actually"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "Starting [Online Business] in [Year] Is Simple, Actually"
- "[Building a Personal Brand] in [Year] Is Simple, Actually"
- "[Hitting $10K/Month] in [Year] Is Simple, Actually"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The anti-complexity title contradicts the audience's expectation that "X is hard." The "Actually" qualifier softens the contradiction enough that it doesn't read as confrontational. Most effective when paired with a year reference that signals current relevance.

## When to use
Niches where the audience feels gatekept by perceived complexity. Less effective in niches that genuinely are complex.
