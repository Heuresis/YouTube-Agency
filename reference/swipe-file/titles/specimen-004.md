# Title Specimen 004 — Social-proof stack formula

## Structure (paraphrased)
"[Number] Students Proof This [Strategy] Works for [Audience]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "[147] Students Proof This [Sales Method] Works for [Introverts]"
- "Why [500+] Creators Use This [Thumbnail Strategy]"
- "[1,200] Students Proof This [Framework] Works for [Beginners]"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Aggregated social proof activates the comparative-self mechanism at scale. The audience auto-calculates "if 147 people like me made this work, statistically I can too." The specific number is critical — "many students" doesn't work.

## When to use
Authority-mode title slot when the operator has earned the social proof. Vulnerable to false-claim fatigue if used without real evidence.
