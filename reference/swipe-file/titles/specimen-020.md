# Title Specimen 020 — Tech-vs-tech opinionated verdict title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"[Tech A] vs [Tech B]: [Opinionated Verdict That Filters For The Right Audience]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "[Stack A] vs [Stack B]: I Switched After [N] Years"
- "[Tool A] or [Tool B]: The Honest Comparison Nobody Will Make"
- "[Approach A] vs [Approach B]: Why [Verdict] Is Almost Always Right"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Tech-vs-tech opinionated titles work because the technical audience is fatigued by hedge-everything content — they want a verdict. The "honest comparison nobody will make" framing signals editorial integrity and filters for senior viewers. The opinionated verdict is load-bearing; without it, the title reads as another neutral comparison the audience has already seen.
