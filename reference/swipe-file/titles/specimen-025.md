# Title Specimen 025 — A-vs-B-with-verdict title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"[Approach A] vs [Approach B]: I Tried Both, [Verdict]"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "[Old Method] vs [New Method]: I Tried Both for [Timeframe]"
- "[Tool A] or [Tool B] — I Used Both for [Period], Here's What Won"
- "[Strategy A] vs [Strategy B]: The Honest Head-to-Head"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
A-vs-B-with-verdict titles work because the audience is implicitly comparing options anyway — the video confirms they'll get the answer. The "I tried both" framing is the load-bearing credibility-anchor; without lived experience, the comparison reads as second-hand. The verdict promise filters for decision-fatigued viewers seeking conclusions, not analysis.
