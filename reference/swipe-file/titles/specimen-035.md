# Title Specimen 035 — Stop-and-do title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"Stop [Common Behavior]. Do This Instead."

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "Stop [Common Practice]. Do [Specific Alternative]."
- "Stop [Conventional Strategy] — Try [New Approach]"
- "Don't [Common Mistake]. Here's What Works."

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Stop-and-do titles work because of their imperative compression — they signal direct, no-frills authority. The two-clause structure ("stop X. Do Y.") is the load-bearing element; it promises both diagnosis and prescription in one breath. The brevity (5–7 words) is high-CTR because it stands out in a feed of longer titles.
