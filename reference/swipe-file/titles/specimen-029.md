# Title Specimen 029 — Failure-as-asset title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"I Lost [Significant Resource / Status] — Here's What I Learned"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "I Lost [Specific Amount / Outcome] in [Domain]. Here's What I Learned."
- "How I [Failed Specifically] and What Came After"
- "The [Specific Failure] That Changed How I [Approach Domain]"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
Failure-as-asset titles work because they invert the success-led credibility pattern — vulnerability is differentiation. The specific quantification ("lost X in Y way") prevents the title from feeling rehearsed. The "what I learned" close converts failure into proprietary knowledge, which is high-LTV positioning.
