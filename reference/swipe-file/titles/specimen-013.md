# Title Specimen 013 — Comprehensive-resource (FREE COURSE) title

## Structure (paraphrased)
"The Complete [Topic] Guide for [Specific Audience] in [Year] (FREE COURSE)"

## Worked structural examples (anonymized)
- "The Complete [Cold Email] Guide for [Agency Owners] in [Year] (FREE COURSE)"
- "The Complete [Channel Growth] Playbook for [Coaches] in [Year]"
- "Zero to $1M/Mo: The Complete Playbook To Scaling [Vehicle] (FREE COURSE)"

## Why this works (the imitation lesson)
The comprehensive-resource title positions the video itself as a discrete artifact. The "FREE COURSE" parenthetical is the load-bearing element — it signals that the video will be unusually long and value-dense. The structural rule: only use this title pattern for videos that are actually 30 minutes+ — using it for shorter videos produces "this isn't what was promised" backlash.

## When to use
Long-form (45-minute+) educational videos. Most effective when paired with a specific year reference that signals current relevance.
